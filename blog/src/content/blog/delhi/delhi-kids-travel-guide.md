## Taking Kids to Delhi: A Parent's Guide

Delhi with kids is absolutely doable and can be incredibly rewarding. Indian culture celebrates children — expect your kids to be smiled at, fussed over, and welcomed wherever you go. That said, some planning around health, logistics, and pacing will make the difference between a memorable family trip and an exhausting one. Here is everything you need to know.

## Health and Safety Essentials

### Before You Go
- **Vaccinations:** Consult your doctor 6-8 weeks before travel. Commonly recommended: Hepatitis A, Typhoid, and routine boosters. Malaria prophylaxis may be suggested for monsoon travel
- **Travel insurance:** Essential for families — ensure it covers medical evacuation and pediatric care
- **Pack a medical kit:** Children's paracetamol/ibuprofen, oral rehydration salts (ORS), anti-diarrhea medication, bandages, antiseptic cream, insect repellent, and any prescription medications

### Food and Water Safety
- **Water:** Only drink bottled or filtered water. Use bottled water for brushing teeth
- **Ice:** Avoid ice at street stalls; hotels and good restaurants use filtered water ice
- **Food:** Start with cooked food from restaurants before trying street food. Avoid raw salads and cut fruits from street vendors
- **Introduce Indian food gradually** — spice levels can be adjusted at most restaurants. Ask for "no mirchi" (no chilli) for kids' meals
- **Carry snacks** — familiar snacks from home provide comfort when kids are hungry and options are limited

### Common Health Concerns
- **Traveler's diarrhea:** The most common issue. Carry ORS packets and keep kids hydrated
- **Sunburn and heat exhaustion:** Use SPF 50+ sunscreen, wide-brimmed hats, and take breaks in shade
- **Air pollution:** In winter months, consider N95 masks for children on high-AQI days
- **Mosquitoes:** Use repellent (DEET-based for effectiveness), especially in the evening

### Hospitals with Pediatric Care
- **Max Super Speciality Hospital, Saket** — excellent pediatric department
- **Indraprastha Apollo Hospital** — 24/7 emergency with pediatric specialists
- **Sir Ganga Ram Hospital** — one of Delhi's best, centrally located
- **Tip:** Keep your hotel's address and your travel insurance details accessible at all times

## Age-Appropriate Activities

### Toddlers (1-4 years)
- Lodhi Gardens or Nehru Park for running around
- Delhi Zoo (National Zoological Park)
- Shankar's International Dolls Museum
- Swimming pool time at your hotel

### Young Children (5-9 years)
- National Rail Museum (trains!)
- Akshardham Temple (boat ride and fountain show)
- Adventure Island amusement park
- Red Fort with a storytelling guide
- Cycle rickshaw ride through Old Delhi

### Older Children (10-14 years)
- National Science Centre (interactive exhibits)
- Guided heritage walks
- Street food walking tour
- Day trip to Agra (Taj Mahal)
- Dilli Haat for handicraft shopping

### Teenagers
- Khan Market shopping and cafes
- Photography tour of Delhi's monuments
- Cooking class for Indian cuisine
- Escape rooms and gaming zones at malls
- Hauz Khas Village for galleries and cafes

## Practical Logistics

### Getting Around with Kids
- **Uber and Ola** are the most convenient option — air-conditioned cars with door-to-door service
- **Metro** works well for older children. Avoid rush hours. The first coach (women's coach) is a good choice for mothers with young children as it is less crowded
- **Avoid auto-rickshaws** with very young children — no seatbelts and exposure to traffic fumes
- **Car seats:** Not standard in India. Bring your own portable car seat or booster for young children
- **Baby carriers** are more practical than strollers in Delhi — uneven sidewalks, crowds, and steps make strollers difficult

### Dining with Kids
- **Indian restaurants are family-friendly** — children are welcomed, and staff will often help entertain them
- **Buffet restaurants** work well for picky eaters — they can choose what they like
- **Kid-friendly chains:** Big Chill (pasta and pizza), Nirula's (burgers and ice cream), Haldiram's (Indian snacks), McDonald's (vegetarian options available)
- **Most restaurants** will adjust spice levels on request
- **High chairs** are available at most mid-range and above restaurants

### Accommodation Tips
- **Hotel pools** are a lifesaver — kids need downtime between sightseeing
- **Suites or interconnecting rooms** give parents privacy after kids sleep
- **Room service** is a blessing for early mornings and tired evenings
- **Laundry service** — use it. Kids in Delhi will need outfit changes

## Sample Family Itinerary (3 Days)

### Day 1: Easy Start
Morning at Akshardham Temple (boat ride, fountain show). Lunch at Haldiram's. Afternoon at hotel pool. Evening walk at India Gate with ice cream.

### Day 2: History Made Fun
Morning cycle rickshaw tour of Old Delhi + Chandni Chowk food tasting. Lunch at Karim's (mild dishes). Afternoon at National Rail Museum. Evening at Connaught Place.

### Day 3: South Delhi Adventure
Morning at Qutub Minar (treasure hunt style). Lunch at Big Chill, Khan Market. Afternoon at Deer Park, Hauz Khas. Evening at Dilli Haat for souvenir shopping.

## Budget Tips for Families

- **Children under 3** typically enter attractions free
- **Children 3-12** get 50% discounts at most monuments
- **Family rooms** at mid-range hotels are often better value than booking two rooms
- **Street food** is a great budget option for adventurous families
- **Metro tourist passes** save money if you ride frequently
- **Free activities** abound: India Gate, Lodhi Gardens, Gurudwara Bangla Sahib, Deer Park

## What to Pack for Kids

- **Medications:** Full first-aid kit as described above
- **Sun protection:** Hat, sunscreen, sunglasses
- **Entertainment:** Tablets loaded with content for downtime and car rides
- **Comfort items:** Favorite toy or blanket for younger kids
- **Snacks:** Familiar snacks from home for picky moments
- **Hand sanitizer:** Large bottle — you will use it constantly
- **Wet wipes:** More than you think you need
- **Reusable water bottles:** Fill with bottled water each morning
