## Making the Most of One Day in Delhi

Delhi is a city that deserves weeks of exploration, but if you only have 24 hours, you can still experience its most iconic highlights. This carefully paced itinerary takes you from the Mughal splendor of Old Delhi to the colonial grandeur of New Delhi, with strategic stops that capture the essence of India's capital.

The key to a successful one-day Delhi visit is starting early, using efficient transport, and being selective about your stops. Here is your hour-by-hour guide.

## Morning: Old Delhi (7:00 AM – 12:00 PM)

### 7:00 AM – Jama Masjid

Start your day at **Jama Masjid**, India's largest mosque, built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan between 1644 and 1656. Arrive early to avoid crowds and experience the peaceful morning atmosphere. The mosque's courtyard can hold 25,000 worshippers, and the views from the southern minaret are spectacular.

- **Entry:** Free (camera fee ₹300, minaret climb ₹100)
- **Timings:** Sunrise to 12:00 PM, then 1:30 PM to before sunset
- **Tip:** Remove shoes before entering, and women should cover their heads (scarves available at the entrance)

### 8:00 AM – Chandni Chowk Breakfast

Walk from Jama Masjid into **Chandni Chowk**, one of Asia's oldest and busiest markets. Head to the legendary **Paranthe Wali Gali** for a traditional Delhi breakfast of stuffed parathas with curd and pickle (₹50-100 per paratha). Alternatively, try **Old Famous Jalebi Wala** near the Chandni Chowk metro station for piping hot jalebis (₹40-60 per plate).

Take 30 minutes to soak in the atmosphere — the narrow lanes, the spice shops, the cycle rickshaws, and the general controlled chaos that defines Old Delhi.

### 9:00 AM – Red Fort (Lal Qila)

Walk to the **Red Fort**, a 10-minute stroll from Chandni Chowk. This UNESCO World Heritage Site served as the main residence of Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years. The massive red sandstone walls stretch for 2 km, and inside you will find stunning marble palaces, gardens, and museums.

- **Entry:** ₹50 (Indians), ₹600 (foreigners)
- **Timings:** Sunrise to 9:00 PM (closed Mondays)
- **Time needed:** 1.5-2 hours
- **Tip:** Buy tickets online to skip the queue. The sound and light show in the evening is excellent but you will not have time on a one-day itinerary.

### 11:00 AM – Raj Ghat

Take a short auto-rickshaw ride (₹30-50) to **Raj Ghat**, the memorial to Mahatma Gandhi. This simple black marble platform marks the spot where Gandhi was cremated in 1948. The eternal flame burns here continuously, surrounded by lush gardens. A deeply moving experience that takes about 20-30 minutes.

- **Entry:** Free
- **Timings:** 5:00 AM to 7:30 PM daily

## Afternoon: New Delhi (12:00 PM – 5:00 PM)

### 12:00 PM – Lunch at Connaught Place

Take the metro from Chandni Chowk station to Rajiv Chowk station (Yellow Line, about 10 minutes) to reach **Connaught Place**, the commercial heart of New Delhi. For lunch, try:

- **Saravana Bhavan** for excellent South Indian vegetarian food (₹200-400 per person)
- **Wenger's** for colonial-era bakery treats and sandwiches (₹200-350)
- **Nizam's** for their famous Calcutta-style rolls (₹150-250)

### 1:30 PM – India Gate and Rajpath

After lunch, take a taxi or auto-rickshaw to **India Gate** (₹50-80 from Connaught Place). This 42-meter-high war memorial, designed by Edwin Lutyens, commemorates Indian soldiers who died in World War I. The surrounding lawns are a popular gathering spot. From India Gate, you can see **Rashtrapati Bhawan** (the President's House) at the other end of the ceremonial boulevard Kartavya Path (formerly Rajpath).

- **Entry:** Free
- **Time needed:** 30-45 minutes
- **Tip:** The area is beautifully illuminated at night, but afternoon visits offer great photo opportunities

### 2:30 PM – Humayun's Tomb

Take a cab (₹100-150) to **Humayun's Tomb**, one of Delhi's most beautiful monuments and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built in 1570, this stunning Mughal tomb is considered the inspiration for the Taj Mahal. The symmetrical gardens and the gorgeous red sandstone and white marble structure deserve at least an hour of exploration.

- **Entry:** ₹40 (Indians), ₹600 (foreigners)
- **Timings:** Sunrise to sunset daily
- **Time needed:** 1-1.5 hours
- **Tip:** The tomb looks magical during golden hour if you visit later in the afternoon

### 4:00 PM – Lodhi Gardens

A short walk or auto-rickshaw ride (₹20-30) from Humayun's Tomb brings you to **Lodhi Gardens**, a 90-acre urban oasis featuring tombs of 15th-century Sayyid and Lodi dynasty rulers. This is one of Delhi's most peaceful spots — perfect for a mid-afternoon stroll among ancient ruins, rose gardens, and shaded pathways.

- **Entry:** Free
- **Timings:** 6:00 AM to 7:30 PM (October-March), 6:00 AM to 8:00 PM (April-September)
- **Time needed:** 30-45 minutes

## Evening: South Delhi and Beyond (5:00 PM – 9:00 PM)

### 5:00 PM – Qutub Minar

Take a cab (₹200-300) or metro (Green Line to Qutub Minar station, then auto-rickshaw) to the **Qutub Minar**, the tallest brick minaret in the world at 72.5 meters. Built in 1193, this UNESCO World Heritage Site also features the mysterious Iron Pillar that has not rusted in over 1,600 years.

- **Entry:** ₹40 (Indians), ₹600 (foreigners)
- **Timings:** Sunrise to sunset daily
- **Time needed:** 1-1.5 hours
- **Tip:** The evening light makes for excellent photography

### 7:00 PM – Dinner at Hauz Khas Village

End your day at **Hauz Khas Village**, a trendy neighborhood built around a 13th-century water reservoir and madrasa ruins. The area combines historical charm with modern restaurants, bars, and boutique shops. For dinner:

- **Coast Cafe** for coastal Indian cuisine (₹800-1,200 per person)
- **Naivedyam** for South Indian thali (₹350-500 per person)
- **Yeti - The Himalayan Kitchen** for Nepali and Tibetan food (₹400-700 per person)

### 8:30 PM – Evening Options

If you still have energy, consider:
- A walk around the illuminated **Connaught Place** for some shopping
- Visiting the **Akshardham Temple** musical fountain show (timings vary by season, free entry)
- Enjoying chai at a rooftop cafe in **Hauz Khas**

## Tips for Your One-Day Visit

- **Start early** — Delhi attractions open at sunrise, and mornings are the coolest part of the day
- **Wear comfortable shoes** — you will walk 10,000-15,000 steps easily
- **Carry a portable charger** — you will be using your phone for maps and photos constantly
- **Book a cab for the afternoon** — metro connections to South Delhi attractions involve transfers that eat into limited time
- **Keep a scarf handy** — useful for religious site visits and sun protection
- **Stay hydrated** — carry a 1-liter water bottle and refill as needed

This one-day itinerary gives you a taste of everything that makes Delhi extraordinary — from Mughal history to colonial architecture, from street food to fine dining, and from ancient bazaars to modern neighborhoods.
