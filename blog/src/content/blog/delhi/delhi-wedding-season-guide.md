## Delhi's Wedding Season

Delhi is the wedding capital of India, and Indian weddings are among the most elaborate celebrations on earth. If you visit Delhi during wedding season, you will witness a spectacle of color, music, food, and joy that is unlike anything else. Whether you are invited to a wedding, planning one, or simply observing from the sidelines, understanding Delhi's wedding culture enhances your trip.

## When Is Wedding Season?

Indian weddings are governed by astrological calendars, with specific dates (muhurat) considered auspicious. The two main wedding seasons are:

- **November to February:** The primary season, peaking in December and January. Cool weather makes outdoor events comfortable
- **April to June:** A shorter season before the monsoon arrives

**Off-seasons:** July to September (monsoon) and some dates in March and October are considered inauspicious and see very few weddings.

## How Weddings Affect Your Trip

### Hotels
During peak wedding weekends (especially in December and February on auspicious dates), Delhi hotels — particularly those with banquet facilities — may be fully booked. Prices spike 20-40%.

**Tip:** Book well in advance for November-February stays. Check Hindu wedding date calendars online to avoid the busiest weekends if you prefer quieter hotels.

### Traffic
Wedding processions (baraats) regularly block Delhi streets, especially in South Delhi and Old Delhi. The groom's party marches with a band, dancing, and sometimes a decorated horse or vintage car.

**Tip:** Allow extra travel time in the evenings during wedding season. Use the metro when possible.

### Noise
Wedding celebrations include loud music, often until late at night. Hotels near wedding venues or banquet halls may be noisy. Request rooms away from banquet areas.

## If You Are Invited to an Indian Wedding

Being invited to a Delhi wedding is an extraordinary experience. Here is what to know:

### What to Wear
- **Women:** A sari, lehenga, or salwar kameez in bright, festive colors. Indian formal wear can be purchased at markets like Chandni Chowk or South Extension for ₹2,000-10,000. Avoid black and white — these are associated with mourning
- **Men:** A kurta-pajama or a sherwani for the main ceremony. A suit works for the reception. Indian formal wear available at shops in Karol Bagh and Connaught Place for ₹3,000-15,000
- **Jewelry:** Women typically wear statement jewelry. Gold-toned accessories work well
- **Footwear:** Embellished flats or heels for women, juttis (embroidered shoes) or formal shoes for men

### Gift Etiquette
- **Cash gifts** are traditional and preferred — given in an envelope at the reception. Amounts typically range from ₹1,001 to ₹11,001 (odd numbers ending in 1 are considered auspicious)
- **Gift registry** items if the couple has one (increasingly common among modern Delhi families)
- **Avoid:** Leather items for Hindu weddings, alcohol unless you know the family drinks

### The Events
Indian weddings span multiple days with different events:
1. **Mehendi:** Henna ceremony for the bride and female guests. Casual and fun
2. **Sangeet:** Music and dance party. Often the most enjoyable event for international guests
3. **Wedding ceremony:** The main event, which may last 2-4 hours with rituals around a sacred fire
4. **Reception:** A grand dinner and party, usually the evening event most accessible to international guests

### Practical Tips
- **Eat** — Indian wedding food is extraordinary and usually includes live cooking stations with 15-30 dishes
- **You do not need to stay for the entire ceremony** — it is acceptable to attend for a few hours
- **Photography is generally welcome** — but ask before photographing the bride closely
- **Prepare for a late night** — weddings often start late and the reception may go past midnight
- **You may not know many people** — Indians are hospitable and will include you in conversations and photos

## Experiencing Weddings as a Tourist

### Street Baraats
You will inevitably encounter a baraat (groom's procession) on Delhi streets during wedding season. Feel free to watch and enjoy — the music, dancing, and decorations are joyful. People in the procession may invite you to join the dancing.

### Wedding Shopping
Even if not attending a wedding, Delhi's wedding shopping districts are worth exploring:
- **Chandni Chowk:** Lehengas, saris, and wedding jewelry at various price points
- **Shahpur Jat:** Boutique designers and contemporary wedding wear
- **South Extension:** Mid-range to luxury Indian formal wear
- **Kinari Bazaar:** Wedding decorations and accessories

### Destination Wedding Venues
Delhi has some of India's most spectacular wedding venues:
- **The Leela Palace** — grand ballrooms and gardens
- **The Taj Palace** — six acres of grounds for outdoor ceremonies
- **Neemrana Fort** — heritage fort weddings (day trip from Delhi)
- **Various farmhouses** in Chattarpur and Mehrauli — massive private venues

## Delhi Wedding Economy

Delhi's wedding industry is estimated at billions of dollars annually. A typical upper-middle-class Delhi wedding costs ₹20-50 lakh (₹2-5 million / $25,000-60,000), while luxury weddings can exceed ₹5-10 crore (₹50-100 million / $600,000-1.2 million). The industry supports thousands of vendors — florists, caterers, decorators, photographers, musicians, and event planners.
