## Navigating Delhi's Transport System

Delhi is a massive city, and getting around efficiently is crucial to enjoying your trip. The good news is that Delhi has one of the world's best metro systems, affordable ride-hailing apps, and various other options for every budget. The bad news is that road traffic can be nightmarish during rush hours. Here is how to move around like a local.

## Delhi Metro (Best Overall Option)

The Delhi Metro is clean, fast, air-conditioned, safe, and covers most tourist areas. It is the single best way to get around the city.

### Key Facts
- **Network:** 390+ km with 290+ stations across 10 lines
- **Hours:** 5:30 AM to 11:30 PM daily
- **Fares:** ₹11 to ₹64 based on distance
- **Payment:** Tokens (single journey), Smart Card (reusable with 10% discount), or QR-code tickets via apps
- **Tourist passes:** 1-day (₹200), 3-day (₹500) for unlimited rides

### Important Lines for Tourists
- **Yellow Line:** Connects Connaught Place (Rajiv Chowk) to Chandni Chowk, HUDA City Centre
- **Blue Line:** Covers Karol Bagh, Rajiv Chowk, and extends to Dwarka and Noida
- **Violet Line:** Connects to Kashmere Gate, INA (Dilli Haat), and Faridabad
- **Orange Line (Airport Express):** Links airport to New Delhi Railway Station in 20 minutes
- **Magenta Line:** Connects Janakpuri to Botanical Garden via Hauz Khas

### Metro Tips
- **Avoid rush hours** (8:30-10:00 AM and 5:30-7:30 PM) when trains are packed
- **Women's coaches:** The first coach of every train is reserved for women
- **No eating or drinking** inside metro stations or trains (fine: ₹200)
- **Luggage limit:** 15 kg per bag, maximum 2 bags
- **Download the DMRC app** for route planning and fare calculation

## Auto-Rickshaws

The iconic three-wheeled auto-rickshaws are ubiquitous and ideal for short-to-medium distances.

### How They Work
- **Metered:** Autos have meters, but many drivers refuse to use them for tourists
- **Negotiated fare:** If the meter is not used, agree on a price before getting in
- **Typical fares:** ₹30-50 for short distances (1-3 km), ₹80-150 for medium distances (3-8 km)
- **Shared autos:** On some routes, shared auto-rickshaws run fixed routes for ₹10-20

### Tips
- **Use the meter** — if a driver refuses, find another auto
- **Carry small change** — drivers often claim they do not have change for large notes
- **Know your destination** — have the address in Hindi or on Google Maps to show the driver
- **Avoid accepting "guided tours"** offered by auto drivers — they earn commissions from shops they take you to
- **App-based autos** on Uber and Ola show transparent pricing and are the safest option

## Ride-Hailing Apps (Uber and Ola)

The most convenient option for tourists, offering transparent pricing, GPS tracking, and no negotiation.

### Uber
- **Available in Delhi:** UberGo (hatchback), UberPremier (sedan), Uber Auto (auto-rickshaw), Uber Moto (motorcycle)
- **Payment:** Cash, card, or Uber wallet
- **Typical fares:** ₹80-150 for 5 km, ₹200-400 for 10-15 km

### Ola
- **India's homegrown app** with similar services to Uber
- **Available options:** Micro (cheapest car), Mini, Prime (sedan), Auto
- **Often slightly cheaper** than Uber for similar routes

### Tips
- **Surge pricing** applies during rush hours, rain, and late nights
- **Share your trip** with a friend or family member for safety
- **Rate is shown before booking** — no surprises
- **Cash option** is available if you do not want to link a card
- **Wait at the designated pickup point** — drivers may cancel if you take too long

## Cycle Rickshaws

Traditional pedal-powered rickshaws are common in Old Delhi, Chandni Chowk, and some residential areas.

- **Best for:** Short distances in Old Delhi's narrow lanes where cars and autos cannot go
- **Fare:** ₹20-50 for short rides, ₹50-100 for longer distances
- **Negotiate before boarding** — there are no meters
- **Experience:** A charming, slow-paced way to see Old Delhi

## Taxis

Traditional taxis (black and yellow, or white) are less common than in the past but still available.

- **Pre-paid taxis** at the airport and railway stations offer fixed fares
- **Radio taxis** (Meru, Mega Cabs) can be booked by phone — metered and air-conditioned
- **Fares:** Higher than auto-rickshaws, slightly more than Uber/Ola
- **Best for:** Airport transfers and pre-booked intercity travel

## Buses

DTC (Delhi Transport Corporation) and cluster buses cover the entire city at very low fares.

- **Fares:** ₹5-15 for most routes
- **AC buses:** ₹10-25
- **Not recommended for tourists** due to crowding, unclear routes, and lack of English signage
- **Exception:** The HOHO (Hop On Hop Off) tourist bus covers major attractions in a convenient loop

## Private Car with Driver

Hiring a car with driver is an excellent option for day-long sightseeing.

- **Half day (4-5 hours):** ₹1,500-2,500
- **Full day (8-10 hours):** ₹2,500-4,000
- **AC sedan:** ₹3,000-5,000 per day
- **Book through:** Hotel concierge, local tour operators, or apps like Savaari
- **Includes:** Fuel, driver, parking. Tips are separate (₹200-500 per day)

## E-Rickshaws

Battery-powered three-wheelers that have become extremely popular for short distances.

- **Fare:** ₹10-30 per ride
- **Best for:** Last-mile connectivity from metro stations to your destination
- **Available at:** Most metro stations and market areas

## Transport Comparison

| Mode | Cost (5 km) | Speed | Comfort | Tourist-Friendly |
|------|-------------|-------|---------|-----------------|
| Metro | ₹22-33 | Fast | High | Very High |
| Auto-rickshaw | ₹60-100 | Moderate | Low | Moderate |
| Uber/Ola car | ₹100-180 | Varies | High | Very High |

| Bus | ₹10-15 | Slow | Low | Low |
| Private car | ₹500+ (hourly) | Varies | Very High | Very High |

## Traffic Tips

- **Rush hours:** 8:30-10:30 AM and 5:00-8:00 PM — avoid road transport during these windows
- **Google Maps** is reliable for real-time traffic and route planning in Delhi
- **Friday evenings** are the worst for traffic
- **Road closures** are common for VIP movements, festivals, and protests — check local news
- **Odd-even scheme** may be implemented during severe pollution — check if it affects your travel plans
