## Delhi: India's Culinary Capital

Delhi is arguably the greatest food city in Asia. Its culinary heritage spans Mughal-era recipes perfected over centuries, Punjabi comfort food brought by refugees during Partition, street food traditions dating back to the Sultanate period, and a booming modern dining scene that rivals any global capital. From a ₹50 plate of chole bhature to a ₹6,000 tasting menu, Delhi offers extraordinary eating at every price point.

## Fine Dining

### Indian Accent, The Lodhi
Chef Manish Mehrotra's flagship restaurant consistently ranks among Asia's best. The cuisine is progressive Indian — classic flavors reimagined with modern techniques. The daulat ki chaat, meetha achaar pork ribs, and blue cheese naan are signature dishes. Tasting menus from ₹4,500. Reservations essential.

### Bukhara, ITC Maurya
Open since 1977, Bukhara is India's most famous restaurant. The menu has barely changed — and that is the point. Dal Bukhara (slow-cooked black lentils), Sikandari Raan (marinated leg of lamb), and jumbo tandoori prawns are legendary. No cutlery provided — you eat with your hands, wearing the signature Bukhara bib. ₹4,000-6,000 per person.

### Dum Pukht Jolly Nabobs, ITC Maurya
Upstairs from Bukhara, this restaurant celebrates the Awadhi slow-cooking technique of dum pukht. The biryani sealed in dough and the kakori kebabs that melt on contact are unforgettable. ₹3,500-5,000 per person.

### Varq, Taj Mahal Hotel
Elegant modern Indian dining where Chef Hemant Oberoi's legacy continues. The interiors are refined, the service impeccable, and the gilawat kebab is among Delhi's best. ₹3,000-4,500 per person.

### Wasabi by Morimoto, Taj Mahal Hotel
Delhi's finest Japanese restaurant uses ingredients flown in from Tokyo's Tsukiji market. The omakase experience is exceptional. ₹5,000-8,000 per person.

## Iconic Delhi Restaurants

### Karim's, Jama Masjid
Established in 1913 by descendants of Mughal court cooks, Karim's serves arguably the most authentic Mughlai cuisine in India. The mutton burra kebab, nihari, and chicken Jahangiri are musts. The original location near Jama Masjid is cramped and chaotic, but that is part of the experience. ₹300-600 per person.

### Moti Mahal, Daryaganj
The restaurant that invented butter chicken and dal makhani in the 1940s. Yes, this is where those now-global dishes originated. The original Daryaganj location still serves both dishes in their purest form. ₹400-700 per person.

### Gulati, Pandara Road
A Delhi institution since 1959, Gulati serves North Indian classics in a comfortable setting. Their butter chicken is consistently voted among Delhi's best, and the dal makhani is excellent. Always packed — arrive early or expect a wait. ₹600-1,000 per person.

### Sita Ram Diwan Chand, Paharganj
Delhi's undisputed king of chole bhature — crispy fried bread with spiced chickpeas. The place has been serving the same dish since 1955, and the queue is constant. Cash only. ₹60-100 per plate.

## Modern and International

### Diva, Greater Kailash
Chef Ritu Dalmia's Italian restaurant is one of Delhi's most awarded. The handmade pasta, wood-fired pizzas, and Italian desserts are prepared with impeccable technique. ₹1,200-2,000 per person.

### Olive Bar & Kitchen, Mehrauli
Mediterranean cuisine in a beautiful white-walled courtyard setting near the Qutub Minar. The brunch is legendary. ₹1,500-2,500 per person.

### MIZO, Mehrauli
Pan-Asian cuisine with a focus on Japanese and Korean flavors. The setting in the Qutub Minar heritage district adds to the experience. ₹1,500-2,500 per person.

### Big Chill, Khan Market
An all-day restaurant loved by Delhi residents for its Italian-American menu, generous portions, and lively atmosphere. The cheesecake is iconic. ₹500-900 per person. Multiple locations.

### SodaBottleOpenerWala, Khan Market
A playful Irani-Parsi cafe serving berry pulao, dhansak, and Parsi-style omelettes in a quirky vintage setting. ₹500-800 per person.

## Casual and Budget Dining

### Saravana Bhavan, Connaught Place
The Chennai-based chain serves outstanding South Indian vegetarian food. The thali, dosa, and filter coffee are excellent and affordable. ₹200-400 per person.

### Nizam's, Connaught Place
Famous for their Calcutta-style kathi rolls since 1932. The chicken and mutton rolls wrapped in flaky paratha are Delhi street food elevated to restaurant form. ₹150-300 per person.

### Al Jawahar, Jama Masjid
Often overlooked in favor of nearby Karim's, Al Jawahar serves equally excellent Mughlai cuisine with less of a wait. The mutton korma and roomali roti are outstanding. ₹300-500 per person.

### Rajinder Da Dhaba, Safdarjung Enclave
A no-frills roadside dhaba (casual eatery) that serves some of Delhi's best tandoori chicken, fish tikka, and keema. Popular with locals for late-night dining. ₹300-500 per person.

## Restaurant Tips for Delhi

- **Reservations are essential** for fine dining restaurants, especially on weekends
- **Lunch is often better value** — many upscale restaurants offer lunch menus at 40-60% of dinner prices
- **Hygiene varies widely** — street food stalls with high turnover are generally safer than those with sitting food
- **Most restaurants accept cards** — but carry cash for Old Delhi establishments and street food
- **Tipping:** 10-15% is standard at restaurants; check if service charge is already added
- **Peak dining hours** are 8:00-10:00 PM — arrive early or late to avoid waits at popular places
- **Zomato and Swiggy apps** are useful for reviews, menus, and delivery if you want a quiet night in
