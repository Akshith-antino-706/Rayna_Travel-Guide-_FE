## Why Visit Delhi?

Delhi is not just a city — it is a living chronicle of empires, revolutions, and reinventions spanning over a thousand years. As the capital of India, Delhi is where Mughal grandeur meets colonial-era architecture, where ancient bazaars sit alongside gleaming shopping malls, and where the aroma of sizzling street food competes with the fragrance of manicured gardens. With a population of over 20 million, Delhi is one of the world's great megacities, offering an intensity of experience that few destinations can match.

Whether you are drawn by the imposing Red Fort, the solemn beauty of Humayun's Tomb, the spiritual serenity of the Lotus Temple, or the chaotic charm of Chandni Chowk, Delhi delivers something unforgettable for every type of traveler.

## Best Time to Visit Delhi

The ideal window for visiting Delhi is **October through March**, when temperatures range from 10-25°C (50-77°F) and the skies are generally clear. This is peak tourist season, so expect higher hotel prices and more crowds at major attractions, but the comfortable weather makes outdoor sightseeing genuinely pleasant.

**Summer (April to June)** brings extreme heat — temperatures regularly exceed 40°C (104°F) and can touch 48°C (118°F) in May and June. Most outdoor activities become uncomfortable, though hotels offer significant discounts.

**Monsoon (July to September)** sees heavy rainfall, especially in August. While the city turns green and beautiful, flooding and humidity can disrupt travel plans. The upside is fewer crowds and lower prices.

**Important note:** Late November through January can bring heavy smog and fog, which reduces visibility and can cause flight delays. February and March offer excellent weather with cleaner air.

## Getting to Delhi

**Indira Gandhi International Airport (DEL)** is India's busiest airport and a major international hub. Terminal 3 handles most international flights and is modern and well-equipped. The airport is located about 16 km southwest of central Delhi.

From the airport, you have several options:
- **Airport Express Metro Line:** The fastest option to reach central Delhi, connecting to New Delhi Railway Station in about 20 minutes for ₹60
- **Pre-paid taxis:** Available at booths outside arrivals, costing ₹350-600 to most central Delhi locations
- **Ride-hailing apps:** Uber and Ola operate at the airport, typically ₹300-500 to central Delhi
- **Hotel shuttles:** Many mid-range and luxury hotels offer airport transfers

## Getting Around Delhi

Delhi is a sprawling city, but its excellent metro system makes navigation straightforward.

**Delhi Metro** is the backbone of the transport system — a clean, affordable, and air-conditioned network with over 290 stations across 10 lines covering 390+ km. Fares range from ₹11 to ₹64 depending on distance, and tourist passes are available for ₹200 (1-day) or ₹500 (3-day).

**Auto-rickshaws** are ubiquitous and ideal for short distances. Always insist on the meter or agree on a fare before starting. Typical rides cost ₹30-150.

**Ride-hailing apps** (Uber, Ola) are widely used and offer transparent pricing. They are the most convenient option for tourists.

**Cycle-rickshaws** offer a charming way to explore Old Delhi's narrow lanes, typically costing ₹20-50 per ride.

## Top Areas to Know

Understanding Delhi's geography helps plan your visit:

**Old Delhi (Shahjahanabad)** is the historic heart of the city, home to Red Fort, Jama Masjid, and the legendary street food of Chandni Chowk. Expect narrow lanes, intense crowds, and sensory overload in the best possible way.

**New Delhi** is the planned capital designed by Edwin Lutyens, featuring wide boulevards, India Gate, Rashtrapati Bhawan (President's House), and Connaught Place — the commercial hub with shopping, dining, and nightlife.

**South Delhi** is the upscale residential area with cultural attractions like Humayun's Tomb, Qutub Minar, Hauz Khas Village, and Lodhi Gardens. It offers the best dining and boutique shopping.

**Aerocity** is the modern hotel district near the airport, ideal for transit stays with excellent connectivity via the Airport Express Metro.

## Must-See Attractions

### Historical Monuments
- **Red Fort (Lal Qila):** Mughal-era masterpiece and UNESCO World Heritage Site. Entry: ₹50 (Indians), ₹600 (foreigners). Closed Mondays.
- **Qutub Minar:** The tallest brick minaret in the world at 72.5 meters. Entry: ₹40 (Indians), ₹600 (foreigners).
- **Humayun's Tomb:** Stunning Mughal architecture that inspired the Taj Mahal. Entry: ₹40 (Indians), ₹600 (foreigners).
- **Jama Masjid:** India's largest mosque, built by Shah Jahan. Free entry (camera fee ₹300).

### Modern Attractions
- **Lotus Temple:** Bahá'í House of Worship shaped like a lotus flower. Free entry. Closed Mondays.
- **Akshardham Temple:** Magnificent Hindu temple complex with exhibits and a musical fountain. Free entry.
- **India Gate:** The 42-meter war memorial, beautifully illuminated at night.

### Cultural Experiences
- **Chandni Chowk:** One of Asia's oldest and busiest markets, dating back to the 17th century
- **Lodhi Gardens:** Peaceful 90-acre park with 15th-century tombs
- **National Museum:** Houses over 200,000 artifacts spanning 5,000 years of Indian civilization

## Cultural Tips for Visitors

Delhi is cosmopolitan and welcoming, but respecting local customs enhances your experience. Remove shoes before entering temples, mosques, and gurudwaras (Sikh temples). Cover your head when visiting Sikh temples — scarves are usually available at the entrance. Dress modestly at religious sites, covering shoulders and knees.

Bargaining is expected at street markets but not at malls or fixed-price shops. Tipping is appreciated — ₹50-100 for restaurant staff, ₹20-50 for auto-rickshaw drivers who provide good service, and ₹100-200 per day for guides.

Use your right hand for giving and receiving items, as the left hand is considered unclean in Indian culture. Public displays of affection should be kept minimal.

## How Much Does Delhi Cost?

Delhi is remarkably affordable for international travelers.

| Budget Level | Daily Cost (INR) | Daily Cost (USD) |
|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Budget | ₹2,000-3,500 | $25-40 |
| Mid-Range | ₹5,000-8,000 | $60-100 |
| Luxury | ₹15,000-20,000+ | $180-250+ |

The biggest savings come from Delhi's incredible street food (meals from ₹50-100), affordable public transport, and reasonably priced accommodations. Even luxury experiences like fine dining cost a fraction of what they would in Western cities.

## Essential Tips for First-Timers

- **Book attraction tickets online** to skip long queues, especially at Red Fort and Qutub Minar
- **Carry a scarf or shawl** for covering your head at religious sites
- **Download Uber and Ola apps** before arriving for hassle-free transport
- **Stay hydrated** — always carry bottled water, especially in warmer months
- **Get a local SIM card** at the airport for affordable data (Airtel or Jio recommended)
- **Avoid tap water** — stick to bottled or filtered water
- **Beware of touts** near major tourist sites offering unsolicited guide services
