## How Much Does Delhi Cost?

Delhi is one of the most affordable major cities in the world for travelers. Your money goes remarkably far — from street food that costs less than a dollar to luxury hotels that would cost three times as much in London or New York. Here is a detailed breakdown of what to expect at every budget level.

## Budget Traveler: ₹2,000-3,500 per day ($25-40 USD)

This is a genuine backpacker budget that still allows for a comfortable, enjoyable trip.

### Accommodation: ₹500-1,500
- Hostel dorm bed: ₹500-900 per night
- Budget hotel room: ₹1,200-2,000 per night
- **Tip:** Paharganj and Karol Bagh offer the best value

### Food: ₹300-600
- Street food breakfast (paratha or chole bhature): ₹50-100
- Lunch at a local dhaba (casual eatery): ₹80-150
- Street food snacks: ₹50-100
- Dinner at a basic restaurant: ₹100-200
- Chai and snacks: ₹20-50

### Transport: ₹150-300
- Metro rides (3-4 per day): ₹50-150
- Auto-rickshaw rides (1-2 per day): ₹60-150
- **Tip:** Get a metro tourist card for unlimited rides (₹200/day)

### Activities: ₹200-500
- Monument entries (Indian pricing): ₹30-50 per site
- Free attractions (India Gate, Lodhi Gardens, temples): ₹0
- Walking tours and free city tours: ₹0-500

### Daily Budget Total: ₹1,150-2,900

## Mid-Range Traveler: ₹5,000-8,000 per day ($60-100 USD)

A comfortable experience with good hotels, restaurant dining, and efficient transport.

### Accommodation: ₹3,000-6,000
- 3-star hotel room: ₹3,000-4,500 per night
- Boutique hotel: ₹4,500-6,000 per night
- **Tip:** Book on MakeMyTrip or Goibibo for deals

### Food: ₹1,000-2,000
- Hotel breakfast (often included): ₹0
- Lunch at a good restaurant: ₹400-600
- Afternoon snack/cafe: ₹150-300
- Dinner at a popular restaurant: ₹600-1,000
- Drinks: ₹200-400

### Transport: ₹400-800
- Metro + auto-rickshaw mix: ₹200-400
- Uber/Ola rides (2-3 per day): ₹300-500
- **Tip:** Uber and Ola are significantly cheaper than in Western countries

### Activities: ₹600-1,500
- Monument entries (foreigner pricing): ₹300-600 per site
- Guided tours: ₹500-1,500
- Museum entries: ₹200-650

### Daily Mid-Range Total: ₹5,000-10,300

## Luxury Traveler: ₹15,000-25,000+ per day ($180-300+ USD)

World-class luxury at a fraction of Western prices.

### Accommodation: ₹10,000-30,000
- 5-star hotel: ₹10,000-20,000 per night
- Heritage luxury hotel: ₹18,000-45,000 per night
- **Tip:** Even Delhi's finest hotels are 40-60% cheaper than comparable properties in London

### Food: ₹3,000-8,000
- Hotel breakfast (usually included): ₹0
- Fine dining lunch: ₹1,500-3,000
- Afternoon tea at The Imperial: ₹1,000-1,500
- Dinner at Bukhara or Indian Accent: ₹4,000-6,000
- Cocktails: ₹500-1,500

### Transport: ₹2,000-4,000
- Hotel car with driver (full day): ₹3,000-5,000
- Airport transfers (luxury car): ₹1,500-3,000
- **Tip:** Hotel transport is convenient but pricier — Uber Black is a good alternative

### Activities: ₹2,000-5,000
- Private guided tours: ₹3,000-8,000 per day
- Monument entries: ₹600-1,000 per site
- Spa treatments: ₹3,000-8,000

### Daily Luxury Total: ₹17,000-47,000

## Money-Saving Tips

- **Eat street food** — Delhi's best food is often the cheapest
- **Use the metro** — significantly cheaper and often faster than taxis
- **Visit free attractions** — India Gate, Lodhi Gardens, Gurudwara Bangla Sahib, and many temples are free
- **Book monument tickets online** — saves time, sometimes offers discounts
- **Carry a student ID** — some attractions offer student discounts
- **Shop at government emporiums** — fixed prices and authentic goods (Central Cottage Industries, Dilli Haat)
- **Avoid hotel restaurants for every meal** — even a short walk outside saves 50-70%
- **Travel in summer** for hotel discounts of 30-50%
- **Use Indian booking platforms** — MakeMyTrip, Goibibo, EaseMyTrip often beat international platforms on price

## Hidden Costs to Watch For

- **Foreigner pricing** at monuments — typically 10-15x the Indian price
- **Camera fees** at some religious sites (₹100-300)
- **Autorickshaw overcharging** — always negotiate or insist on the meter
- **Tourist shop markups** — bargain hard at Chandni Chowk and Janpath
- **Surge pricing** on Uber/Ola during rush hours and late nights
- **Hotel minibar** — prices are 3-5x market rate, buy from nearby shops instead
