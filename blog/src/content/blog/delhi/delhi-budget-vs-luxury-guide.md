## Two Delhis: Budget and Luxury Compared

One of Delhi's great strengths is that it rewards both budget and luxury travelers equally — just in different ways. The same city that offers a world-class meal at Bukhara for ₹6,000 also serves arguably better chole bhature from a street stall for ₹60. Here is an honest side-by-side comparison of both approaches.

## Accommodation

### Budget (₹1,000-3,000/night)
**Experience:** Clean but basic rooms in Paharganj, Karol Bagh, or Arakashan Road. Hostel dorms for the most social experience. Expect small rooms, variable Wi-Fi, and thin walls — but functional and well-located.
**Highlight:** The social atmosphere of backpacker hostels like Zostel and Moustache, where you meet travelers from around the world.

### Luxury (₹15,000-45,000/night)
**Experience:** Marble lobbies, butler service, art collections on the walls, and rooms the size of apartments. Properties like The Imperial, The Oberoi, and Aman New Delhi are destinations in themselves.
**Highlight:** Waking up to views of Lodhi Gardens from your private plunge pool at The Lodhi.

**Verdict:** Luxury hotels in Delhi offer extraordinary value compared to equivalent properties worldwide. But budget accommodation in Delhi is also far above what the price suggests.

## Dining

### Budget (₹200-500/day)
**Experience:** Street food breakfasts at Paranthe Wali Gali, lunch thalis at local dhabas, and evening chaat at Bengali Market. The food quality at the ₹50-100 price point is genuinely exceptional.
**Highlight:** The ₹60 plate of chole bhature at Sita Ram Diwan Chand.

### Luxury (₹4,000-12,000/day)
**Experience:** Tasting menus at Indian Accent, Mughlai feasts at Bukhara, Japanese omakase at Wasabi, and afternoon tea at The Imperial.
**Highlight:** The dal makhani at Dum Pukht, slow-cooked for 18 hours.

**Verdict:** Delhi is the rare city where budget food is genuinely extraordinary. Luxury dining adds ambiance and service but not necessarily better flavors.

## Transport

### Budget (₹150-400/day)
**Experience:** Delhi Metro supplemented by auto-rickshaws. Clean, efficient, and covers most tourist areas.

### Luxury (₹3,000-8,000/day)
**Experience:** Private car with driver, hotel transfers in luxury sedans. Door-to-door comfort.

**Verdict:** The metro is so good that luxury transport is about convenience rather than necessity.

## The Smart Approach

The savviest Delhi travelers mix both worlds:
- **Splurge on** one fine-dining dinner, a good guide for Old Delhi, and a comfortable hotel
- **Save on** street food meals, metro transport, and self-guided walks at parks and free attractions
- **The sweet spot:** ₹6,000-10,000 per day gets you a comfortable hotel, excellent food, convenient transport, and guided experiences
