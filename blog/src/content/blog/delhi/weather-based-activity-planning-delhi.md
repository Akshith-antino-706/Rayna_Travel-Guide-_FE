## Planning Activities Around Delhi's Weather

Delhi's extreme seasonal variation means the ideal itinerary in October looks very different from one in May or August. Smart planning around weather conditions ensures you see the best of Delhi regardless of when you visit. Here is how to optimize your activities for every weather scenario.

## Cool Winter Days (October-February, 15-25°C)

This is the golden window for outdoor exploration. Take advantage of comfortable temperatures to cover the most ground.

### Best Activities
- **Full-day monument tours** — Red Fort, Qutub Minar, Humayun's Tomb in a single day
- **Walking tours** through Old Delhi (3-4 hours without heat exhaustion)
- **Cycling tours** at dawn through Lutyens' Delhi
- **Lodhi Gardens and Sunder Nursery** walks
- **Outdoor dining** at Olive Bar & Kitchen or Lodi - The Garden Restaurant
- **Heritage walks** through Civil Lines, Mehrauli, or Nizamuddin

### Schedule
- Start outdoor activities at 9:00-10:00 AM (after fog clears in Dec-Jan)
- Outdoor sightseeing comfortable until 5:00-5:30 PM
- Evening outdoor dining is pleasant

## Foggy Winter Mornings (December-January)

Dense fog can disrupt morning plans but creates a unique atmosphere.

### Best Activities
- **Late-start sightseeing** — begin outdoor activities after 10:00 AM when fog lifts
- **Indoor morning activities** — National Museum, National Gallery of Modern Art, or Crafts Museum
- **Hotel breakfast experiences** — use foggy mornings for leisurely hotel dining
- **Shopping** — Khan Market, Connaught Place, and malls open early
- **Akshardham Temple** — indoor exhibits are perfect for foggy mornings

### Fog-Photography Opportunities
Fog creates atmospheric conditions that experienced photographers love — the Red Fort, India Gate, and Humayun's Tomb look hauntingly beautiful through morning mist.

## Hot Summer Days (April-June, 35-48°C)

Extreme heat requires a completely different strategy. Focus on indoor and early/late-day outdoor activities.

### Best Activities
- **Sunrise sightseeing (5:30-8:30 AM)** — monuments are open from sunrise and mornings are the only comfortable outdoor window
- **Air-conditioned museums** — National Museum, Crafts Museum, National Science Centre
- **Mall visits** — Select Citywalk, DLF Emporio, and Ambience Mall for shopping and dining
- **Hotel pool time** — the most comfortable afternoon activity
- **Cooking classes** — indoor and available year-round
- **Spa treatments** — excellent at Delhi's luxury hotels
- **Evening sightseeing (after 6:00 PM)** — India Gate at night, Connaught Place nightlife

### Schedule
- Outdoor activities: 5:30-8:30 AM only
- Indoor activities: 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM
- Evening outdoor: After 6:30 PM

### Survival Tips
- Carry 2+ liters of water per person per day
- Wear light, loose cotton clothing and a hat
- Take an afternoon siesta like locals do
- Eat light — heavy meals in extreme heat cause lethargy

## Monsoon Days (July-September)

Rain transforms Delhi but requires flexible planning.

### Best Activities for Rainy Days
- **Museum hopping** — dedicate a full day to Delhi's excellent museums
- **Shopping at malls** — air-conditioned, weatherproof, and entertaining
- **Cooking classes** — a perfect rainy-day activity
- **Spa day** — luxury hotel spas offer monsoon packages
- **Food crawl** — hot chai and pakoras at roadside stalls are a quintessential monsoon experience

### Between Showers
Delhi monsoon rain comes in bursts, not all-day drizzle. Use dry spells for:
- **Quick monument visits** — post-rain light is beautiful for photography
- **Lodhi Gardens** — lush and green during monsoon
- **Humayun's Tomb** — the gardens look spectacular in the rain

### Monsoon Precautions
- Check weather forecasts hourly — sudden downpours are common
- Carry a compact umbrella and waterproof phone case always
- Wear waterproof shoes — streets flood quickly
- Avoid Old Delhi during heavy rain — narrow lanes flood
- Be cautious of mosquitoes — use repellent, especially in evenings

## High Pollution Days (AQI 300+)

Occasionally, especially in November and December, pollution levels make outdoor activities inadvisable.

### Indoor Alternatives
- **Museum marathon** — National Museum + National Gallery of Modern Art
- **Shopping** — DLF Emporio, Select Citywalk, Khan Market (enclosed sections)
- **Akshardham Temple** — entirely enclosed
- **Hotel spa and pool** — indoor pools are available at most 4-star+ hotels
- **Food experiences** — cooking classes, restaurant meals, hotel food events
- **Cultural performances** — check India Habitat Centre and Kamani Auditorium schedules

### Precautions
- Wear N95 masks outdoors
- Choose hotels with air purifiers
- Limit outdoor exposure to essential transport only
- Keep windows closed in your hotel room
- Stay hydrated — dry, polluted air dehydrates

## Activity Planner Quick Reference

|---------|---------------|-----------------|---------|

| Hot (35-40°C) | Morning only | Sunrise sights, indoor rest | Carry 2L water minimum |
