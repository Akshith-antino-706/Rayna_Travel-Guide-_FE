## India Visa Options for Visiting Delhi

India requires most foreign nationals to obtain a visa before visiting. Unlike some countries that offer hassle-free visa-on-arrival for many nationalities, India's visa system requires advance planning. The good news is that the e-Visa process has been streamlined significantly, making it relatively simple for most travelers.

## Visa Types Compared

### e-Tourist Visa (e-TV) — Most Common for Tourists

The e-Visa is the most convenient option for leisure travelers and is available to nationals of 166+ countries.

**How it works:**
1. Apply online at indianvisaonline.gov.in at least 4 days before arrival
2. Upload passport photo and passport scan
3. Pay the fee online
4. Receive approval via email within 72 hours (usually faster)
5. Print the approval and present at immigration

**Options:**
| Type | Validity | Entries | Max Stay | Fee (Approx.) |
|------|----------|---------|----------|----------------|
| 30-day | 30 days from arrival | Double entry | 30 days | $10-25 |
| 1-year | 365 days from grant | Multiple | 180 days/year | $40-80 |
| 5-year | 5 years from grant | Multiple | 180 days/year | $80-100 |

**Fees vary by nationality.** US citizens typically pay $25 for 30-day and $75 for 1-year. UK and EU citizens often pay less.

**Key restrictions:**
- Valid for entry only through 28 designated airports (Delhi's IGI Airport is included)
- Must enter India within the visa's validity period
- Cannot be extended within India

### Regular Tourist Visa (Sticker Visa)

Applied for at Indian consulates or embassies in your home country. Requires an in-person or postal application.

**When to choose this:**
- Your country is not eligible for e-Visa
- You plan to stay longer than 180 days
- You need to enter through a land border (e-Visa is for air and sea entry only)
- You want the security of a physical visa stamp

**Process:**
- Visit the Indian consulate or use a visa service
- Submit application form, passport, photos, and supporting documents
- Processing time: 3-15 business days
- Fee: Varies by country ($10-160)

### Visa on Arrival (Very Limited)

India technically offers visa on arrival only to nationals of **Japan, South Korea, and UAE** — and this program has been intermittent. For most nationalities, there is **no visa on arrival** for India. The e-Visa must be obtained before travel.

**Important:** Do not arrive in Delhi without a valid visa or e-Visa approval. You will be denied entry.

## Step-by-Step e-Visa Application

1. **Visit** indianvisaonline.gov.in/evisa
2. **Select** "e-Tourist Visa" and your nationality
3. **Fill the application** — personal details, passport info, travel plans, and Indian reference (hotel address works)
4. **Upload documents:**
   - Passport-size photo (white background, face centered)
   - Passport bio page scan (PDF format)
5. **Pay online** — Visa, Mastercard, or selected payment methods
6. **Wait for approval** — typically 24-72 hours
7. **Print the ETA (Electronic Travel Authorization)** and carry it with your passport

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Photo rejected:** Use a plain white background, front-facing, no glasses
- **Wrong passport details:** Triple-check passport number and date of birth
- **Applying too late:** Apply at least 7 days before travel for safety, even though the minimum is 4 days
- **Not printing the ETA:** Carry a physical printout; digital copies may not be accepted

## Special Categories

### OCI (Overseas Citizen of India) Card
If you are of Indian origin, the OCI card provides lifelong visa-free travel to India with no stay limits. Apply through the Indian consulate.

### Business Visa
For business travel to Delhi, the e-Business Visa is available with different fee structures and conditions.

### Transit Visa
Not needed if staying in the international transit area. Required if you need to exit the airport during a layover.

## Documents to Carry

Regardless of visa type, carry these at immigration:
- Valid passport (6+ months validity, 2 blank pages)
- Printed e-Visa approval (if applicable)
- Return or onward flight booking
- Hotel booking confirmation
- Proof of sufficient funds (bank statement or credit card — rarely asked but good to have)
- Completed e-Arrival card (can be done online before arrival)

## Which Visa Should You Choose?

| Situation | Recommended Visa |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Short trip (under 30 days) | 30-day e-Visa |
| Multiple India trips planned | 1-year or 5-year e-Visa |
| First visit, unsure about plans | 1-year e-Visa (flexibility) |
| Not eligible for e-Visa | Regular tourist visa via consulate |
| Entering by land | Regular tourist visa |
| Indian origin | OCI card |

The e-Visa has made visiting Delhi simpler than ever. Apply early, double-check your details, and keep a printout with your passport for a smooth arrival.
