## Arriving at Delhi's Indira Gandhi International Airport

Indira Gandhi International Airport (IATA: DEL) is India's busiest airport and the gateway to the country for millions of international visitors each year. Terminal 3 (T3), which handles most international flights, is a modern, efficient facility that rivals the best airports in Asia. With some advance knowledge, your arrival can be smooth and stress-free.

## Airport Layout

### Terminal 3 (International)
The main international terminal is a striking glass and steel structure that opened in 2010. It handles most international airlines including Air India, Emirates, Singapore Airlines, British Airways, Lufthansa, and many others.

### Terminal 1 (Domestic)
Used primarily by domestic budget carriers like IndiGo and SpiceJet for some routes. If you are connecting to a domestic flight on a different terminal, allow at least 2-3 hours for the inter-terminal transfer.

### Terminal 2 (Domestic)
Serves additional domestic airlines. Connected to T3 by a shuttle bus.

## Step-by-Step Arrival Process

### 1. Immigration (30-60 minutes)
After deboarding, follow signs to Immigration. There are separate queues for Indian nationals, e-Visa holders, and OCI/PIO cardholders.

**E-Visa holders:** Have your printed e-Visa approval, passport, return flight booking, and hotel confirmation ready. Officers may ask about the purpose of visit and duration of stay.

**Documents to keep handy:**
- Passport with at least 6 months validity
- Printed e-Visa or visa stamp
- Return or onward flight ticket
- Hotel booking confirmation
- Completed e-Arrival card (can be filled online 72 hours before arrival at indianvisaonline.gov.in)

**Tip:** Immigration queues can be long during peak arrival times (midnight to 3:00 AM when many international flights land). The e-Arrival card system has reduced waiting times significantly.

### 2. Baggage Claim (15-30 minutes)
After immigration, proceed to baggage claim. Screens display carousel numbers by flight. Free luggage trolleys are available.

### 3. Customs
Most travelers can use the Green Channel (nothing to declare). The duty-free allowance includes 2 liters of alcohol, 100 cigarettes, and gifts/souvenirs up to ₹50,000 in value.

**Items requiring declaration:** More than $5,000 in cash (or equivalent), expensive electronics, and gold above the allowed limit.

### 4. Arrivals Hall
After exiting customs, you will enter the arrivals hall. Immediately available:
- **Currency exchange:** Thomas Cook and other authorized changers operate 24/7
- **SIM cards:** Airtel and Jio counters are in the arrivals area
- **ATMs:** Multiple ATMs from HDFC, SBI, ICICI, and others
- **Pre-paid taxi counter:** Delhi Traffic Police-operated booth for fixed-rate taxis
- **Hotel desks:** Many major hotels have representatives

## Getting to Your Hotel

### Airport Express Metro (Recommended)
The Orange Line Airport Express connects T3 to New Delhi Railway Station in just 20 minutes, with stops at Aerocity, Dhaula Kuan, and Shivaji Stadium. Clean, fast, and air-conditioned.
- **Fare:** ₹60 to New Delhi station
- **Hours:** 4:45 AM to 11:40 PM
- **Frequency:** Every 10-15 minutes
- **Where to find it:** Follow signs to Metro from the arrivals hall. The station is connected to T3 via a covered walkway.

### Pre-Paid Taxi
The safest taxi option for first-time visitors. Go to the Delhi Traffic Police pre-paid taxi counter in the arrivals hall, tell them your destination, pay the fixed fare, and receive a receipt. You will be assigned a specific taxi.
- **Fare:** ₹350-600 to most central Delhi destinations
- **AC surcharge:** ₹25-50 extra
- **Tip:** Keep the receipt until you reach your destination; do not pay anything extra to the driver

### Ride-Hailing Apps (Uber/Ola)
Both Uber and Ola operate at the airport. Book through the app and follow signs to the designated pickup area.
- **Fare:** ₹250-500 to central Delhi (varies by traffic and surge)
- **Pickup:** Designated ride-hailing pickup zone outside arrivals
- **Tip:** Requires a working phone with internet — get a SIM card first or use airport Wi-Fi

### Hotel Transfers
Most 4-star and 5-star hotels offer airport pickup. Confirm at the time of booking.
- **Cost:** ₹1,500-3,000 for luxury car service, sometimes complimentary for top-tier rooms
- **Benefit:** Driver waiting with your name, no hassle after a long flight

## Essential Airport Services

### SIM Cards
**Airtel** and **Jio** counters at the airport sell tourist SIM cards with data and calling packs.
- **Price:** ₹250-500 for 28-day plans with 1.5-2 GB daily data
- **Documents needed:** Passport and one passport-size photo
- **Activation time:** 1-4 hours (sometimes up to 24 hours)
- **Tip:** Buy the SIM at the airport but download offline maps in advance in case activation is delayed

### Currency Exchange
Exchange only ₹2,000-3,000 at the airport for immediate transport and food needs. Better rates are available in the city.

### Airport Wi-Fi
Free Wi-Fi is available at T3 for 45 minutes. Connect to the airport network and verify via OTP sent to your phone.

### Left Luggage
A left luggage facility operates 24/7 at T3.
- **Cost:** ₹100-200 per bag per day
- **Location:** Near departure gates

### Lounges
Several paid lounges are available in the arrivals area if you need to freshen up or rest before heading into the city. Access via Priority Pass, credit card benefits, or direct purchase (₹1,500-3,000).

## Common Arrival Mistakes to Avoid

- **Do not accept rides from unofficial taxi touts** who approach you inside the terminal — always use pre-paid taxis, the metro, or ride-hailing apps
- **Do not exchange large amounts of currency** at the airport — rates are worse than in the city
- **Do not forget to fill out the e-Arrival card** before landing — it saves significant time at immigration
- **Do not leave without a SIM card** — you will need it for ride-hailing, maps, and communication
- **Do not carry prohibited items** through customs — check India's customs regulations before packing
- **Do not expect your SIM to work immediately** — have your hotel address written down as backup
