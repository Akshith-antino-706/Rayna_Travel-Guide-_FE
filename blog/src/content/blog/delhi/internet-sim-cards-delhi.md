## Staying Connected in Delhi

Having reliable internet access in Delhi is essential for navigation, ride-hailing, translation, and staying in touch. The good news is that India has some of the cheapest mobile data in the world, and getting connected is straightforward — though the process involves a bit more paperwork than in some countries.

## Getting a SIM Card

### Best Providers for Tourists

**Jio (Reliance Jio)**
- India's largest and newest network with excellent 4G/5G coverage
- Best for data-heavy users
- Tourist plans: ₹249-599 for 28 days with 1.5-2 GB daily data
- Available at the airport and Jio stores across the city

**Airtel**
- India's most established private network with reliable coverage and customer service
- Best overall choice for tourists
- Tourist plans: ₹299-699 for 28 days with 1.5-2.5 GB daily data
- Strong coverage even in metro tunnels and rural areas

**Vi (Vodafone Idea)**
- Decent coverage in Delhi, but network quality has been inconsistent
- Cheapest plans: ₹199-499 for 28 days
- Not recommended as a first choice for tourists

### Where to Buy

**At the Airport (Recommended)**
Airtel and Jio have counters in the arrivals area of Terminal 3. The staff are accustomed to tourist purchases and the process is relatively quick.
- **Wait time:** 15-30 minutes
- **Activation:** 1-4 hours (sometimes up to 24 hours)
- **Documents needed:** Passport, visa, one passport-size photo (some counters can take a photo digitally)

**In the City**
Official Airtel and Jio stores are found in most markets and malls. Connaught Place, Khan Market, and Saket malls all have stores.
- **Wait time:** 20-45 minutes
- **Activation:** 2-24 hours
- **Tip:** Visit a company-owned store rather than a small retailer for smoother processing

### The Activation Wait

Indian SIM cards require identity verification through a government database, which causes the activation delay. During this wait:
- Use your hotel Wi-Fi
- Download offline Google Maps of Delhi before leaving your hotel
- Keep your hotel's address and phone number written down

## Wi-Fi Availability

### Hotels
All Delhi hotels, including budget ones, offer Wi-Fi. Quality varies:
- **Luxury hotels:** Fast, reliable connections (30-100 Mbps)
- **Mid-range hotels:** Generally adequate (10-30 Mbps)
- **Budget hotels:** Variable and sometimes slow (2-10 Mbps)

### Cafes and Restaurants
Most cafes, Starbucks locations, and mid-to-upscale restaurants offer free Wi-Fi. Ask for the password when ordering.

### Delhi Metro
Free Wi-Fi is available on the Airport Express Line through Tata Communications. Coverage on other lines is limited.

### Public Wi-Fi
The Delhi government has set up Wi-Fi hotspots at some public locations, but connectivity is unreliable. Do not depend on public Wi-Fi.

## Using Your International Phone

### International Roaming
Check with your home provider about international data roaming in India. Rates vary wildly — some plans include India, others charge ₹1,000+ per GB.

### eSIM Options
If your phone supports eSIM, services like Airalo, Holafly, and Nomad offer India eSIM plans that activate instantly — no paperwork or waiting. Plans start around $5-10 for 1-5 GB.
- **Pros:** Instant activation, no documents needed
- **Cons:** More expensive per GB than local SIM, data-only (no local phone number for calls)

## Tips for Staying Connected

- **Buy your SIM at the airport** — it is the easiest process for tourists
- **Download offline maps** before arriving or during the SIM activation wait
- **Carry a portable charger** — constant app use drains batteries fast
- **WhatsApp is king in India** — almost every business, tour operator, and contact uses WhatsApp for communication
- **VPN:** India does not block major websites, but a VPN is useful for accessing geo-restricted content from your home country
- **Save important info offline** — hotel addresses, emergency numbers, and booking confirmations should not depend on data availability
- **Data recharges** can be done through the Jio or Airtel apps, Paytm, or at any corner shop displaying the provider's logo
