## Online Booking Safety for Delhi Travel

Booking travel online for Delhi is not only safe — it is generally the smartest way to plan your trip. Online platforms offer transparent pricing, verified reviews, secure payment, and cancellation protection that you simply cannot get from cash transactions with local operators. That said, knowing which platforms to trust and what red flags to watch for makes all the difference.

## Trusted Booking Platforms

### Hotels
- **Booking.com:** Excellent for Delhi with wide inventory, free cancellation options, and verified reviews
- **Expedia / Hotels.com:** Reliable with reward programs that offer free nights
- **MakeMyTrip:** India's largest online travel agent — often has exclusive Delhi hotel deals
- **Goibibo:** Popular Indian platform with competitive pricing
- **Agoda:** Strong in Asia with good Delhi coverage
- **Direct hotel websites:** Often the best rates and most flexible cancellation

### Tours and Activities
- **GetYourGuide:** Curated activities with verified reviews and free cancellation
- **Viator (TripAdvisor):** Large inventory of Delhi tours with review transparency
- **Klook:** Growing India presence with competitive prices
- **Airbnb Experiences:** Unique local-hosted experiences (cooking classes, photo tours)

### Flights
- **Google Flights:** Best for comparing fares across airlines
- **Skyscanner:** Excellent for finding the cheapest Delhi flights
- **Direct airline websites:** Best for managing bookings and changes
- **MakeMyTrip / Cleartrip:** Good for domestic Indian flights

### Train Tickets
- **IRCTC (irctc.co.in):** The only official platform for Indian Railways tickets. Create an account well before your trip as the registration process includes OTP verification.

## Red Flags to Watch For

### Fake Websites
- **Too-good-to-be-true prices** — if a 5-star hotel is 70% cheaper than any other platform, it is likely a scam
- **Poor website design** with grammatical errors and broken links
- **No HTTPS** in the website URL (look for the padlock icon)
- **No verifiable contact information** — no phone number, physical address, or clear company identity
- **Pressure tactics** — "Only 1 room left!" or countdown timers

### Fake Reviews
- **All 5-star reviews** with generic language ("Amazing!" "Best ever!")
- **Reviews posted in clusters** on the same dates
- **No photos or detailed descriptions** in reviews
- **Reviewer profiles** with only one review (likely created for that purpose)

### Scam Operators
- **Request payment by wire transfer** or cryptocurrency instead of credit card
- **No cancellation policy** or vague terms
- **Cannot provide references** or verifiable business registration
- **Insist on full prepayment** for tours without any platform backing
- **Use personal email addresses** (Gmail, Yahoo) instead of business domains

## Payment Security Tips

- **Use credit cards** rather than debit cards — credit cards offer better fraud protection and chargeback rights
- **Avoid bank transfers** to unknown operators — you have almost no recourse if something goes wrong
- **Use platform payment** — book and pay through GetYourGuide or Viator rather than being redirected to a separate payment page
- **Check your statements** after booking for unauthorized charges
- **Enable transaction alerts** on your card for immediate notification of any charges
- **Never share card details** via email or WhatsApp — always use secure payment pages

## Booking Tips for Best Results

- **Read the 2-3 star reviews** — they are often more informative than 5-star or 1-star reviews
- **Check review dates** — a hotel that was excellent 3 years ago may have deteriorated
- **Compare prices across 3 platforms** before booking — rates can vary by 15-30%
- **Book cancellable rates** when possible, especially for trips 30+ days away
- **Screenshot your booking confirmation** immediately after booking
- **Verify directly** — after booking through a platform, call the hotel to confirm your reservation
- **Book early for peak season** (October-March) — popular properties sell out months in advance

## What to Do If Something Goes Wrong

1. **Contact the booking platform first** — they have dispute resolution processes
2. **Document everything** — screenshots, emails, chat transcripts, photos
3. **Escalate through platform** — most have tiered complaint systems
4. **Credit card chargeback** — if the platform does not resolve the issue, dispute the charge with your credit card company
5. **Review your experience** — honest reviews help other travelers and motivate businesses to improve
6. **Travel insurance** may cover financial losses from booking fraud or operator failure

## The Bottom Line

Online booking for Delhi is safe when you use established platforms, verified operators, and secure payment methods. The transparency and protection offered by major booking platforms far outweigh the risks — and are significantly safer than cash transactions with unknown local operators at tourist sites.
