## Delhi's Legendary Markets

Delhi's markets are not just shopping destinations — they are cultural experiences, sensory spectacles, and windows into the city's soul. From the 350-year-old lanes of Chandni Chowk to the fashionable boutiques of Khan Market, each market has its own character, specialty, and rhythm. Here is your guide to Delhi's best shopping districts.

## Old Delhi Markets

### Chandni Chowk
Delhi's oldest and most famous market, built by Shah Jahan in the 17th century. The main road and its branching lanes specialize in different products, and the experience of navigating the crowds is unforgettable.

**Specialty areas:**
- **Dariba Kalan:** Silver jewelry and gemstones — one of Asia's oldest jewelry markets
- **Kinari Bazaar:** Wedding accessories, decorative borders, and ceremonial items
- **Khari Baoli:** Asia's largest spice market — the aromas are overwhelming
- **Nai Sarak:** Books and stationery at wholesale prices
- **Ballimaran:** Footwear, especially traditional juttis (embroidered shoes)

**Bargaining:** Essential — start at 40% of the asking price
**Hours:** 10:00 AM to 8:00 PM (many shops closed Sundays)
**Metro:** Chandni Chowk (Yellow Line)
**Tips:** Go early to avoid peak crowds. Wear comfortable shoes. Keep valuables secure.

### Meena Bazaar (Near Jama Masjid)
Adjacent to Jama Masjid, this market specializes in fabric, readymade garments, embroidered goods, and traditional Muslim attire. Excellent for buying kurtas, sherwanis, and embroidered textiles at wholesale prices.

## South Delhi Markets

### Khan Market
Delhi's most upscale market and one of the most expensive retail spaces in India. This L-shaped market houses boutiques, bookshops, international brand outlets, cafes, and restaurants.

**Best for:** Books (Bahrisons, Full Circle), boutique fashion, artisanal jewelry, gourmet food
**Price range:** Premium — minimal bargaining
**Hours:** 10:00 AM to 8:00 PM (closed Sundays)
**Metro:** Khan Market (Violet Line)

### Sarojini Nagar Market
Delhi's most popular street market for fashion, known for export surplus and rejected stock from international brands at throwaway prices. You can find branded clothing, shoes, and accessories for ₹100-500.

**Best for:** Budget fashion, export surplus clothing, everyday wear
**Bargaining:** Aggressive — start at 30% of asking price
**Hours:** 10:00 AM to 9:00 PM (closed Mondays)
**Metro:** INA station (Yellow Line), then auto-rickshaw
**Tips:** Visit on weekdays to avoid crushing weekend crowds

### Lajpat Nagar Central Market
A bustling South Delhi market popular for women's clothing, home furnishings, and accessories. Less touristy than Sarojini Nagar but equally rewarding.

**Best for:** Indian ethnic wear, wedding shopping, home decor, mehndi (henna) artists
**Bargaining:** Expected
**Hours:** 10:00 AM to 9:00 PM (closed Mondays)
**Metro:** Lajpat Nagar (Violet Line)

## Government-Run Markets

### Dilli Haat, INA
A must-visit for tourists. This open-air bazaar is run by the Delhi Tourism department and features handicraft stalls from every Indian state, rotating monthly. No bargaining drama, fair prices, and authentic regional products.

**Best for:** Handicrafts, textiles, jewelry, paintings, and regional food stalls from across India
**Entry:** ₹30 (Indians), ₹100 (foreigners)
**Hours:** 10:30 AM to 10:00 PM daily
**Metro:** INA station (Yellow Line)
**Tip:** The food stalls here offer authentic cuisine from different Indian states — try Rajasthani dal bati, Nagaland pork, or Kerala fish curry

### Central Cottage Industries Emporium, Janpath
A government-run multi-story store selling authentic Indian handicrafts at fixed prices. No bargaining needed, quality is guaranteed, and everything is genuinely handmade.

**Best for:** High-quality handicrafts, textiles, jewelry, wood carvings, and gifts
**Prices:** Fixed (slightly higher than market, but quality guaranteed)
**Hours:** 10:00 AM to 7:00 PM daily
**Location:** Janpath Road, near Connaught Place

### Janpath Market
The street market on Janpath Road is popular with tourists for Tibetan jewelry, cotton clothing, leather goods, and souvenirs. Persistent vendors make bargaining an art form.

**Best for:** Souvenirs, hippie clothing, Tibetan handicrafts
**Bargaining:** Heavy — start at 25-30% of asking price
**Hours:** 10:00 AM to 8:00 PM (closed Sundays)

## Specialty Markets

### Nehru Place
Asia's largest IT market with hundreds of shops selling computers, laptops, accessories, and software. Genuine and counterfeit goods exist side by side — buy from established shops only.

### Lodhi Colony Art District
Not a traditional market, but the walls of Lodhi Colony have been transformed into an open-air street art gallery. Visit for photography and to see Delhi's contemporary art scene.

### INA Market
One of Delhi's best food markets, selling fresh produce, imported foods, gourmet ingredients, and kitchen equipment. Great for food enthusiasts.

## Bargaining Guide

### Where to Bargain
- Chandni Chowk and Old Delhi markets
- Sarojini Nagar and Lajpat Nagar
- Janpath Market
- Auto-rickshaw fares

### Where NOT to Bargain
- Khan Market boutiques
- Government emporiums (Central Cottage Industries, Dilli Haat)
- Shopping malls
- Restaurants and cafes
- Branded stores

### Bargaining Tips
1. **Know the approximate value** before starting — ask prices at 2-3 shops
2. **Start at 40% of asking price** and settle at 60-70%
3. **Walk away** if the price does not come down — vendors often call you back with a better offer
4. **Be friendly** — bargaining should be enjoyable, not adversarial
5. **Bundle purchases** for better discounts — buying multiple items gives leverage
6. **Carry small denominations** — vendors may claim they cannot give change for large notes
7. **Morning shoppers** sometimes get better prices as the first sale of the day is considered lucky
