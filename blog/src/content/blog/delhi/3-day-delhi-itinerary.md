## Three Days in Delhi: A Complete Guide

Three days is the sweet spot for experiencing Delhi's highlights without feeling rushed. This itinerary balances iconic monuments with local neighborhoods, street food with fine dining, and history with modern culture. Each day focuses on a different part of the city to minimize travel time and maximize experiences.

## Day 1: Old Delhi — Mughal Heritage and Street Food

### Morning (8:00 AM – 12:30 PM)

Start at **Jama Masjid**, arriving early when the mosque is peaceful and the light is perfect for photography. Climb the southern minaret (₹100) for panoramic views of Old Delhi. From here, walk into **Chandni Chowk** for breakfast at **Paranthe Wali Gali** — order the stuffed aloo or gobhi paratha with mint chutney (₹50-100 each).

Spend an hour exploring the lanes of Chandni Chowk: visit the **Spice Market** at Khari Baoli (the largest spice market in Asia), peek into **Gurudwara Sis Ganj Sahib** (the Sikh temple commemorating Guru Tegh Bahadur), and browse the fabric and wedding shops along the main road.

At 10:00 AM, head to the **Red Fort**. Allow 2 hours to explore the Diwan-i-Aam (Hall of Public Audience), Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience), the Mughal gardens, and the museum inside. Entry: ₹600 for foreigners.

### Afternoon (12:30 PM – 5:00 PM)

Lunch at **Karim's** near Jama Masjid — this legendary restaurant has been serving Mughal cuisine since 1913. Try the mutton burra kebab (₹250-350) or the chicken Jahangiri (₹300-400). The experience is as much about the atmosphere as the food.

After lunch, visit **Raj Ghat** (free entry), Gandhi's memorial. Then take the metro to **Nizamuddin Dargah**, the Sufi shrine of Hazrat Nizamuddin Auliya. If you visit on a Thursday evening, you can witness mesmerizing qawwali (Sufi devotional music) performances.

### Evening (5:00 PM – 9:00 PM)

Head to **Humayun's Tomb** (₹600 foreigners) to catch the late afternoon light on this gorgeous UNESCO site. End the day with dinner at **Chor Bizarre** at Hotel Broadway (₹800-1,500 per person) for eclectic North Indian cuisine in a quirky antique-filled setting.

## Day 2: New Delhi — Colonial Grandeur and Modern Culture

### Morning (8:00 AM – 12:30 PM)

Begin at **India Gate** and walk along **Kartavya Path** (formerly Rajpath) toward **Rashtrapati Bhawan** for photos of the grand presidential palace. The avenue is lined with manicured gardens and government buildings designed by Edwin Lutyens.

Head to **Gurudwara Bangla Sahib**, one of Delhi's most prominent Sikh temples. The serene atmosphere, the beautiful sarovar (sacred pool), and the free community kitchen (langar) serving thousands daily make this a moving cultural experience. Free entry.

Visit the **National Museum** on Janpath Road (₹20 Indians, ₹650 foreigners), which houses over 200,000 works of art spanning 5,000 years. Highlights include the Harappan gallery, the Buddhist sculpture collection, and the manuscript room. Allow 1.5-2 hours.

### Afternoon (12:30 PM – 5:30 PM)

Lunch at **Saravana Bhavan** in Connaught Place for an excellent South Indian thali (₹250-400), or try **United Coffee House** for continental and Indian classics in a grand colonial-era setting (₹600-1,000 per person).

After lunch, explore **Connaught Place** — browse the shops at **Janpath Market** for souvenirs (bargain hard — start at 40% of the asking price), visit **Hanuman Mandir** on Baba Kharak Singh Marg, and stop for cold coffee at one of the many cafes.

Take the metro to **Lotus Temple** in the afternoon. This stunning white marble Bahá'í House of Worship is shaped like a lotus flower with 27 free-standing petals. Free entry, closed Mondays. Allow 45 minutes.

### Evening (5:30 PM – 9:00 PM)

Head to **Khan Market**, one of Delhi's most upscale shopping and dining districts. Browse bookshops, boutiques, and handicraft stores, then dine at **Big Chill** for Italian comfort food (₹500-900 per person) or **Perch Wine & Coffee Bar** for a European-style evening (₹800-1,500 per person).

## Day 3: South Delhi — Culture, Markets, and Monuments

### Morning (8:00 AM – 12:30 PM)

Start early at **Qutub Minar** (₹600 foreigners), arriving at opening time to beat the crowds. This 72.5-meter UNESCO World Heritage Site is surrounded by fascinating ruins including the Iron Pillar, Alai Darwaza, and the partially built Alai Minar.

From Qutub Minar, drive to **Mehrauli Archaeological Park**, a lesser-known treasure containing over 100 historically significant structures spanning multiple dynasties. It is free and rarely crowded.

Visit **Garden of Five Senses** nearby, a beautifully landscaped 20-acre park with art installations, amphitheaters, and themed gardens (₹35 entry).

### Afternoon (12:30 PM – 5:30 PM)

Lunch at **Diva** in Greater Kailash for award-winning Italian cuisine by Chef Ritu Dalmia (₹1,000-1,800 per person), or for a budget option, try **Sagar Ratna** for South Indian dishes (₹200-350 per person).

Spend the afternoon exploring **Hauz Khas Village** — start with the medieval ruins and lake, then wander through the galleries, boutiques, and cafes. This neighborhood perfectly captures Delhi's ability to blend ancient heritage with contemporary creativity.

If time permits, visit **Safdarjung's Tomb** (₹25 Indians, ₹300 foreigners), a beautiful example of late Mughal architecture that is often less crowded than the bigger monuments.

### Evening (5:30 PM – 9:00 PM)

For your final evening, head to **Lodhi Gardens** for a sunset walk among 15th-century tombs and rose gardens. Then choose your farewell dinner:

- **Indian Accent** at The Lodhi for progressive Indian cuisine — arguably Delhi's finest restaurant (₹4,000-6,000 per person, reservations essential)
- **Bukhara** at ITC Maurya for legendary dal bukhara and tandoori dishes (₹4,000-6,000 per person)
- **SodaBottleOpenerWala** in Khan Market for Parsi-Irani cafe food at a more modest price (₹600-1,000 per person)

## Practical Tips for 3 Days in Delhi

- **Stay centrally** — Connaught Place, Paharganj, or South Delhi offer the best access to attractions
- **Get a 3-day metro tourist card** (₹500) for unlimited rides
- **Pre-book Akshardham tickets** online if adding it to your itinerary
- **Carry cash** — many Old Delhi shops and street food stalls do not accept cards
- **Keep mornings for outdoor sights** and afternoons for indoor or shaded attractions
- **Download Delhi Metro Rail app** for route planning

Three days lets you scratch beneath Delhi's surface and discover why this ancient capital continues to fascinate visitors from around the world.
