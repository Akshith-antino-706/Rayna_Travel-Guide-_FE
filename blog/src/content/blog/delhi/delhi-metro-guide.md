## Delhi Metro: Your Best Friend in Delhi

The Delhi Metro, operated by DMRC (Delhi Metro Rail Corporation), is one of the world's best metro systems and by far the most efficient way to navigate India's capital. With over 290 stations across 390+ km, it connects virtually every tourist area and operates with remarkable punctuality.

- **Rajiv Chowk** — Yellow + Blue Lines (the busiest station, avoid during rush hour)
- **Kashmere Gate** — Red + Yellow + Violet Lines
- **Central Secretariat** — Yellow + Violet Lines
- **Mandi House** — Blue + Violet Lines
- **Hauz Khas** — Yellow + Magenta Lines

| 21-32 km | ₹54 | ₹49 |
| 32+ km | ₹64 | ₹58 |

### Payment Options

**Tokens:** Single-use plastic coins purchased at station counters or automatic vending machines. Best for occasional rides.

**Smart Card:** Reusable card with 10% discount on every journey. Costs ₹150 (₹50 refundable deposit + ₹100 initial balance). Available at any station counter. Can be recharged at stations or online.

**Tourist Card:** 1-day unlimited travel for ₹200, 3-day unlimited for ₹500. Excellent value if you plan to use the metro frequently.

**QR-Code Tickets:** Available through the DMRC app, Paytm, PhonePe, and WhatsApp. Scan to enter and exit. The most modern option.

## Station Facilities

Most Delhi Metro stations offer:
- **Clean restrooms** (free)
- **ATMs** and cash machines
- **Drinking water** (filtered water dispensers on platforms)
- **Escalators and elevators** for accessibility
- **Information kiosks** with English-speaking staff
- **Security screening** at every station entrance (bags are X-rayed, you walk through a metal detector)
- **First aid** available at station control rooms

## Rules and Etiquette

### Prohibited Items
- No food or drink (including water) inside trains and stations
- No smoking
- Luggage over 15 kg per bag not allowed (max 2 bags)
- No inflammable materials
- Fines: ₹200 for eating/drinking, ₹500 for smoking

### Behavioral Guidelines
- **Women's coach:** The first coach of every train is reserved for women — men will be fined ₹250 for traveling in it
- **Offer seats** to elderly, pregnant women, and disabled passengers (marked priority seats)
- **Stand on the right** of escalators, walk on the left
- **Let passengers exit** before boarding
- **Keep belongings secure** — petty theft, while rare, can happen in crowded trains

## Practical Tips

- **Rush hours (8:30-10:00 AM, 5:30-7:30 PM)** — trains are extremely crowded. If possible, travel outside these windows
- **Last train** varies by line but is generally around 11:00-11:30 PM. Plan your evening accordingly
- **Google Maps** integrates with Delhi Metro routing — just enter your destination and it will suggest the best metro route
- **Download the DMRC Travel app** for real-time updates, route planning, and QR ticket purchase
- **Keep your token/card** until you exit — you need it to pass through the exit gates
- **Charge your Smart Card** before it drops below ₹20 — you cannot enter if the balance is too low for the minimum fare
- **Photography** is technically restricted in the metro, though casual tourist photos are rarely questioned. Professional photography requires DMRC permission.

## Airport Express Line

The dedicated Airport Express Line (Orange Line) is the fastest way between the airport and the city center.

- **Route:** Dwarka Sector 21 → Aerocity → IGI Airport T3 → Dhaula Kuan → Delhi Aero City → Shivaji Stadium → New Delhi
- **Journey time:** Airport to New Delhi in 20 minutes
- **Fare:** ₹60 from airport to New Delhi
- **Frequency:** Every 10-15 minutes
- **Hours:** 4:45 AM to 11:40 PM
- **Features:** Wider seats, luggage racks, faster speeds
