## Is Delhi Safe? An Honest Assessment

Delhi's safety reputation is worse than its reality. Millions of tourists visit Delhi each year without incident, and with basic awareness and precautions, your visit is very likely to be trouble-free. That said, Delhi is a massive, developing-world megacity — it has different risks than visiting Tokyo or Zurich. This guide provides a balanced, practical assessment.

## The Short Answer

Delhi is **generally safe for tourists** who exercise normal travel precautions. Violent crime against tourists is rare. The most common issues are petty scams, overcharging, and stomach problems from unfamiliar food and water. These are annoyances, not dangers, and are easily managed with awareness.

## Common Tourist Scams

### The "Closed" Attraction Scam
A friendly person near a tourist site tells you the attraction is closed today (for a holiday, renovation, etc.) and offers to take you to another place instead — usually a shop where they earn commission. **Reality:** Check hours online. Attractions rarely close without notice.

### The Inflated Taxi/Auto Fare
Drivers at airports, stations, and tourist sites quote wildly inflated fares. **Solution:** Use the pre-paid taxi counter, ride-hailing apps, or agree on a fare before getting in.

### The Gem/Carpet Shop Scam
A friendly local befriends you and eventually leads you to a shop where they pressure you to buy gems, carpets, or handicrafts at inflated prices, sometimes with a story about reselling them for profit in your home country. **Reality:** Walk away.

### The "Tourist Office" Scam
Fake tourist information offices near New Delhi Railway Station and Connaught Place try to sell overpriced tour packages or redirect you to commission-paying hotels. **Solution:** India Tourism's official office is at 88 Janpath — do not trust random offices with similar-sounding names.

### The Charitable Donation Scam
Someone approaches asking for donations to a temple, school, or charity with an official-looking receipt book. Most are fake. **Solution:** If you want to donate, go directly to the organization.

## Areas and Safety

### Safe Tourist Areas
- Connaught Place (well-policed, well-lit)
- Khan Market and South Delhi neighborhoods
- Aerocity (modern, secure)
- Museum complexes and major monuments
- Metro stations (security screened, CCTV monitored)

### Areas Requiring More Caution
- Paharganj at night (petty crime, drug solicitation)
- Isolated areas after dark
- Crowded markets during festivals (pickpocketing)
- Railway station surroundings (touts and scams)

## Women's Safety

Delhi has a problematic reputation for women's safety, and it would be dishonest to ignore this. However, context matters — most incidents involve locals, not tourists, and tourist-frequented areas are well-policed.

### Practical Tips for Women
- **Travel with companions** after dark, or use ride-hailing apps rather than walking
- **Dress modestly** — this does not prevent harassment but reduces unwanted attention
- **Use women's coaches** on the Delhi Metro
- **Trust your instincts** — if a situation feels wrong, leave
- **Keep your hotel's number on speed dial**
- **Avoid isolated areas** at night, including empty streets and parks
- **Many restaurants and bars have women's sections** or are well-supervised
- **Pre-book transport** rather than flagging autos or taxis on the street at night

### Women-Only Services
- Delhi Metro women's coach
- Women-only Uber option (where available)
- Women helpline: 181

## Health Safety

### Water and Food
- **Never drink tap water** — only bottled or filtered
- **Check bottle seals** — ensure they have not been refilled
- **Ease into street food** — start with cooked, hot items from busy stalls
- **Carry hand sanitizer** and use it before eating
- **ORS packets** are available at every pharmacy for rehydration

### Air Quality
- **Check AQI daily** during November-February
- **Carry N95 masks** for high-pollution days
- **Stay indoors** when AQI exceeds 300 if you have respiratory conditions
- **Hotels with air purifiers** are worth the investment in winter months

## General Safety Tips

- **Keep photocopies of your passport** — store originals in the hotel safe
- **Use hotel safes** for valuables, extra cash, and backup cards
- **Share your itinerary** with someone at home
- **Travel insurance** is non-negotiable — ensure it covers medical emergencies
- **Be aware in crowds** — pickpocketing occurs in busy markets and metro
- **Do not flash expensive jewelry, cameras, or large amounts of cash**
- **Use ATMs inside banks** rather than standalone machines
- **Trust registered guides** only — verify credentials, or book through your hotel
- **Do not accept food or drinks from strangers** — drug-facilitated robbery, while rare, has been reported

## Perspective

Delhi has the same issues as any large city — some areas are safer than others, some people try to take advantage of tourists, and common sense goes a long way. The vast majority of people you encounter will be genuinely friendly and helpful. India's culture of hospitality means strangers will often go out of their way to assist you.

With reasonable precautions — the same you would take in any major city — Delhi is a safe and deeply rewarding destination.
