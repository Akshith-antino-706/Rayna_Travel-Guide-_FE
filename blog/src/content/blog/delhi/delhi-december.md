## Delhi in December

December brings Delhi's full winter character — crisp mornings, foggy dawns, cozy chai sessions, and a festive atmosphere leading up to Christmas and New Year. It is peak tourist season with the best weather for sightseeing (when the fog lifts), though air quality concerns and cold nights require preparation.

## Weather

- **Average high:** 23°C (73°F)
- **Average low:** 8°C (46°F), dropping to 4-5°C on the coldest nights
- **Rainfall:** Minimal (5-10 mm)
- **Humidity:** Low
- **Sunshine hours:** 6-7 hours per day

### What to Expect
December mornings are cold and frequently foggy, especially in the second half of the month. Dense fog can delay flights by hours and reduce visibility dramatically before 10:00 AM. Once the fog burns off, afternoons are pleasant and sunny at 18-23°C. Evenings turn cold quickly after sunset (around 5:15 PM).

**Air quality:** December air quality varies significantly day to day. Some days are clear and pleasant, others see AQI levels in the "poor" to "very poor" range. Check real-time readings daily.

## Christmas in Delhi

While India is predominantly Hindu and Muslim, Delhi's cosmopolitan character means Christmas is celebrated with enthusiasm.

### Where to Experience Christmas
- **Sacred Heart Cathedral** — Delhi's largest Catholic church holds midnight mass on Christmas Eve with beautiful choral music
- **St. James' Church, Kashmere Gate** — Delhi's oldest church, built in 1836, holds special services
- **Connaught Place** — the central shopping area is decorated with lights and Christmas displays
- **Select Citywalk Mall** — elaborate Christmas decorations, Santa meets, and holiday events
- **Hotel celebrations** — luxury hotels host Christmas Eve dinners and brunches (₹3,000-8,000 per person)

### Christmas Markets
- **German Christmas Market** at The Imperial or similar venues (check annual listings)
- **Dastkar Nature Bazaar** for handcrafted Christmas gifts
- **Khan Market and Lodhi Colony** boutiques for unique gift shopping

## New Year's Eve

Delhi offers a range of New Year celebrations:
- **Hotel parties** at The Oberoi, Taj Palace, ITC Maurya, and others (₹8,000-25,000 per couple)
- **Club and bar parties** at CP and South Delhi venues (₹2,000-8,000 per person)
- **India Gate** — locals gather to celebrate at midnight (crowded but festive and free)
- **Quiet dinners** at restaurants offering special New Year menus

**Note:** Alcohol sales are restricted on certain dates around New Year — check local announcements.

## Things to Do in December

### Winter Food Experiences
December is peak season for Delhi's most comforting foods:
- **Sarson ka saag and makki ki roti** at any Punjabi dhaba — the ultimate winter comfort food
- **Nihari** (slow-cooked stew) at Old Delhi's stalls — traditionally a breakfast dish eaten on cold mornings
- **Gajar ka halwa** — warm carrot pudding available at sweet shops citywide
- **Kulhad chai** — tea served in small clay cups at roadside stalls, especially soulful in the December cold

### Cultural Activities
- **Qutub Festival** (if scheduled) — a cultural music and dance festival at Qutub Minar
- **Theater season** — Delhi's best theater is in winter, with productions at India Habitat Centre, NSD, and Kamani Auditorium
- **Art exhibitions** at National Gallery of Modern Art and galleries in Lado Sarai

### Outdoor Activities
- **Sunrise at India Gate** (after fog clears) for beautiful winter light
- **Bird watching at Okhla Bird Sanctuary** — peak migratory bird season with species from Siberia and Central Asia
- **Lodhi Gardens** for crisp morning walks
- **Heritage walks** in the comfortable midday weather

## Packing for December

- **Warm winter coat** — essential, not optional
- **Layers** — thermals, sweaters, and a jacket for mornings
- **Scarf, gloves, and warm hat** for early mornings and evenings
- **Closed-toe shoes** with warm socks
- **Moisturizer and lip balm** — the cold, dry air is harsh on skin
- **N95 mask** for poor air quality days
- **Portable hand warmers** — useful for very cold mornings

## Practical Tips

- **Flight delays are frequent** — Delhi's fog is notorious for disrupting air travel. Book afternoon flights when possible, and build buffer time into connections
- **Train delays** are also common — North Indian trains can be delayed by 2-8 hours due to fog
- **Hotels book up fast** — December is peak season with Christmas, New Year, and wedding season converging. Book well in advance
- **Wedding season:** December is one of Delhi's biggest wedding months. Hotels may be fully booked on certain auspicious dates, and some roads may be blocked by wedding processions (baraats)
- **Start sightseeing after 10:00 AM** when fog clears, and finish outdoor activities by 5:00 PM
- **Carry a power bank** — cold weather drains phone batteries faster

## December Events

- **Christmas Eve and Day** (December 24-25) — church services, hotel celebrations
- **New Year's Eve** (December 31) — parties across the city
- **Winter wedding season** — spectacular celebrations across Delhi
- **Cultural festivals** — music, dance, and theater events at various venues
