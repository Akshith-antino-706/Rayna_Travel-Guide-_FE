## Delhi: A Vegetarian Paradise

Delhi is arguably the best city in the world for vegetarians. In a country where roughly 30-40% of the population is vegetarian, meat-free food is not an afterthought — it is an art form perfected over centuries. From the chole bhature that fuels Delhi's mornings to the paneer tikka that graces evening gatherings, vegetarian food in Delhi is rich, diverse, and extraordinarily satisfying.

For vegans, Delhi is also increasingly accommodating, with traditional dishes that happen to be naturally vegan and a growing number of explicitly vegan restaurants.

## Must-Try Vegetarian Dishes

### Street Food Essentials
- **Chole Bhature:** Spiced chickpeas with puffy fried bread — Delhi's iconic breakfast
- **Aloo Tikki:** Spiced potato patties with chutneys and yogurt
- **Golgappe (Pani Puri):** Crispy shells filled with tangy spiced water and potato
- **Dahi Bhalle:** Lentil dumplings in yogurt with chutneys
- **Stuffed Parathas:** Flatbread stuffed with potato, cauliflower, paneer, or radish
- **Chaat varieties:** Papdi chaat, bhel puri, aloo chaat — all tangy, spicy, and addictive

### Restaurant Classics
- **Dal Makhani:** Slow-cooked black lentils with butter and cream — Delhi's signature dish
- **Paneer Tikka:** Marinated cottage cheese grilled in a tandoor
- **Rajma Chawal:** Kidney bean curry with rice — Delhi's ultimate comfort food
- **Palak Paneer:** Spinach and cottage cheese curry
- **Malai Kofta:** Paneer and potato dumplings in creamy gravy
- **Baingan Bharta:** Smoky roasted eggplant mash

## Best Vegetarian Restaurants

### Pure Vegetarian Chains
**Saravana Bhavan, Connaught Place**
Chennai's famous vegetarian chain serves impeccable South Indian food. The dosa varieties, idli, and filter coffee are outstanding. The thali offers the best value. ₹200-400 per person.

**Haldiram's, Multiple Locations**
India's largest vegetarian snack chain has full-service restaurants serving North and South Indian vegetarian food, plus their famous sweets and namkeen (savory snacks). ₹200-500 per person.

**Sagar Ratna, Multiple Locations**
Excellent South Indian vegetarian food with over 20 Delhi locations. The rava dosa and masala dosa are consistently good. ₹200-400 per person.

### Fine Dining Vegetarian
**Indian Accent, The Lodhi**
While not exclusively vegetarian, the vegetarian tasting menu is a revelation — dishes like daulat ki chaat, truffle papad, and wild mushroom galouti redefine what vegetarian Indian food can be. ₹4,000-6,000 per person.

**Varq, Taj Mahal Hotel**
Elegant Indian dining with a dedicated vegetarian menu that treats each dish with the same seriousness as the non-vegetarian offerings. ₹3,000-4,500 per person.

**Burma Burma, Multiple Locations**
Burmese-inspired vegetarian restaurant with unique dishes like Khow Suey, Burmese Thali, and Samosa Soup. Modern, fun atmosphere. ₹700-1,200 per person.

### Casual Vegetarian Favorites
**Evergreen Sweet Corner, Green Park**
A Delhi institution for chaat, sweets, and vegetarian snacks. The raj kachori and aloo tikki are legendary. ₹100-300 per person.

**Naivedyam, Hauz Khas**
Temple-style South Indian vegetarian food in a traditional setting. The banana leaf meals and filter coffee transport you to Chennai. ₹300-500 per person.

**Andhra Pradesh Bhawan Canteen, Feroze Shah Road**
Government-run canteen serving unlimited South Indian thali for ₹100-150. The queue is always long, but the value and authenticity are unmatched.

## Vegan Guide

### Naturally Vegan Indian Dishes
Many traditional Indian dishes are naturally vegan (dairy-free):
- **Chana masala** (chickpea curry) — when cooked without cream
- **Dal tadka** (yellow lentils with spice tempering)
- **Aloo gobi** (potato and cauliflower curry)
- **Baingan bharta** (smoky eggplant) — when made without cream
- **Rajma** (kidney bean curry) — often vegan
- **Roti/Chapati** (whole wheat flatbread) — usually vegan (unlike naan which contains yogurt)
- **Plain rice and biryani** (vegetable biryani without yogurt marinade)

### Vegan-Friendly Restaurants
**Vegan Farmer, Multiple Locations**
Dedicated vegan restaurant with Indian and international options. The vegan butter chicken (made with soy protein) is surprisingly convincing. ₹400-800 per person.

**Rose Cafe, Saket**
European-style cafe with clearly labeled vegan options on the menu. Good for brunch. ₹500-900 per person.

**Greenr Cafe, Aurobindo Marg**
Health-focused cafe with extensive vegan options including smoothie bowls, salads, and plant-based mains. ₹400-700 per person.

### Vegan Tips
- **Ask for "bina doodh, bina makhan, bina ghee"** — without milk, without butter, without clarified butter
- **Ghee (clarified butter)** is used extensively in Indian cooking — specify no ghee when ordering
- **Naan** typically contains yogurt — opt for roti or chapati instead
- **Street food** parathas are cooked in ghee — ask if they can use oil instead (some stalls will accommodate)
- **Sweets** almost always contain dairy — look for dry fruit and nut-based options

## Jain Food

Delhi also caters to **Jain dietary restrictions** (no onion, garlic, root vegetables). Many restaurants have Jain sections on their menus, and Jain thali restaurants are common in areas like Chandni Chowk and Karol Bagh.

## Tips for Vegetarian and Vegan Travelers

- **Look for green dots** — Indian food packaging uses a green dot for vegetarian and brown/red dot for non-vegetarian
- **Ask about cooking mediums** — even vegetarian dishes may be cooked in ghee or with cream
- **"Pure veg" restaurants** serve no meat, fish, or eggs — they are safe for vegetarians
- **Hotel restaurants** always have vegetarian options, but street food offers more variety
- **Diwali and Navratri seasons** see even more vegetarian options as many non-vegetarians go meat-free during festivals
- **Delhi's Jain community** ensures strict vegetarian (no onion/garlic) food is always available
