## Why India Visa Applications Get Rejected

India's e-Visa system is generally efficient and most applications are approved quickly. However, rejections do happen, and they can derail travel plans if you do not understand the common pitfalls. Here are the most frequent reasons for rejection and how to avoid each one.

## Top Rejection Reasons

### 1. Incorrect or Mismatched Passport Details
The most common reason for rejection. Even a single wrong digit in your passport number, a misspelled name, or an incorrect date of birth will trigger a rejection.

**How to avoid:**
- Triple-check every field against your physical passport
- Match the name exactly as it appears in the machine-readable zone (MRZ) of your passport
- If your passport has special characters or hyphenated names, enter them exactly as printed

### 2. Poor Quality Photo Upload
The photo requirements are strict. Common issues include wrong background color, face too large or too small, wearing glasses, shadows on face, or low resolution.

**Requirements:**
- White background (not cream or off-white)
- Front-facing, eyes open, mouth closed
- No glasses or head covering (unless for religious reasons)
- Minimum 350x350 pixels, JPEG format
- File size between 10 KB and 1 MB
- Recent photo (within last 6 months)

### 3. Invalid or Damaged Passport Scan
The passport bio page scan must be clear, complete, and in the correct format.

**Requirements:**
- PDF format (not JPEG)
- Complete bio page visible including MRZ lines at the bottom
- Minimum 300 DPI scan quality
- File size between 10 KB and 300 KB
- No glare, shadows, or cut-off edges

### 4. Passport Validity Issues
Your passport must be valid for at least **6 months from the date of arrival in India** and have at least **2 blank pages** for stamps.

**How to avoid:** Check your passport expiry date before applying. If it expires within 6 months of your planned arrival, renew first.

### 5. Incomplete Application Form
Leaving required fields blank or entering "N/A" inappropriately can cause rejection.

**Common fields people miss:**
- Previous Indian visa details (if applicable)
- Reference in India (enter your hotel name and address)
- Contact details in India (hotel phone number is acceptable)
- Purpose of visit details

### 6. Applying Too Late
The e-Visa requires a minimum of 4 days' advance application. Applications submitted less than 4 days before arrival are automatically rejected.

**How to avoid:** Apply at least 7-10 days before travel. Processing times can vary.

### 7. Previous Visa Violations
If you have previously overstayed an Indian visa or violated visa conditions, your new application may be rejected.

**How to avoid:** Always comply with visa conditions. If you have a prior violation, apply for a regular visa at the consulate where you can explain circumstances.

### 8. Nationality Restrictions
Citizens of some countries are not eligible for the e-Visa and must apply through an Indian embassy or consulate. Pakistani nationals, for example, must use the traditional visa process.

## What to Do If Rejected

### For e-Visa Rejections
- **Reapply immediately** after fixing the issue — there is no limit on reapplication
- **The rejection does not affect future applications** if it was a technical error
- **Check the rejection reason** — the email usually mentions the specific problem
- **Apply again carefully** — use the exact same details, but fix the specific issue

### For Consular Visa Rejections
- **Request clarification** from the consulate
- **Reapply with additional documentation** addressing the reason for rejection
- **Consider using a visa agency** that specializes in Indian visas

## Application Checklist

Use this checklist before submitting your e-Visa application:

- [ ] Passport valid for 6+ months from arrival date
- [ ] At least 2 blank pages in passport
- [ ] Photo meets all requirements (white background, correct size, JPEG)
- [ ] Passport scan is clear and complete (PDF format)
- [ ] All personal details match passport exactly
- [ ] Passport number entered correctly (verify twice)
- [ ] Reference in India provided (hotel name and address)
- [ ] Arrival date is at least 4 days from application date
- [ ] Previous Indian visa details entered if applicable
- [ ] Payment method ready (Visa/Mastercard)
- [ ] Applied at the official website (indianvisaonline.gov.in)

## Beware of Fake Visa Websites

Numerous fraudulent websites mimic the official Indian visa portal and charge inflated fees (sometimes 3-5 times the actual cost). Some are scams that take money without processing any visa.

**The only official portal is:** indianvisaonline.gov.in

**Red flags for fake sites:**
- URLs that do not end in .gov.in
- Fees significantly higher than official rates
- Guarantees of "instant approval"
- Requests for payment through unofficial channels
- Poor grammar or unprofessional design
