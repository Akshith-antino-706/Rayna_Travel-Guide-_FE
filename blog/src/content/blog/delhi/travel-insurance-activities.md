## Travel Insurance for Delhi

Travel insurance is not just recommended for Delhi — it is essential. India's healthcare quality is excellent at private hospitals but expensive without insurance, and common travel issues like traveler's diarrhea, flight delays (especially in foggy winter months), and lost luggage can all be covered by a good policy. Here is what you need to know.

## Why You Need Insurance for Delhi

### Medical Reasons
- **Traveler's diarrhea** is the most common health issue — most cases are mild but some require hospital visits
- **Air pollution** in winter can trigger respiratory issues, especially for those with asthma
- **Heat-related illness** in summer months can require emergency treatment
- **Private hospital costs** in Delhi range from ₹5,000-50,000+ per day without insurance
- **Medical evacuation** costs can exceed $50,000 if you need to be transferred to your home country

### Travel Disruption Reasons
- **Fog-related flight cancellations** are frequent in December-January
- **Lost luggage** is always a risk with connecting flights
- **Trip cancellation** due to unforeseen circumstances
- **Theft** of valuables, though rare, does occur

## What to Look for in a Policy

### Essential Coverage
| Coverage Type | Minimum Recommended |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Medical expenses | $100,000+ |
| Medical evacuation | $250,000+ |
| Trip cancellation | Full trip cost |
| Baggage loss/delay | $1,000-2,000 |
| Flight delay/cancellation | $500-1,000 |
| Personal liability | $100,000+ |

### Adventure Activity Coverage
If you plan any adventure activities in or around Delhi, verify your policy covers:
- **Standard activities** (usually covered): Cycling tours, walking tours, camel rides, cooking classes
- **Adventure activities** (may need upgrade): Hot air ballooning, zip-lining, quad biking, rock climbing
- **High-risk activities** (often excluded, need specific rider): White water rafting in Rishikesh, bungee jumping, paragliding
- **Check the fine print:** Some policies exclude activities above certain heights or on motorized vehicles

### India-Specific Considerations
- **Pre-existing conditions:** Ensure your policy covers flare-ups of existing conditions (asthma is relevant due to Delhi's air quality)
- **Food poisoning:** Should be covered under medical expenses
- **Terrorism coverage:** Some policies exclude this — check, as India occasionally has security alerts
- **Natural disasters:** Monsoon-related flooding should be covered for trip disruption

## Recommended Insurance Providers

### For Comprehensive Coverage
- **World Nomads:** Popular with backpackers and adventure travelers. Good coverage for adventure activities. Easy online purchase and claims.
- **Allianz Travel Insurance:** Strong medical coverage and 24/7 assistance hotline.
- **AXA Travel Insurance:** Comprehensive policies with good India coverage.

### For Budget Travelers
- **SafetyWing:** Affordable monthly subscription model, good for longer trips. Covers 65+ countries including India.
- **True Traveller:** Budget-friendly with adequate basic coverage.

### For Luxury Travelers
- **Check credit card coverage:** Many premium credit cards (Amex Platinum, Chase Sapphire) include travel insurance. Verify coverage limits and exclusions for India.
- **Chubb:** Premium policies with high coverage limits and concierge services.

## How to Make a Claim

### During Your Trip
1. **Seek treatment** at a reputable hospital (Max, Apollo, Fortis, or Gangaram in Delhi)
2. **Contact your insurance provider's emergency line** within 24 hours
3. **Keep all receipts** — hospital bills, pharmacy receipts, taxi receipts to hospital
4. **Get a written diagnosis** from the treating doctor
5. **File a police report** for theft-related claims (visit the nearest police station)

### After Your Trip
1. **Submit claim online** or by mail within the policy's time limit (usually 30-90 days)
2. **Include all documentation:** receipts, medical reports, police reports, proof of expenses
3. **Follow up** regularly — claims can take 2-8 weeks to process

## Tips for Buying Insurance

- **Buy before you book flights** to get trip cancellation coverage from day one
- **Read exclusions carefully** — the most common frustration with insurance is discovering something is not covered
- **Take photos of valuables** before your trip as proof of ownership
- **Carry your policy number and emergency contact** at all times (store in your phone and on paper)
- **Do not over-insure** — a mid-range policy with $100,000 medical coverage is sufficient for most Delhi trips
- **Compare at least 3 providers** using comparison sites like Squaremouth or InsureMyTrip
- **Consider annual policies** if you travel frequently — often better value than single-trip policies
