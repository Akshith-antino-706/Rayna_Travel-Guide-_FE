## Delhi in October

October marks the beautiful transition from monsoon to winter in Delhi. The rains have stopped, the city is lush green, and the intense summer heat is a distant memory. Temperatures are warm but manageable, and the festive season kicks into high gear with Navratri, Dussehra, and the build-up to Diwali. This is when Delhi truly comes alive.

## Weather

- **Average high:** 34°C (93°F) early month, dropping to 30°C (86°F) by late October
- **Average low:** 19°C (66°F)
- **Rainfall:** Light (10-20 mm, mostly early October)
- **Humidity:** Moderate, decreasing through the month
- **Sunshine hours:** 8-9 hours per day

### What to Expect
Early October can still feel warm and slightly humid as the monsoon recedes. By mid-October, the air becomes noticeably drier and cooler, especially in the evenings. Late October offers some of the year's most pleasant weather — warm days and comfortable nights.

**Air quality note:** Late October sees the beginning of crop burning in neighboring Punjab and Haryana, which can degrade air quality. Monitor AQI levels, especially in the last week of October.

## Navratri and Dussehra

October's highlight is the **Navratri-Dussehra** festival period, one of the most important in the Hindu calendar.

### Navratri (Nine Nights)
Nine nights of worship dedicated to Goddess Durga. Across Delhi, community celebrations include:
- **Garba and Dandiya Raas** — traditional dance events where communities dance in circles with sticks. Many events welcome tourists (₹500-2,000 entry at organized venues)
- **Ramleela performances** — dramatic enactments of the Ramayana epic in parks and grounds across the city. The Ramleela at Red Fort grounds is the most famous

### Dussehra (Vijayadashami)
The culmination of Navratri, Dussehra celebrates the victory of good over evil with the burning of giant effigies of the demon king Ravana, his brother Kumbhkaran, and son Meghnad.
- **Where to watch:** Ramleela Maidan near Red Fort hosts the biggest celebration, attended by the Prime Minister. Other major grounds across Delhi also host effigy burnings
- **Timing:** Evening, typically between 6:00-8:00 PM
- **Tip:** Arrive 2 hours early for good viewing spots at popular locations

## Things to Do in October

### Festival Experiences
- **Attend a Ramleela** — free public performances happen across Delhi neighborhoods from early October
- **Join a Garba night** — organized events at hotels and community centers (some tourist-friendly)
- **Visit Chandni Chowk** for festive shopping — the market is decorated and buzzing with Diwali preparation shopping
- **Shop for Diwali decorations** — colorful lanterns, diyas (earthen lamps), and rangoli materials available at markets

### Outdoor Activities
October's weather is perfect for:
- **Heritage walks** through Old Delhi and Lutyens' Delhi
- **Cycling tours** at dawn
- **Picnics at Lodhi Gardens** or Nehru Park
- **Photography** — the post-monsoon greenery combined with warm golden light creates beautiful conditions

### Cultural Visits
- **Crafts Museum** — excellent ethnographic museum in Pragati Maidan area
- **National Gallery of Modern Art** — worth a half-day visit
- **Dargah Nizamuddin Auliya** — Thursday evening qawwali is especially evocative

## Packing for October

- **Light cotton clothing** for warm days
- **One light layer** for cooler evenings (late October)
- **Sunscreen and sunglasses** — still strong daytime sun
- **Light rain jacket** or umbrella (early October only)
- **N95 mask** for potential air quality issues in late October
- **Comfortable walking shoes**

## Practical Tips

- **Hotel prices start rising** — October marks the beginning of peak season. Book early for better rates
- **Festival dates vary** — Navratri and Dussehra follow the lunar calendar. Check specific dates for your travel year
- **Markets are festive** — shopping areas are beautifully decorated and offer festival sales
- **Restaurants may be crowded** — especially during Navratri when many people eat out for dandiya dinners
- **Some attractions may have modified hours** on Dussehra day
- **Air quality:** Monitor daily, especially late October. The AQI app is essential

## October Events

- **Navratri** (9 days, dates vary) — dance celebrations, religious observances
- **Dussehra** (day after Navratri) — effigy burning across the city
- **Gandhi Jayanti** (October 2) — Gandhi's birthday, ceremonies at Raj Ghat, national holiday
- **Pre-Diwali shopping season** — markets are at their busiest and most colorful
