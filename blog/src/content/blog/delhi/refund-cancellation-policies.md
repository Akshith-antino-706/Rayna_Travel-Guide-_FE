## Understanding Cancellation Policies for Delhi Travel

Plans change, emergencies happen, and weather disrupts. Understanding the cancellation and refund landscape for Delhi bookings helps you protect your money and book with confidence. Here is a practical overview of what to expect across different booking categories.

## Hotel Cancellations

### Booking.com and Expedia
Most Delhi hotels listed on international platforms offer two rate types:
- **Free cancellation:** Cancel up to 24-48 hours before check-in for a full refund. Rates are typically 10-20% higher than non-refundable
- **Non-refundable:** Lower rate but no refund for cancellation

### MakeMyTrip and Indian Platforms
Indian booking platforms follow similar structures but often have stricter cancellation windows. Check the specific policy before booking — it varies by property.

### Direct Hotel Bookings
Booking directly with hotels often provides the most flexibility:
- Luxury hotels typically allow cancellation 48-72 hours before arrival
- Many will waive cancellation fees for rescheduling rather than canceling
- Loyalty program members often get more flexible terms

### Peak Season Warning
During peak season (October-March), especially around Diwali, Christmas, New Year, and Republic Day:
- Many hotels switch to stricter cancellation policies
- Some require non-refundable deposits
- Cancellation windows may extend to 7-14 days before arrival

## Flight Cancellations

### International Flights
Policies vary by airline:
- **Full-service airlines** (Emirates, Singapore Airlines, Lufthansa): Usually offer free cancellation within 24 hours of booking. After that, cancellation fees of $100-300 apply
- **Budget airlines:** Often non-refundable. Travel credit may be offered instead
- **Tip:** Book directly with the airline for easiest cancellation handling

### Domestic Indian Flights
- **IndiGo, SpiceJet, Air India:** Cancellation fees of ₹2,000-4,000 depending on timing
- **Cancellation more than 72 hours before:** Lowest fees
- **Within 72 hours:** Higher fees, sometimes 50%+ of ticket price
- **No-show:** Full forfeit on most domestic airlines

### Fog-Related Cancellations
During December-January fog season, airlines cancel and reschedule flights frequently:
- Airlines are obligated to offer alternative flights or full refunds for airline-initiated cancellations
- This applies to both domestic and international flights
- Keep your boarding pass and booking confirmation as proof
- Claim refunds through the airline's website or customer service

## Tour and Activity Cancellations

### GetYourGuide and Viator
Both platforms generally offer:
- **Free cancellation** up to 24 hours before the activity
- **Full refund** if the operator cancels (weather, minimum participants not met)
- **No refund** for no-shows or same-day cancellations

### Local Tour Operators
Policies vary widely:
- **Reputable operators** typically offer 24-48 hour free cancellation
- **Small operators** may have no formal cancellation policy — ask in advance
- **Private tours:** Often require 50% deposit with the balance due on the day
- **Get cancellation terms in writing** (WhatsApp or email) before paying

### Cooking Classes and Workshops
Most allow cancellation 24 hours before. Some home-based cooking classes may have stricter policies as ingredients are purchased in advance.

## Train Ticket Cancellations

If you have booked Indian Railways tickets for day trips or onward travel:

| Timing | Refund |
|--------|--------|
| More than 48 hours before | Full refund minus ₹240 (AC classes) |
| 12-48 hours before | 75% refund |
| 4-12 hours before | 50% refund |
| Less than 4 hours | No refund |

### Tatkal (Last-Minute) Tickets
No refund on cancellation, regardless of timing. These are used for emergency bookings.

## Tips for Protecting Your Bookings

- **Book flexible rates** when possible — the small premium is worth the peace of mind
- **Use credit cards** for bookings — many offer purchase protection and chargeback options
- **Travel insurance** covers trip cancellation for covered reasons (medical emergency, family emergency, airline bankruptcy)
- **Book directly** when possible — third-party platform disputes can be complex
- **Screenshot booking confirmations** and cancellation policies at the time of booking
- **Avoid advance full payment** to unknown local operators — pay deposits only, with balance on completion
- **Check weather forecasts** before committing to outdoor activities or flights during fog season
- **Rescheduling is often easier than canceling** — ask about date changes before requesting a refund

## Consumer Protection

India's consumer protection laws support tourists:
- The **Consumer Protection Act 2019** covers online and offline purchases
- **National Consumer Helpline:** 1800-11-4000 (toll-free)
- For airline complaints: **DGCA** (Directorate General of Civil Aviation) — complain online at aai.aero
- For severe disputes: Contact your embassy for guidance
