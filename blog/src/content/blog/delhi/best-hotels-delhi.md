## Finding the Perfect Hotel in Delhi

Delhi offers an extraordinary range of accommodation, from heritage properties that were once maharaja's palaces to ultra-modern business hotels and cozy backpacker hostels. The key to a great Delhi hotel experience lies in choosing the right neighborhood and the right property for your travel style and budget.

With over 5,000 hotels across the city, the choices can be overwhelming. This guide narrows it down to the best options in each category.

## Luxury Hotels (₹15,000-50,000+ per night)

### The Imperial, Janpath
Built in 1931, The Imperial is Delhi's most iconic heritage hotel. Located on Janpath, it is walking distance from Connaught Place and India Gate. The art deco architecture, museum-worthy art collection, and impeccable service make it a destination in itself. Rooms start around ₹18,000.

### The Oberoi, Dr. Zakir Husain Marg
Overlooking the Delhi Golf Club greens, The Oberoi offers minimalist luxury with floor-to-ceiling windows, butler service, and one of Delhi's finest spas. Rooms from ₹22,000.

### The Lodhi, Lodhi Road
Delhi's first all-suite luxury hotel, The Lodhi features suites with private plunge pools and is home to Indian Accent restaurant. Rooms from ₹25,000.

### ITC Maurya, Diplomatic Enclave
Home to the legendary Bukhara restaurant, ITC Maurya is the preferred choice for diplomats and heads of state. The hotel's location in Chanakyapuri is quiet yet central. Rooms from ₹16,000.

### Taj Palace, Sardar Patel Marg
A sprawling luxury property with multiple award-winning restaurants, extensive gardens, and grand event spaces. Rooms from ₹14,000.

## Mid-Range Hotels (₹5,000-15,000 per night)

### The Claridges, Aurangzeb Road
A heritage property with colonial charm, excellent restaurants, and a central location near Lodhi Gardens. Rooms from ₹8,000.

### Radisson Blu Plaza, Mahipalpur
Modern comforts near the airport with excellent connectivity. Good for business travelers and those with early flights. Rooms from ₹6,500.

### Holiday Inn New Delhi International Airport
A reliable international chain hotel in Aerocity with direct metro access to the city center. Rooms from ₹6,000.

### Bloomrooms @ New Delhi Railway Station
A stylish boutique hotel offering excellent value in a central location. Modern rooms with essential amenities. Rooms from ₹4,500.

### The Muse Sarovar Portico, Kapashera
A contemporary hotel with artistic interiors and good dining options. Well-connected to both the airport and South Delhi. Rooms from ₹5,500.

## Budget Hotels (₹1,500-5,000 per night)

### Zostel Delhi
India's popular hostel chain offers clean dorm beds from ₹600 and private rooms from ₹2,500. Great common areas for meeting fellow travelers.

### Hotel Amax Inn, Paharganj
A well-maintained budget option in Delhi's backpacker district. Rooms are basic but clean, with AC and Wi-Fi. Rooms from ₹1,800.

### FabHotel Prime The Residency, Connaught Place
Affordable rooms in a prime location. The hotel is simple but functional with 24-hour reception. Rooms from ₹2,500.

### Backpacker Panda, Connaught Place
A social hostel with excellent common areas, organized events, and clean dorms. Beds from ₹500, private rooms from ₹2,000.

### OYO Townhouse, Various Locations
Standardized budget rooms with guaranteed amenities across multiple Delhi locations. Rooms from ₹1,500.

## Booking Tips

- **Book 2-3 months ahead** during peak season (October-March) for the best rates
- **Check for breakfast inclusion** — many mid-range hotels include buffet breakfast worth ₹500-1,000
- **Use Indian booking sites** like MakeMyTrip or Goibibo for local properties, as they often have exclusive deals
- **Verify airport transfer options** — many hotels offer complimentary or discounted pickup
- **Read recent reviews** — Delhi hotels can change management or quality quickly
- **Consider location over stars** — a well-located 3-star hotel often beats a remote 5-star for sightseeing convenience

## What to Expect

Indian hotels generally provide complimentary bottled water, tea/coffee makers, and toiletries even in budget categories. Wi-Fi is standard but speeds vary — luxury hotels offer the most reliable connections. Most hotels have 12:00 PM checkout, and early check-in is usually available for an additional fee or complimentary for loyalty members.

Air conditioning is essential from March through October. Even in winter, heated rooms are important as Delhi nights can drop below 5°C in December and January.
