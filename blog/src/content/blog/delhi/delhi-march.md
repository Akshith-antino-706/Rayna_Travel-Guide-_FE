## Delhi in March

March is one of the finest months to visit Delhi. The winter cold has retreated, summer heat has not yet arrived, and the city bursts with spring flowers. The highlight is **Holi**, India's famous Festival of Colors, which typically falls in March and transforms Delhi into a kaleidoscope of joy, music, and colored powder. Air quality improves significantly compared to the winter months.

## Weather

- **Average high:** 30°C (86°F)
- **Average low:** 15°C (59°F)
- **Rainfall:** Minimal (10-15 mm)
- **Humidity:** Low
- **Sunshine hours:** 8-9 hours per day

### What to Expect
Early March feels like late spring — warm days, cool nights, and comfortable outdoor temperatures. By late March, afternoon temperatures can touch 33-35°C, hinting at the approaching summer. Mornings and evenings remain pleasant at 15-20°C. There is virtually no rain, and the fog season is completely over.

This is arguably the best weather window in Delhi's calendar — warm enough for outdoor activities all day, cool enough to walk comfortably.

## Holi: The Festival of Colors

### About Holi
Holi is one of India's most joyous festivals, celebrating the triumph of good over evil and the arrival of spring. It typically falls in mid-March (dates vary with the lunar calendar). On Holi day, people throw colored powders (gulal) and water at each other in a communal celebration of joy and unity.

### Experiencing Holi as a Tourist
- **Morning:** The main celebration happens from 9:00 AM to 1:00 PM. People gather in neighborhoods, parks, and organized events to play with colors
- **Afternoon:** The celebration winds down. People clean up and visit friends and family
- **Food:** Special Holi treats include **gujiya** (sweet dumplings), **thandai** (a spiced milk drink, sometimes with bhang — cannabis-infused paste), and **puran poli**

### Tips for Holi
- **Wear old white clothes** you do not mind ruining — the colors stain
- **Apply coconut oil** generously on exposed skin and hair before heading out — it makes color removal much easier
- **Carry waterproof protection** for your phone and wallet
- **Ask before playing** — while Holi is communal, always check if someone wants to participate
- **Book a Holi event** — several hotels and tour operators organize safe, tourist-friendly Holi celebrations (₹1,500-5,000 per person including colors, food, and DJ)
- **Avoid thandai from unknown sources** — some versions contain bhang, which can be disorienting
- **Note:** Most shops, restaurants, and attractions are closed on Holi day

## Things to Do in March

### Gardens in Full Bloom
March is peak blooming season in Delhi:
- **Mughal Gardens at Rashtrapati Bhawan** — open to the public annually, typically February-March. Hundreds of rose varieties, tulips, and seasonal flowers. Free entry with online registration
- **Lodhi Gardens** — bougainvillea, jacaranda, and gulmohar trees in bloom
- **Sunder Nursery** — the restored Mughal heritage garden is at its most beautiful
- **Rose Garden, Chanakyapuri** — over 1,500 rose varieties

### Outdoor Sightseeing
With comfortable temperatures and clear skies, March is perfect for:
- **Full-day monument tours** without midday heat exhaustion
- **Cycling tours** through Lutyens' Delhi
- **Evening strolls** at India Gate and Connaught Place
- **Boat rides** at Purana Qila lake

### Cultural Events
- **Holi events** at various hotels and venues
- **Art fairs** at India Art Fair (if scheduled in March)
- **Sufi Night at Nizamuddin Dargah** — Thursday evening qawwali performances are especially atmospheric in pleasant weather

## Packing for March

- **Light cotton clothing** — T-shirts, cotton pants, summer dresses
- **One light jacket** or cardigan for cooler evenings and air-conditioned spaces
- **Sunscreen (SPF 30+)** and sunglasses
- **Comfortable walking shoes** — sneakers or cushioned sandals
- **Scarf** for temple and mosque visits
- **Old clothes for Holi** if your trip coincides

## Practical Tips

- **Book hotels early** — March is shoulder season trending toward peak, with Holi boosting demand
- **Holi day closures** — most attractions, shops, and services are closed on Holi. Plan accordingly
- **Sunscreen is essential** — the March sun is deceptively strong
- **Stay hydrated** — as temperatures rise, carry water at all times
- **Air quality** is significantly better than winter months — typically "moderate" range
- **Flight disruptions** are rare — fog season is over, making March one of the most reliable months for air travel

## March Events

- **Holi** (varies with lunar calendar) — Festival of Colors
- **International Women's Day events** (March 8) — various celebrations across the city
- **Mughal Gardens open** — usually through mid-March
- **Food festivals** — various food and wine festivals at city hotels
