## Luxury Stays in India's Capital

Delhi's luxury hotel scene is among the finest in Asia. The city's top properties combine Indian hospitality traditions — where guests are treated as gods — with world-class facilities, Michelin-worthy dining, and spa experiences drawn from centuries of Ayurvedic wisdom. Whether you seek colonial-era grandeur, contemporary minimalism, or Mughal-inspired opulence, Delhi delivers.

## The Heritage Icons

### The Imperial, Janpath
**From ₹18,000 per night**

Built in 1931 by Blomfield, The Imperial is Delhi's most storied hotel. Walking through its palm-lined driveway feels like stepping into a bygone era. The hotel houses one of India's finest private art collections, including original works by Daniell, Fraser, and rare lithographs. The 1911 restaurant serves excellent colonial-era recipes, while Spice Route offers pan-Asian cuisine in a jaw-dropping interior modeled after the ancient spice trading route.

- **Rooms:** 235 rooms and suites with art deco furnishings
- **Dining:** 1911, Spice Route, San Gimignano, Daniell's Tavern
- **Spa:** Full-service with Ayurvedic treatments
- **Location:** Walking distance to Connaught Place and India Gate

### The Oberoi, Dr. Zakir Husain Marg
**From ₹22,000 per night**

The Oberoi epitomizes understated luxury. Every room overlooks the lush Delhi Golf Club greens, and the service is intensely personal — each floor has a dedicated butler. The Oberoi Spa is one of Delhi's best, and Threesixtyone restaurant holds its own among the city's top dining venues.

- **Rooms:** 220 rooms with floor-to-ceiling windows and marble bathrooms
- **Dining:** Threesixtyone, Omya (Indian), Baoshuan (Chinese)
- **Spa:** Oberoi Spa with hammam, vitality pool, and Ayurveda
- **Location:** Central Delhi near Humayun's Tomb and Lodhi Gardens

### Taj Palace, Sardar Patel Marg
**From ₹14,000 per night**

Set in six acres of manicured gardens, the Taj Palace feels like a resort within the city. The hotel's restaurants — Orient Express (European), Masala Art (Indian), and Blue Ginger (Vietnamese) — are all destination-worthy. The Presidential Suite has hosted countless heads of state.

- **Rooms:** 403 rooms with Mughal-inspired decor
- **Dining:** 5 restaurants and lounges
- **Spa:** Jiva Spa with traditional Indian wellness therapies
- **Location:** Diplomatic Enclave, Chanakyapuri

## The Modern Luxury Flagship Hotels

### The Lodhi, Lodhi Road
**From ₹25,000 per night**

Delhi's first all-suite luxury hotel, every room at The Lodhi features a private balcony and many have personal plunge pools. The hotel is home to **Indian Accent**, consistently ranked among Asia's best restaurants. The design is contemporary with generous use of space — the smallest suite is 1,000 square feet.

- **Suites:** 40 suites with personal pools and butler service
- **Dining:** Indian Accent, Elan (lounge)
- **Spa:** Full-service with a stunning pool area
- **Location:** On Lodhi Road, near Lodhi Gardens

### ITC Maurya, Diplomatic Enclave
**From ₹16,000 per night**

Home to **Bukhara**, India's most famous restaurant, ITC Maurya has been the hotel of choice for visiting US Presidents and global leaders. The Towers Wing offers an elevated experience with a private lounge, dedicated check-in, and exclusive dining.

- **Rooms:** 438 rooms including the ITC One luxury floor
- **Dining:** Bukhara, Dum Pukht Jolly Nabobs, Pavilion
- **Spa:** Kaya Kalp Royal Spa
- **Location:** Near the diplomatic enclave

### The Leela Palace, Chanakyapuri
**From ₹20,000 per night**

The newest of Delhi's grand luxury hotels, The Leela Palace draws on Lutyens' Delhi architecture with a modern sensibility. The lobby is dramatic with a soaring atrium, and rooms feature handpicked artworks and luxurious furnishings.

- **Rooms:** 254 rooms with Lutyens-inspired design
- **Dining:** Le Cirque (Italian), Jamavar (Indian), Megu (Japanese)
- **Spa:** ESPA with 12 treatment rooms
- **Location:** Diplomatic enclave near Rashtrapati Bhawan

## Boutique Luxury

### Roseate House, Aerocity
**From ₹10,000 per night**

A design-forward hotel near the airport with stunning interiors, an excellent spa, and Kheer restaurant serving progressive Indian cuisine. Ideal for travelers who appreciate contemporary design.

### Aman New Delhi, Lodhi Road
**From ₹45,000 per night**

The pinnacle of luxury in Delhi, Aman offers just 40 suites with private gardens, pools, and the serene minimalism the brand is known for. The spa, dining, and service are in a class of their own.

## What Luxury Gets You in Delhi

Delhi's luxury hotels offer extraordinary value compared to similar properties in London, New York, or Paris. For the price of a standard room at a top New York hotel, you can secure a suite with butler service in Delhi. Highlights include:

- **Personalized service:** Staff-to-guest ratios of 3:1 or higher at top properties
- **World-class dining:** Multiple award-winning restaurants on-site
- **Spa excellence:** Treatments drawing on Ayurvedic, Unani, and modern wellness traditions
- **Airport transfers:** Complimentary luxury car pickup and drop-off
- **Cultural experiences:** Private heritage walks, cooking classes, and guided tours arranged by the concierge

## Booking Tips for Luxury Hotels

- **Book direct** for the best perks — upgrades, late checkout, and welcome amenities
- **Join loyalty programs** — Taj InnerCircle, ITC One, and Oberoi One offer immediate benefits
- **Travel in summer (April-September)** for rates up to 40% lower than peak season
- **Ask about packages** — many properties bundle spa credits, dining, and experiences at better value than booking separately
- **Request a high floor** for views and quieter rooms
