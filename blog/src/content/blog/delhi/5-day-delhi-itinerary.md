## Five Days to Truly Know Delhi

Five days gives you the luxury of truly immersing yourself in Delhi — exploring beyond the guidebook highlights, discovering neighborhood characters, and developing your own rhythm in this extraordinary city. This itinerary includes the essential monuments, deep neighborhood explorations, a day trip, food adventures, and cultural experiences that most visitors miss.

## Day 1: Old Delhi — Mughal Grandeur

Dedicate your first full day to **Old Delhi (Shahjahanabad)**, the walled city built by Emperor Shah Jahan in 1639.

### Morning
Begin at **Jama Masjid** at 8:00 AM. After exploring the mosque, walk to **Chandni Chowk** for a guided street food breakfast. Stop at **Old Famous Jalebi Wala** for fresh jalebis (₹40-60), **Natraj Dahi Bhalle Wala** for the famous dahi bhalla (₹60-80), and **Paranthe Wali Gali** for stuffed parathas (₹50-100).

Explore the **Red Fort** (₹600 foreigners) — allow a full 2 hours to appreciate the Diwan-i-Aam, Diwan-i-Khas, Rang Mahal, and the Archaeological Museum inside.

### Afternoon
Lunch at **Karim's** for Mughal-era recipes passed down over generations (₹300-500 per person). Afterward, hire a cycle rickshaw for a tour of Chandni Chowk's specialty markets: **Khari Baoli** (spice market), **Dariba Kalan** (silver and jewelry), and **Kinari Bazaar** (wedding accessories).

### Evening
Visit **Raj Ghat** at sunset, then head to **Nizamuddin Dargah** for Thursday qawwali (or any evening for the spiritual atmosphere). Dinner at **Chor Bizarre** (₹800-1,500 per person).

## Day 2: New Delhi — Imperial Capital

### Morning
Start at **India Gate** and walk the length of **Kartavya Path** toward **Rashtrapati Bhawan**. Visit **Gurudwara Bangla Sahib** and participate in the langar (community meal). Tour the **National Museum** (₹650 foreigners, 1.5 hours).

### Afternoon
Lunch at **United Coffee House** in Connaught Place (₹600-1,000). Explore **Agrasen Ki Baoli**, a dramatic 14th-century stepwell hidden in the business district (free entry). Visit **Jantar Mantar**, the 18th-century astronomical observation site (₹25 Indians, ₹200 foreigners).

### Evening
Take the metro to the **Lotus Temple** for a late afternoon visit. Browse **Khan Market** for books and boutiques. Dinner at **Perch Wine & Coffee Bar** (₹800-1,500 per person).

## Day 3: South Delhi — Art, Culture, and Ruins

### Morning
Start at **Qutub Minar** (₹600 foreigners), then explore the adjacent **Mehrauli Archaeological Park** — a sprawling site with ruins from the Sultanate, Mughal, and British periods.

### Afternoon
Lunch at **Olive Bar & Kitchen** in Mehrauli for Mediterranean cuisine in a beautiful courtyard (₹1,200-2,000). Visit **Hauz Khas Complex** — the medieval madrasa ruins, the lake, and the deer park, followed by browsing the village's galleries and cafes.

### Evening
Sunset walk at **Lodhi Gardens**, admiring the tombs of Muhammad Shah and Sikandar Lodi. Dinner at **Indian Accent** at The Lodhi (₹4,000-6,000 per person — reserve well in advance) for one of India's finest culinary experiences.

## Day 4: Day Trip to Agra (Taj Mahal)

### Full Day
Depart at 6:00 AM via the **Gatimaan Express** from Hazrat Nizamuddin station (India's fastest train, 1 hour 40 minutes, ₹1,500 for Executive Class). Alternatively, hire a private car via the Yamuna Expressway (3-3.5 hours, ₹4,000-5,000 round trip).

In Agra, visit the **Taj Mahal** (₹1,100 foreigners, closed Fridays), **Agra Fort** (₹650 foreigners), and if time permits, **Mehtab Bagh** for sunset views of the Taj from across the river. Lunch at **Pinch of Spice** or **Joney's Place** in Agra.

Return by evening train or car. Dinner near your hotel.

**Tip:** Book Gatimaan Express tickets on IRCTC at least 2 weeks in advance as they sell out quickly.

## Day 5: Hidden Delhi and Farewell

### Morning
Visit **Akshardham Temple** — this massive Hindu temple complex features intricate carvings, exhibitions on Indian culture, and a musical fountain. Free entry, but allow 3 hours. No phones or cameras allowed inside, so leave electronics in the lockers.

### Afternoon
Lunch at **Sita Ram Diwan Chand** in Paharganj for Delhi's best chole bhature (₹60-100). Explore **Paharganj** — the backpacker district with its chaotic charm, budget shops, and eclectic cafes.

Visit **Purana Qila** (Old Fort), one of Delhi's oldest structures on a site inhabited since 3000 BCE (₹40 Indians, ₹300 foreigners). The boating lake adjacent is pleasant for a break.

### Evening
Final shopping at **Dilli Haat** in INA (₹30 entry) — a government-run open-air bazaar featuring handicrafts from all Indian states and authentic regional food stalls. This is the best place for souvenirs at reasonable fixed prices.

Farewell dinner at **Bukhara** at ITC Maurya (₹4,000-6,000) for the legendary Dal Bukhara and Sikandari Raan, or at **Gulati** in Pandara Road Market for excellent butter chicken in a more casual setting (₹600-1,000).

## Essential Tips

- **Pace yourself** — Delhi can be overwhelming. Build in rest time, especially midday in warmer months
- **Mix up your meals** — alternate between street food, casual restaurants, and fine dining
- **Carry a power bank and water** every day without exception
- **Book the Agra trip early** — train tickets and Taj Mahal tickets sell out in peak season
- **Keep Day 5 flexible** — you might want to revisit a favorite spot or explore a neighborhood recommended by locals

Five days in Delhi transforms you from a tourist into a temporary resident. You will leave understanding why this city has been the seat of empires for a millennium, and why millions continue to be drawn to its irresistible energy.
