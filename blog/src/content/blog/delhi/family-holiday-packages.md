## Family Holiday Packages for Delhi

Booking a pre-planned package for Delhi — especially as part of a larger India trip — can save time, reduce stress, and often save money compared to booking everything separately. For families in particular, having logistics handled by professionals means more time enjoying Delhi and less time navigating the chaos.

## Types of Packages Available

### Golden Triangle Packages (Delhi-Agra-Jaipur)
The most popular India package, covering Delhi, Agra (Taj Mahal), and Jaipur (Pink City) in 4-7 days. Ideal for first-time visitors to India.

**Budget Package (₹25,000-40,000 per person):**
- 3-star hotels with breakfast
- AC car with driver
- English-speaking guide at major sites
- Monument entry tickets
- Duration: 4-5 days

**Mid-Range Package (₹50,000-80,000 per person):**
- 4-star hotels with breakfast and some meals
- AC sedan with professional driver
- Private guide at all sites
- All entry tickets included
- Some cultural experiences (cooking class, village visit)
- Duration: 5-6 days

**Luxury Package (₹100,000-200,000+ per person):**
- 5-star heritage hotels (Oberoi, Taj, Aman properties)
- Luxury car with driver and escort
- Private expert guides
- All meals at top restaurants
- Exclusive experiences (private Taj Mahal sunrise, palace dinners)
- Duration: 5-7 days

### Delhi-Only Packages (2-4 days)
Focused exploration of Delhi suitable for travelers with limited time.

**Typical inclusions:**
- Airport pickup and drop-off
- Hotel accommodation
- Full-day guided sightseeing of Old and New Delhi
- Street food walking tour
- Monument entry tickets
- **Price range:** ₹15,000-80,000 per person depending on hotel category

### Extended India Packages
Packages covering Delhi as part of longer India itineraries:
- **Delhi + Varanasi + Khajuraho** (7-10 days) — for culture and spirituality
- **Delhi + Rajasthan circuit** (10-14 days) — Delhi, Jaipur, Jodhpur, Udaipur, Jaisalmer
- **Delhi + North India Heritage** (7-10 days) — Delhi, Amritsar, Dharamsala, Shimla

## What to Look for in a Package

### Must-Have Inclusions
- **Airport transfers** — arriving in Delhi without a planned transfer can be stressful for families
- **AC vehicle with driver** — essential for comfortable travel between cities
- **English-speaking guide** — makes monuments and cultural sites come alive for children
- **Hotel breakfast** — one less thing to worry about each morning
- **Flexibility for rest time** — packages that pack every hour leave no room for family pace

### Nice-to-Have Inclusions
- All monument entry tickets (saves queue time)
- Domestic flights between cities (saves hours on road)
- Cultural experiences (cooking classes, artisan visits)
- 24/7 on-call support for emergencies
- Travel insurance

### Red Flags
- **No reviews or testimonials** — insist on verified reviews
- **Vague itineraries** — "sightseeing in Delhi" without specifics
- **Hidden costs** — check if monument entries, guide fees, and meals are truly included
- **No emergency contact** — reputable operators provide 24/7 local support
- **Unusually cheap prices** — rock-bottom packages often cut corners on hotels and guides

## Booking Tips for Families

- **Book 3-6 months ahead** for peak season (October-March) to secure family rooms and preferred vehicles
- **Customize rather than buy off-the-shelf** — most operators will adjust itineraries for family pace
- **Request a larger vehicle** — an SUV or Innova (7-seater) gives families more space than a sedan
- **Ask about child discounts** — many operators offer 25-50% discounts for children under 12
- **Include buffer days** — do not pack every day with activities. Rest days keep children (and parents) happy
- **Verify cancellation policies** — family plans change. Ensure you have reasonable cancellation terms
- **Get everything in writing** — hotel names, vehicle type, guide language, included meals, and total price

## DIY vs. Package: What's Best for Families?

| Factor | Package | DIY |
|--------|---------|-----|
| Convenience | High | Low |
| Flexibility | Moderate | High |
| Cost | Often cheaper | Variable |
| Stress level | Low | Higher |
| Local knowledge | Expert guides included | Self-researched |
| Customization | By request | Complete control |
| Best for | First-time India visitors | Experienced India travelers |

For families visiting India for the first time, a package is almost always worth the investment. The logistical complexity of Indian travel — train bookings, driver negotiations, monument timings, local navigation — is significantly reduced with a good operator handling the details.
