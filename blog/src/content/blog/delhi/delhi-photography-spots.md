## Delhi Through a Lens

Delhi is one of the most photogenic cities in Asia. The contrast between Mughal-era grandeur and chaotic street life, between colonial boulevards and ancient bazaars, creates endless visual possibilities. Whether you shoot with a professional DSLR or a smartphone, Delhi rewards patient photographers with extraordinary images.

## Iconic Monument Shots

### Humayun's Tomb
The most photographed monument in Delhi for good reason. The symmetrical gardens, the central waterway leading to the tomb, and the warm sandstone against blue skies create stunning compositions.
- **Best time:** Sunrise (6:00-7:30 AM) for soft golden light and no crowds
- **Best angles:** From the entrance gateway looking down the water channel; from the side gardens for the full dome profile
- **Tip:** Visit on weekday mornings for clean shots without people

### Qutub Minar
The soaring minaret's intricate carvings and dramatic height offer unique perspectives.
- **Best time:** Late afternoon (3:00-5:00 PM) when the sandstone glows warm
- **Best angles:** Low angle from the base looking up; from across the courtyard with the Iron Pillar in foreground
- **Tip:** The Alai Darwaza gateway makes a beautiful frame for the minaret

### Red Fort
The massive red sandstone walls are most photogenic from the exterior and the approach.
- **Best time:** Early morning for the front facade with warm light
- **Best angles:** From across the road for the full wall panorama; inside the Diwan-i-Khas for the marble interiors

### India Gate
The war memorial is beautiful at any time but especially stunning at night when illuminated.
- **Best time:** Blue hour (just after sunset) for the illuminated monument against dark blue sky
- **Best angles:** From the central Kartavya Path for a symmetrical shot; from the gardens for a softer perspective

## Street Photography

### Chandni Chowk
Old Delhi's main market is a street photographer's dream — colors, chaos, characters, and light pouring through narrow lanes.
- **Best time:** Morning (8:00-10:00 AM) for the best light in narrow lanes
- **Subjects:** Spice market workers, cycle rickshaws, food vendors, fabric shops
- **Tip:** A 35mm or 50mm equivalent lens captures the tight spaces well. Ask permission before photographing people up close.

### Jama Masjid Area
The streets surrounding the mosque offer incredible scenes of daily life against a historic backdrop.
- **Best time:** After Friday prayers for the most activity
- **Best angles:** From the minaret looking down over Old Delhi

### Nizamuddin Basti
The medieval village surrounding the Dargah offers atmospheric images of narrow lanes, old buildings, and spiritual life.
- **Best time:** Thursday evening during qawwali for atmospheric shots

## Hidden and Emerging Spots

### Agrasen Ki Baoli
This stepwell's symmetrical architecture and dramatic descent into shadow create haunting, geometric images.
- **Best time:** Midday when harsh light creates dramatic shadows on the steps
- **Tip:** Wide-angle lenses work best here

### Lodhi Colony Street Art
The Lodhi Art District features large-scale murals by international artists on residential buildings. Walk through the colony for colorful, contemporary images.
- **Best time:** Any time, but morning light is softest
- **Tip:** Combine with a visit to nearby Lodhi Gardens

### Sunder Nursery
Beautifully restored Mughal gardens with flowering trees, ancient tombs, and reflective pools.
- **Best time:** February-March when flowers are in bloom
- **Best spot:** The lotus pond with the tomb in background

### Hauz Khas Complex
The medieval ruins overlooking the reservoir with the modern Hauz Khas Village in the background create an evocative contrast.
- **Best time:** Late afternoon for golden light on the ruins

## Camera Tips for Delhi

### Gear Recommendations
- **Wide-angle lens (16-35mm):** Essential for interiors of monuments and tight Old Delhi lanes
- **Standard zoom (24-70mm):** The most versatile for general sightseeing photography
- **Telephoto (70-200mm):** Useful for street portraits and architectural details
- **Smartphone:** Modern smartphones handle Delhi's contrasty light well. Night modes work at illuminated monuments.

### Practical Tips
- **Dust protection:** Delhi is dusty, especially in summer. Keep lens caps on when not shooting. Carry a lens cloth.
- **Heat protection:** In summer, do not leave cameras in direct sunlight or hot cars
- **Monsoon protection:** Carry waterproof bags or rain covers for sudden downpours
- **Tripod rules:** Some monuments charge extra for tripods (₹25-100) or prohibit them. Ask at the ticket counter.
- **Drone photography:** Strictly prohibited at all ASI-protected monuments and most of Delhi's controlled airspace. Do not fly drones without explicit permission.
- **People photography:** Always ask before taking close-up portraits. Most Delhiites enjoy being photographed and will often pose enthusiastically.

### Photography Ethics
- Do not photograph military installations, airports, or bridges
- Ask permission before photographing inside places of worship
- Respect the dignity of people — do not photograph beggars, homeless individuals, or people in distress for "poverty tourism"
- Some temples prohibit photography in inner sanctums
- The Red Fort prohibits commercial photography without permission
