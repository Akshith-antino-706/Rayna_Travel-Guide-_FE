## Delhi's Green Oases

Despite its reputation as a bustling, sometimes overwhelming megacity, Delhi is one of the greenest capitals in the world. The city is home to hundreds of parks and gardens — from 15th-century tomb gardens to beautifully landscaped modern parks. These green spaces offer respite from the noise, excellent birdwatching, morning joggers' paths, and some of the city's finest photography opportunities.

## Historic Gardens

### Lodhi Gardens
Delhi's most beloved park spans 90 acres along Lodhi Road, dotted with tombs of 15th-century Sayyid and Lodi dynasty rulers. The Mohammed Shah's Tomb, Sikandar Lodi's Tomb, Sheesh Gumbad, and Bara Gumbad are all within the gardens. Morning walkers, yoga practitioners, and couples share the lawns with history buffs and photographers.
- **Entry:** Free
- **Hours:** 6:00 AM to 7:30 PM (winter), 6:00 AM to 8:00 PM (summer)
- **Location:** Lodhi Road, near Khan Market
- **Best time:** Early morning for photography, or late afternoon for golden light on the tombs
- **Birdwatching:** Parakeets, mynahs, hoopoes, and kingfishers are commonly spotted

### Humayun's Tomb Gardens
The formal Mughal char-bagh (four-part garden) surrounding Humayun's Tomb is one of the finest examples of Persian garden design in India. The symmetrical water channels, pathways, and manicured lawns create a serene setting that makes the tomb even more striking.
- **Entry:** Included with Humayun's Tomb ticket (₹40 Indians, ₹600 foreigners)
- **Hours:** Sunrise to sunset
- **Note:** The Sunder Nursery adjacent to the tomb complex has been beautifully restored as a heritage park with its own gardens and historical structures

### Sunder Nursery
A restored 16th-century Mughal-era garden complex adjacent to Humayun's Tomb. The 90-acre site features six restored World Heritage monuments, a lotus pond, herb gardens, an amphitheater, and over 300 tree species. It is one of Delhi's most beautiful and underrated spaces.
- **Entry:** ₹35 (Indians), ₹100 (foreigners)
- **Hours:** 7:00 AM to 6:00 PM (winter), 7:00 AM to 7:00 PM (summer)
- **Tip:** Visit during spring (February-March) when the flowering trees are in full bloom

### Mughal Gardens (Rashtrapati Bhawan Gardens)
The gardens of the President's House are open to the public for a limited period each year, typically February to March. Designed by Edwin Lutyens with Mughal-inspired features, the gardens showcase hundreds of rose varieties, tulips, and seasonal flowers in a spectacular display.
- **Entry:** Free (online booking required)
- **Hours:** Limited seasonal opening, typically 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM
- **Tip:** Book well in advance online as slots fill up quickly

## Modern Parks

### Garden of Five Senses, Said-ul-Ajaib
A 20-acre park designed to stimulate all five senses, featuring themed sections with bamboo courts, herbal gardens, spiral walkways, and art installations. The park hosts seasonal food and cultural festivals.
- **Entry:** ₹35
- **Hours:** 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM (winter), 9:00 AM to 7:00 PM (summer)
- **Location:** Near Saket, close to Qutub Minar
- **Best for:** Couples, families, photography

### Nehru Park, Chanakyapuri
A large, well-maintained park popular for morning walks, yoga, and evening strolls. The park hosts open-air concerts and cultural programs, especially during festival seasons. The Japanese-style rock garden section is particularly peaceful.
- **Entry:** Free
- **Hours:** 5:00 AM to 9:00 PM
- **Location:** Chanakyapuri, near the diplomatic enclave

### Deer Park, Hauz Khas
A compact urban park where spotted deer, peacocks, and rabbits roam in semi-wild enclosures. The park surrounds the medieval Hauz Khas reservoir and connects to the Hauz Khas ruins and Rose Garden. Popular with joggers and families.
- **Entry:** Free
- **Hours:** 5:30 AM to 8:00 PM (summer), 5:30 AM to 7:00 PM (winter)
- **Location:** Hauz Khas, South Delhi

### Buddha Jayanti Park
Set in the heart of the Ridge forest area, this park features a Japanese-style garden, meditation areas, and a beautiful lake with a golden Buddha statue. It is one of Delhi's most peaceful spots, rarely visited by tourists.
- **Entry:** Free
- **Hours:** 5:00 AM to 8:00 PM
- **Location:** Near Vandemataram Marg, Ridge Road

## Botanical and Specialized Gardens

### National Rose Garden, Chanakyapuri
Home to over 1,500 rose varieties across 23 acres, this garden is at its most spectacular during the annual Rose Festival in February. Even outside the festival, the garden is a fragrant, colorful retreat.
- **Entry:** Free
- **Hours:** 5:00 AM to 8:00 PM

### Yamuna Biodiversity Park
A 457-acre ecological restoration project on the Yamuna floodplain featuring wetlands, grasslands, and forests. Over 1,300 plant species and 180 bird species have been recorded here. Guided nature walks are available on weekends.
- **Entry:** Free (registration required for guided walks)
- **Location:** Near Wazirabad

### Waste to Wonder Park, Sarai Kale Khan
A unique theme park where replicas of the Seven Wonders of the World are built entirely from industrial and scrap waste. The park is illuminated at night with LED lights and makes for a fun photo stop.
- **Entry:** ₹50 (adults), ₹25 (children)
- **Hours:** 11:00 AM to 11:00 PM (closed Mondays)

## Best Time to Visit Delhi's Parks

- **October-November:** Pleasant weather, migrant birds arriving
- **February-March:** Flowering season — roses, bougainvillea, and jacaranda in full bloom
- **Avoid May-June:** Extreme heat makes outdoor visits uncomfortable
- **Monsoon (July-September):** Lush greenery but muddy paths and mosquitoes

## Tips for Park Visits

- **Carry water and sunscreen** — most parks have limited shade in open areas
- **Visit early morning (6:00-8:00 AM)** for birdwatching, photography, and pleasant temperatures
- **Weekdays are quieter** — weekends can be crowded, especially at Lodhi Gardens and India Gate
- **Pack a picnic** — most parks allow food. Nehru Park and Lodhi Gardens are excellent picnic spots
- **Photography is generally allowed** in public parks; check rules at heritage sites within gardens
