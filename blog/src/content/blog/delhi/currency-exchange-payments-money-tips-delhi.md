## Managing Money in Delhi

India uses the **Indian Rupee (INR/₹)**, and understanding how to exchange, withdraw, and spend money in Delhi will save you both time and unnecessary fees. The good news is that India has rapidly modernized its payment infrastructure, and Delhi is at the forefront — digital payments are accepted almost everywhere, ATMs are abundant, and currency exchange options are plentiful.

## Currency Basics

- **Currency:** Indian Rupee (₹/INR)
- **Denominations:** Notes: ₹10, ₹20, ₹50, ₹100, ₹200, ₹500, ₹2,000. Coins: ₹1, ₹2, ₹5, ₹10
- **Exchange rate:** Approximately ₹83-85 = 1 USD (check current rates before traveling)
- **Tip:** The ₹2,000 note was withdrawn from circulation in 2023 — it is still legal tender but increasingly rare. ₹500 notes are the largest commonly used denomination.

## Where to Exchange Currency

### At the Airport
Thomas Cook and other authorized money changers operate 24/7 in the arrivals area of Terminal 3. Rates are slightly below market but convenient for getting your first rupees.
- **Pros:** Convenient, open 24/7, safe
- **Cons:** Rates are 2-3% worse than city exchange shops
- **Tip:** Exchange only what you need for transport and first day expenses (₹2,000-3,000)

### Authorized Money Changers in the City
Delhi has numerous RBI-authorized money changers, particularly around Connaught Place, Paharganj, and Karol Bagh. They typically offer the best rates.
- **Recommended:** Baluja Forex (Connaught Place), Thomas Cook, UAE Exchange
- **Tip:** Always check the rate before handing over your currency. Count the money carefully before leaving.
- **Avoid:** Unauthorized changers in markets who offer suspiciously good rates

### Hotel Exchange
Most 4-star and 5-star hotels offer currency exchange at the front desk. Rates are convenient but typically 3-5% worse than authorized money changers.

### Banks
You can exchange currency at major banks (SBI, HDFC, ICICI) but the process is slower and requires passport copies. Generally not worth the hassle unless you need to exchange large amounts.

## ATMs

ATMs are ubiquitous in Delhi — you will find them at metro stations, malls, markets, and on virtually every commercial street.

### Best ATMs for Foreign Cards
- **HDFC Bank:** Widely available, accepts most international cards, per-transaction limit of ₹10,000-25,000
- **ICICI Bank:** Good international card compatibility
- **SBI:** Government bank with extensive network
- **Citibank:** Best for Citibank cardholders with no fees

### ATM Tips
- **Withdrawal limits:** Most ATMs allow ₹10,000-20,000 per transaction. Some allow ₹25,000
- **Fees:** Expect ₹200-250 per transaction from the Indian bank, plus your home bank's international fee
- **Use ATMs inside banks** for security — avoid standalone ATMs in isolated areas
- **Notify your bank** before traveling to prevent card blocks
- **Carry a backup card** — ATM networks occasionally experience outages

## Card Payments

### Credit and Debit Cards
Visa and Mastercard are widely accepted at hotels, restaurants, malls, and larger shops. American Express acceptance is limited. Rupay (India's domestic card) is ubiquitous but irrelevant for most tourists.

### Where Cards Work Well
- Hotels and resorts
- Restaurants (mid-range and above)
- Shopping malls and branded stores
- Tour operators and travel agencies
- Online bookings (hotels, trains, attractions)

### Where You Will Need Cash
- Street food stalls
- Auto-rickshaws
- Small shops in Old Delhi and local markets
- Temple and mosque donations
- Some monument ticket counters
- Smaller restaurants and dhabas

## Digital Payments (UPI)

India's Unified Payments Interface (UPI) has revolutionized payments. Apps like **Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm** are used everywhere — from luxury restaurants to street chai stalls. However, these require an Indian bank account to fully function.

**Tourist workaround:** Some international travelers have success linking international cards to Google Pay or using Paytm's wallet feature, but this is not reliable. Carry cash as your primary backup.

## Tipping Guide

Tipping is appreciated in Delhi but not as rigidly expected as in the US.

| Service | Suggested Tip |
|---------|--------------|
| Restaurant | 10-15% (check if service charge included) |
| Hotel porter | ₹50-100 per bag |
| Hotel housekeeping | ₹100-200 per day |
| Taxi/auto-rickshaw | Round up or ₹20-50 |
| Tour guide | ₹300-500 per half day, ₹500-1,000 per full day |
| Spa therapist | ₹200-500 |
| Delivery person | ₹20-50 |

## Money Safety Tips

- **Use a money belt or hidden pouch** for carrying larger amounts of cash
- **Do not flash large wads of cash** in public, especially at markets
- **Keep small denominations** (₹10, ₹20, ₹50, ₹100) for daily transactions — vendors may claim they cannot change large notes
- **Refuse torn or very worn notes** — some shops and auto drivers will not accept them
- **Keep receipts** from authorized money changers in case customs asks on departure
- **Save emergency cash** separately from your daily spending money
- **Photograph your cards** (front and back) and store securely in the cloud in case of loss

## How Much Cash to Carry

For a typical day of sightseeing in Delhi, ₹2,000-3,000 in cash is sufficient if you also have a card. Keep ₹5,000-10,000 as a reserve in your hotel safe. Exchange or withdraw as needed rather than carrying large amounts.
