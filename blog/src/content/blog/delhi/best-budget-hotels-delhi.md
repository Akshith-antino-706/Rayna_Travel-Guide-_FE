## Budget-Friendly Stays in Delhi

Delhi is one of the most affordable capital cities in the world for accommodation. Whether you are a backpacker looking for a social hostel or a couple seeking a clean private room without breaking the bank, Delhi has excellent options under ₹3,000 per night ($35 USD). The trick is knowing where to look and what to expect.

## Best Budget Hostels (₹500-1,500 per night)

### Zostel Delhi, Paharganj
India's largest hostel chain delivers consistent quality. The Delhi outpost features colorful dorms with personal lockers, reading lights, and power outlets at every bed. The rooftop common area is perfect for meeting fellow travelers.
- **Dorm beds:** ₹600-900
- **Private rooms:** ₹2,500-3,000
- **Location:** Near New Delhi Railway Station
- **Metro:** New Delhi station (5-minute walk)

### Backpacker Panda, Connaught Place
Located right in the heart of Delhi, this hostel offers a social atmosphere with organized pub crawls, walking tours, and movie nights. The common kitchen saves on food costs.
- **Dorm beds:** ₹500-800
- **Private rooms:** ₹2,000-2,800
- **Location:** Connaught Place inner circle
- **Metro:** Rajiv Chowk (3-minute walk)

### Moustache Hostel, Paharganj
A well-designed hostel with female-only dorms, mixed dorms, and private rooms. The cafe serves affordable meals, and the staff organizes Old Delhi walking tours.
- **Dorm beds:** ₹550-750
- **Private rooms:** ₹1,800-2,500
- **Location:** Main Bazaar, Paharganj
- **Metro:** New Delhi station (8-minute walk)

### goSTOPS Delhi
A modern hostel chain popular with young Indian and international travelers. Clean design, good Wi-Fi, and a lively common area make it stand out.
- **Dorm beds:** ₹500-700
- **Private rooms:** ₹2,000-2,800
- **Metro:** Varies by location

## Best Budget Hotels (₹1,500-3,000 per night)

### Hotel Amax Inn, Paharganj
A reliable mid-budget option that has maintained quality for years. Rooms are small but clean, with AC, flat-screen TV, and en-suite bathrooms. The rooftop restaurant serves decent food.
- **Rooms:** ₹1,800-2,800
- **Location:** Main Bazaar, Paharganj
- **Highlights:** 24-hour room service, travel desk, laundry service

### FabHotel Prime The Residency, Connaught Place
Part of the FabHotels chain that standardizes budget accommodation in India. Expect clean rooms, reliable Wi-Fi, and a central location. No restaurant, but surrounded by food options.
- **Rooms:** ₹2,500-3,500
- **Location:** Connaught Place area
- **Highlights:** Consistent quality, online booking guarantee

### Bloomrooms @ New Delhi Railway Station
A trendy micro-hotel concept offering compact but stylishly designed rooms with everything you need — power shower, comfortable bed, fast Wi-Fi, and a grab-and-go cafe.
- **Rooms:** ₹2,800-4,000
- **Location:** Opposite New Delhi Railway Station
- **Highlights:** Modern design, excellent location, airport express metro access

### Hotel Palace Heights, Connaught Place
A boutique budget hotel right on the inner circle of Connaught Place. Rooms are well-maintained with city views from higher floors.
- **Rooms:** ₹2,500-3,500
- **Location:** Connaught Place inner circle
- **Highlights:** Rooftop restaurant, central location

### Hotel Ajanta, Arakashan Road
A long-running budget hotel popular with international travelers. Simple but clean rooms with good service and a helpful travel desk.
- **Rooms:** ₹1,500-2,500
- **Location:** Near New Delhi Railway Station
- **Highlights:** Good value, experienced staff, currency exchange

## Best Budget Areas in Delhi

### Paharganj (Main Bazaar)
The traditional backpacker district opposite New Delhi Railway Station. Dozens of budget hotels, street food stalls, and travel agencies line the narrow lanes. Noisy and chaotic, but unbeatable for convenience and price.

### Karol Bagh
A commercial district with many mid-range and budget hotels. Great street shopping and food. Well-connected by metro (Blue Line). Hotels here tend to be quieter than Paharganj.

### Arakashan Road
Just north of New Delhi Railway Station, this street has a concentration of budget hotels in a relatively calm setting. Walking distance to the metro and major transport hubs.

## Budget Travel Tips for Delhi

- **Book on Indian platforms** — MakeMyTrip, Goibibo, and OYO often have flash sales with rooms under ₹1,000
- **Negotiate for multi-night stays** — many budget hotels offer 15-25% discounts for 3+ nights if you ask at reception
- **Check-in person** — walk-in rates at budget hotels are sometimes lower than online rates
- **Avoid touts** at railway stations and airports who will steer you to commission-paying hotels
- **Verify hot water** — in winter (December-February), confirm that your room has a working geyser (water heater)
- **Check reviews from the last 3 months** — budget hotel quality can change rapidly in Delhi
- **Carry a padlock** — useful for hostel lockers and budget hotel room security

## What ₹2,000 Per Night Gets You

At this price point, expect a clean private room with AC, attached bathroom with hot water, a flat-screen TV, free Wi-Fi (variable speed), and basic toiletries. Breakfast may or may not be included. Location will typically be Paharganj, Karol Bagh, or Arakashan Road — all well-connected areas with plenty of food options within walking distance.
