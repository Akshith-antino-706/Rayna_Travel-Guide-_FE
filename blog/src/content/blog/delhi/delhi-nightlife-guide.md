## Delhi After Dark

Delhi's nightlife has evolved dramatically in recent years, from a city that essentially shut down at 10:00 PM to one with a thriving bar and club scene. While it still does not rival Mumbai or Bangkok, Delhi offers sophisticated cocktail bars, pulsating nightclubs, atmospheric live music venues, and a late-night food culture that is among India's best.

## Cocktail Bars and Lounges

### PCO, Khan Market
A speakeasy-style bar hidden behind an unassuming phone booth door. The cocktails are creative, the ambiance is intimate, and the crowd is Delhi's creative set. No reservations — arrive before 9:00 PM to get a spot.
- **Drinks:** ₹600-1,200 per cocktail
- **Vibe:** Intimate, dimly lit, conversational
- **Hours:** 5:00 PM to 1:00 AM
- **Dress code:** Smart casual

### Sidecar, The Oberoi
One of Delhi's most sophisticated bars with an Art Deco-inspired interior and an award-winning cocktail menu. The bartenders here compete at international levels.
- **Drinks:** ₹800-1,500 per cocktail
- **Vibe:** Elegant, upscale
- **Hours:** 6:00 PM to midnight

### Cocktails and Dreams, Gurugram
A speakeasy-style bar with innovative cocktails and a lively atmosphere. Consistently rated among Delhi-NCR's best.
- **Drinks:** ₹500-1,000 per cocktail
- **Vibe:** Modern, trendy

### Ek Bar, Defence Colony
Contemporary Indian-inspired cocktails using local ingredients — think kokum martinis and chai-infused whiskey sours. The interiors are whimsical and Instagram-worthy.
- **Drinks:** ₹500-900 per cocktail
- **Vibe:** Quirky, creative
- **Hours:** 12:00 PM to 12:30 AM

## Nightclubs

### Kitty Su, The Lalit
Delhi's most famous nightclub, known for its progressive music policy, international DJ nights, and inclusive atmosphere. The sound system and production quality rival European clubs.
- **Entry:** ₹1,500-3,000 (stag), couples often discounted. Entry includes some drink credits on select nights
- **Music:** House, techno, electronic
- **Hours:** 10:00 PM to 4:00 AM (weekends)
- **Dress code:** Strict — no casual wear, no flip-flops

### Privee, Shangri-La Hotel
A luxury nightclub with international DJs and a high-energy dance floor. Popular with Delhi's affluent crowd.
- **Entry:** ₹2,000-5,000 depending on night
- **Music:** Commercial, Bollywood, EDM

### Toy Room, Aerocity
A modern nightclub with multiple zones, from the dance floor to the terrace lounge. Popular for weekend parties.
- **Entry:** ₹1,000-2,500
- **Music:** Commercial, hip-hop, Bollywood mix

## Live Music Venues

### The Piano Man Jazz Club, Safdarjung Enclave
Delhi's premier jazz venue hosts live music six nights a week. The intimate setting, quality sound, and strong cocktail menu make it a favorite among music lovers.
- **Entry:** ₹500-1,500 depending on the artist
- **Music:** Jazz, blues, soul, and occasional indie acts
- **Hours:** 7:00 PM to midnight

### BluFrog (if operational) / Summer House Cafe, Hauz Khas
Live music venue and lounge that hosts local bands, DJs, and cultural events. The Hauz Khas location adds to the atmospheric appeal.
- **Entry:** ₹300-800 depending on events
- **Music:** Indie, rock, electronic

## Rooftop Bars

### Junkyard Cafe, Connaught Place
A quirky rooftop space with recycled decor, good cocktails, and a young, energetic crowd. Affordable by Delhi bar standards.
- **Drinks:** ₹350-700
- **Food:** Decent bar food
- **Hours:** 12:00 PM to 1:00 AM

### Unplugged Courtyard, Connaught Place
Multi-level restaurant and bar with a rooftop terrace offering city views. Regular live music and DJ nights.
- **Drinks:** ₹400-800
- **Hours:** 12:00 PM to 1:00 AM

## Late-Night Food

Delhi's late-night food scene is legendary. After the bars close, these spots keep the city fed:

- **Pandara Road Market:** Multiple restaurants open until midnight — Gulati, Pindi, and Havemore serve excellent North Indian food
- **Moolchand Parantha:** Late-night paratha stall near Lajpat Nagar metro, open until 2:00 AM
- **Khan Chacha, Khan Market:** Rolls and kebabs available late into the evening
- **Rajinder Da Dhaba, Safdarjung:** The legendary late-night dhaba for tandoori chicken and keema. Often packed at midnight

## Practical Nightlife Tips

- **Carry photo ID** — all bars and clubs check age (21+ for alcohol in Delhi). Passport or a copy works
- **Dress code matters** — most clubs enforce smart casual minimum. No flip-flops, shorts, or sleeveless shirts for men
- **Ladies' nights** — many venues offer free entry and discounted drinks for women on select nights (usually Wednesday or Thursday)
- **Last call** — most bars and restaurants close by 12:30-1:00 AM. Clubs stay open until 2:00-4:00 AM on weekends
- **Use Uber or Ola** to get home — do not drink and take auto-rickshaws late at night
- **Alcohol laws:** Legal drinking age is 25 in Delhi (enforcement is lax at 21+). No public drinking. Dry days prohibit alcohol sales entirely.
- **Budget:** Expect to spend ₹2,000-5,000 per person for a night out including drinks, food, and transport
