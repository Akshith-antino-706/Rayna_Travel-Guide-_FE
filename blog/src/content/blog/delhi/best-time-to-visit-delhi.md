## When Should You Visit Delhi?

The best time to visit Delhi depends on what matters most to you — perfect weather, festival experiences, budget savings, or manageable crowds. Delhi experiences extreme seasonal variation, from near-freezing winter mornings to scorching 48°C summer afternoons, with a monsoon season in between. Here is everything you need to know to pick the right time for your trip.

## Season Overview

### Peak Season: October to March
This is the prime tourist window. Temperatures range from 8-25°C, the air is generally clear (except late November-January smog), and outdoor sightseeing is comfortable. Major festivals including Diwali (October/November), Christmas, and Republic Day (January 26) fall in this period.

**Pros:** Best weather, major festivals, full hotel and restaurant operations
**Cons:** Highest prices, most crowded attractions, potential smog in November-January
**Hotel prices:** Peak rates, 30-50% higher than off-season

### Summer: April to June
Extreme heat dominates, with April at 35-40°C and May-June reaching 40-48°C. Dust storms (loo winds) can occur. This is decidedly off-season for tourism.

**Pros:** Lowest prices (30-50% discounts on hotels), no crowds, shorter queues
**Cons:** Dangerously hot, outdoor sightseeing limited to early mornings, dust storms
**Hotel prices:** Lowest of the year

### Monsoon: July to September
Heavy rainfall transforms the city. July and August see the most rain, with occasional flooding. September brings lighter rain and the beginning of pleasant weather.

**Pros:** Lush green landscapes, dramatic skies for photography, low prices, reduced crowds
**Cons:** Flooding, humidity, mosquitoes, some outdoor activities disrupted
**Hotel prices:** Low to moderate

## Our Top Recommendations

### Best Overall: February-March
The sweet spot. Winter chill has passed, summer heat has not arrived, and the Mughal Gardens at Rashtrapati Bhawan are in full bloom (open to public in February-March). Comfortable temperatures around 20-30°C, clear skies, and moderate crowds.

### Best for Festivals: October-November
Experience Diwali (the Festival of Lights) when Delhi is illuminated with millions of lights, firecrackers, and celebrations. Markets are buzzing, families are in festive mood, and the cultural energy is at its peak. Also, Dussehra (giant effigy burning) in October is spectacular.

### Best for Budget: May-June
Hotels drop rates by 30-50%, restaurants offer deals, and attractions are empty. But you must be prepared for extreme heat. Plan indoor activities for midday and sightsee only at dawn and dusk.

### Best for Photography: October and February
Clear skies, golden light, and comfortable temperatures make these the best months for capturing Delhi's monuments and street life.

## Weather Warnings

### Smog Season (November-January)
Delhi experiences severe air pollution during November and December, caused by crop burning in neighboring states combined with winter temperature inversions. Air Quality Index (AQI) can reach "severe" levels (400+). If you have respiratory issues, consider:
- Carrying N95 masks
- Choosing hotels with air purifiers
- Limiting outdoor activities during high-AQI days
- Checking real-time AQI at aqicn.org before heading out

### Dust Storms (May-June)
Hot, dry winds called "loo" can bring dust storms with sudden temperature drops and reduced visibility. These typically last a few hours.

### Monsoon Flooding (July-August)
Heavy downpours can cause waterlogging, especially in low-lying areas of Old Delhi. Check weather forecasts daily and avoid areas prone to flooding.

## Planning Tips

- **Book 2-3 months ahead** for peak season (October-March) to get better hotel rates
- **Check festival dates** — they follow the lunar calendar and change each year
- **Pack layers in winter** — mornings and evenings are cold, but afternoons can be warm
- **Travel insurance** is important for monsoon season due to potential flight disruptions
- **If visiting November-January**, monitor AQI forecasts and pack accordingly
