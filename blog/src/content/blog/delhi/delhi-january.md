## Delhi in January

January is Delhi's coldest month and one of the most eventful, anchored by the spectacular Republic Day celebrations on January 26. The weather demands warm clothing, but the clear winter skies (when not foggy) and festive energy make it a rewarding time to visit.

## Weather

- **Average high:** 20°C (68°F)
- **Average low:** 7°C (45°F), dropping to 3-4°C on the coldest nights
- **Rainfall:** Minimal (15-20 mm)
- **Humidity:** Low to moderate
- **Sunshine hours:** 6-7 hours per day

### What to Expect
January mornings start cold and often foggy — dense fog can reduce visibility to under 50 meters, causing flight delays and road disruptions. By mid-morning (10:00-11:00 AM), the fog usually lifts and afternoons can be pleasant at 18-22°C with bright sunshine. Evenings turn cold quickly after 5:00 PM.

**Air quality alert:** January air quality can be poor, though typically better than November-December. Check AQI levels daily.

## Republic Day (January 26)

The highlight of January in Delhi is **Republic Day**, commemorating the adoption of India's constitution in 1950. The grand military parade along Kartavya Path (formerly Rajpath) is one of the world's most impressive parades.

### What to Expect
- **Military parade** featuring tanks, missile launchers, march-pasts by different regiments, and a spectacular flypast by the Indian Air Force
- **Cultural tableaux** from each Indian state showcasing their heritage
- **Beating Retreat Ceremony** on January 29 at Vijay Chowk — a beautiful musical ceremony marking the end of Republic Day celebrations
- **Security:** Areas around India Gate and Kartavya Path are heavily secured for days before and after. Access requires tickets obtained through the government portal or embassies.

### How to Get Tickets
Tickets for the Republic Day parade are available through the official government website (typically released in early January). Foreign tourists can also request tickets through their embassy. Free seats are available but go quickly.

## Things to Do in January

### Outdoor Activities
Despite the cold, January offers several unique experiences:
- **Morning bird walks** at Okhla Bird Sanctuary — winter migratory birds from Central Asia and Siberia are present
- **Lodhi Gardens** morning walks in the crisp winter air
- **Kite flying** on Makar Sankranti (January 14) — the festival of kites fills Delhi's sky with thousands of colorful kites
- **Mughal Gardens** at Rashtrapati Bhawan may start opening in late January (check dates)

### Cultural Activities
- **National Gallery of Modern Art** for indoor art exploration
- **Dastkar Nature Bazaar** — a winter crafts fair at Andheria Modh featuring handmade goods from across India
- **Live music and theater** at India Habitat Centre and Kamani Auditorium

### Food Experiences
January is peak season for winter delicacies:
- **Sarson ka saag and makki ki roti** (mustard greens and corn bread) — the quintessential Punjabi winter dish, available at dhabas across Delhi
- **Gajar ka halwa** — a warm carrot dessert made with fresh winter carrots
- **Chai** (spiced tea) — January is when Delhi's roadside chai stalls are at their most appealing
- **Rewri and gajak** — sesame and jaggery sweets associated with Makar Sankranti

## Packing for January

- **Warm jacket or puffer coat** — essential for mornings and evenings
- **Layers** — a sweater or fleece under a jacket works well
- **Scarf and gloves** — mornings and evenings can be bitingly cold
- **Thermals** — recommended for early risers or those exploring before 9:00 AM
- **Closed-toe shoes** with warm socks
- **N95 mask** — useful for fog and poor air quality days
- **Moisturizer and lip balm** — the cold, dry air chaps skin quickly

## Practical Tips

- **Flight delays are common** — book morning flights with buffer time, or prefer afternoon departures
- **Fog affects trains too** — North Indian trains can be delayed by hours in January
- **Hotels have heaters** — most mid-range and above hotels have room heaters or central heating, but verify at budget hotels
- **Start sightseeing at 10:00 AM** after fog clears rather than at dawn
- **Sunset is early** — around 5:30 PM. Plan outdoor activities accordingly
- **Hotel prices are at peak** — book well in advance for the best rates, especially around Republic Day

## January Events Calendar

- **New Year celebrations** (January 1) — parties across the city
- **Lohri** (January 13) — Punjabi harvest festival with bonfires
- **Makar Sankranti** (January 14) — kite flying festival
- **Republic Day Parade** (January 26)
- **Beating Retreat** (January 29)
