## Bali Visa Options Explained

Understanding your visa options before traveling to Bali saves time, money, and stress at the airport. Indonesia offers several visa types for tourists, and choosing the right one depends on your nationality, length of stay, and purpose of visit. Here is a clear breakdown of each option.

## Visa on Arrival (VoA)

### Overview
The most common visa for tourists visiting Bali. Available to citizens of over 90 countries including the US, UK, Canada, Australia, and most EU nations.

### Key Details
- **Cost:** IDR 500,000 (approximately USD 35)
- **Validity:** 30 days from date of entry
- **Extension:** Extendable once for an additional 30 days (maximum 60 days total)
- **Purpose:** Tourism, social/cultural visits, business meetings, government visits

### How to Get It
**Option 1: e-VoA (Recommended)**
Apply online at **molina.imigrasi.go.id** at least 48 hours before departure. Pay by credit card. You receive a QR code that lets you skip the payment queue and use automated gates at the airport. Processing is usually instant.

**Option 2: VoA at the Airport**
Pay at the dedicated VoA counter after landing. Accepts IDR cash, USD cash, or credit cards. Queues can be 30-60+ minutes during busy periods.

### Requirements
- Passport valid for at least 6 months with 2+ blank pages
- Return or onward flight ticket
- Payment (IDR 500,000 or USD 35)

## VoA Extension

If you want to stay beyond 30 days, you can extend your VoA once:

### Extension Process
1. Visit a local immigration office (at least 7 days before your visa expires)
2. Bring: passport, current VoA, passport photos, proof of accommodation
3. Provide biometric data (fingerprints, photos) — required since mid-2025
4. Pay the extension fee: IDR 500,000 (USD 35)
5. Processing takes 3-5 working days (you may need to visit twice)

### Extension Tips
- Start the process at least 14 days before expiry to allow time
- Immigration offices can be busy — arrive early (7-8 AM)
- Some travelers use visa agents to handle the process (IDR 800,000-1,200,000 including agent fee)
- Denpasar and Kuta immigration offices are the most efficient for tourists

## B211A Tourist Visa (60-Day Single Entry)

### Overview
For travelers planning a longer stay (31-60 days) or who want to avoid the extension hassle.

### Key Details
- **Cost:** IDR 1,500,000 (approximately USD 100) plus agent fees if applicable
- **Validity:** 60 days, extendable up to 4 times (30 days each, maximum 180 days total)
- **Application:** Must be applied for before arrival through an Indonesian embassy/consulate or approved visa agent
- **Processing time:** 3-7 working days

### When to Choose B211A
- Staying 31-60 days and do not want to deal with extensions
- Planning a long-term stay (up to 180 days with extensions)
- Digital nomads or slow travelers

## Visa-Free Entry

### Overview
Citizens of certain ASEAN countries (Singapore, Malaysia, Thailand, Philippines, etc.) and a select few other nations may enter Indonesia visa-free for up to 30 days.

### Key Limitation
Visa-free entry **cannot be extended**. If there is any chance you might want to stay longer than 30 days, opt for the VoA instead (which can be extended).

## Comparison Table

| Feature | Visa Free | VoA | e-VoA | B211A |
|---------|-----------|-----|-------|-------|
| Cost | Free | IDR 500,000 | IDR 500,000 | IDR 1,500,000+ |
| Initial stay | 30 days | 30 days | 30 days | 60 days |
| Extendable | No | Yes (1x30 days) | Yes (1x30 days) | Yes (4x30 days) |
| Max stay | 30 days | 60 days | 60 days | 180 days |
| Apply before travel | No | No | Yes (recommended) | Yes (required) |
| Airport queue | None | Long | Short (e-gates) | None |

## Bali Tourist Levy

In addition to your visa, all tourists must pay the mandatory **Bali Tourist Levy:**
- **Cost:** IDR 150,000 (approximately USD 10)
- **Payment:** Online via lovebali.baliprov.go.id (recommended) or at the airport
- **Validity:** Per visit (not per day)
- **Purpose:** Funds environmental conservation and cultural preservation

## Important Reminders

- **Never overstay your visa.** Overstaying incurs fines of IDR 1,000,000 per day and possible detention and deportation.
- **Your passport must have 6+ months validity** from your date of entry — airlines may refuse boarding if it does not.
- **Keep a copy of your visa** (digital photo) separate from your passport.
- **The e-VoA is strongly recommended** — the time saved at the airport is worth the 5 minutes of online application.

Bali's visa process is straightforward for most nationalities. For stays of 30 days or less, the e-VoA is the clear best choice. For longer stays, the B211A visa offers flexibility without the hassle of frequent extensions.
