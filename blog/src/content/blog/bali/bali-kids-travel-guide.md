## Bali with Kids: A Parent's Complete Guide

Bali is one of Asia's most family-friendly destinations, but traveling with children always requires extra planning. This guide covers the practical details that matter most to parents — from health precautions and food safety to age-appropriate activities and getting around with little ones.

## Health and Safety

### Vaccinations and Health Prep
Consult your doctor 4-6 weeks before travel. Commonly recommended vaccinations for Bali include:
- Hepatitis A and B
- Typhoid
- Routine vaccinations (up to date)
- Consider anti-malarial medication if visiting very rural areas (low risk in tourist zones)

### Water Safety
- **Never drink tap water** — use bottled or filtered water for drinking and brushing teeth
- **Ice in restaurants** is generally safe (factory-made) but avoid ice from street vendors
- **Bring a reusable bottle** and refill at filtered water stations (available at most hotels)

### Sun Protection
The equatorial sun is intense. For children:
- Apply SPF 50+ sunscreen every 2 hours
- Sun-protective clothing (UV rash guards) for beach and pool time
- Wide-brimmed hats
- Seek shade during peak hours (10 AM - 2 PM)

### Mosquitoes
Dengue fever is present in Bali, especially during wet season. Protect children with:
- DEET-based repellent (20-30% DEET is safe for children over 2 months)
- Long sleeves and pants at dawn and dusk
- Mosquito nets at night if accommodation does not have screens

### Medical Facilities
- **BIMC Hospital Kuta** — International-standard private hospital with pediatric services
- **Siloam Hospital Denpasar** — Good general hospital
- **Pharmacies** are widespread — common children's medications are available (Paracetamol, rehydration salts)

## Food for Kids

### What Kids Usually Enjoy
Indonesian food has many kid-friendly options:
- **Nasi goreng** — Fried rice (ask for "tidak pedas" — not spicy)
- **Mie goreng** — Fried noodles
- **Satay** — Grilled chicken skewers with peanut sauce
- **Pisang goreng** — Fried banana fritters
- **Fresh fruit** — Mango, watermelon, dragon fruit, pineapple
- **Pancakes and toast** — Available at most hotel breakfasts and cafes

### Food Safety Tips
- Start with hotel restaurants and established cafes where hygiene standards are visible
- Gradually introduce warung food if children's stomachs are adjusting well
- Avoid very spicy food initially — always specify "tidak pedas" when ordering
- Carry snacks from home for emergencies (familiar crackers, granola bars)
- Baby food and formula are available at pharmacies and minimarts (Guardian, Kimia Farma)

## Age-Appropriate Activities

### Toddlers (0-3 years)
- Beach play at calm-water beaches (Nusa Dua, Sanur, Jimbaran)
- Hotel pools (many have shallow children's areas)
- Watching monkeys at the Monkey Forest (from a safe distance, in a carrier)
- Rice terrace walks (bring a carrier — paths are uneven)
- Baby-friendly spa treatments at some resorts

### Young Children (4-7 years)
- Waterbom Bali water park (specific kids' zones)
- Bali Safari and Marine Park
- Bali Bird Park
- Beach days with sand castle building
- Simple snorkeling in shallow, calm water
- Kite flying at Sanur Beach
- Balinese cooking class (family-friendly ones available)

### Older Children (8-12 years)
- Surf lessons at Kuta or Batu Bolong
- White water rafting on the Ayung River (age 5+ for most operators)
- ATV riding (as passenger with adult)
- Snorkeling at Blue Lagoon or Amed
- Mountain cycling tours
- Sacred Monkey Forest exploration
- Silver jewelry making class in Celuk village

### Teenagers (13+)
- Surfing at intermediate breaks
- Scuba diving (PADI Junior Open Water from age 10)
- Canyoning adventures
- Mount Batur sunrise trek
- Cooking classes
- Exploring Ubud Art Market independently

## Getting Around with Kids

### Private Driver (Recommended)
The most comfortable family option. A private car with driver costs IDR 500,000-700,000 per day and eliminates the stress of navigation, parking, and traffic. Most drivers can accommodate car seats if you bring your own.

### Grab/Gojek
Useful for short trips. Note that:
- **Car seats are not provided** — bring a portable booster if needed
- GrabFamily may not be available in Bali
- Always request a car (not motorbike) when traveling with children

### Scooters
**Not recommended with children.** Scooters are dangerous for adults; adding a child multiplies the risk. Despite seeing local families on scooters, tourists should avoid this.

## Practical Tips for Parents

- **Pace your itinerary.** Build in pool time and rest every afternoon. Over-scheduling leads to meltdowns.
- **Pack infant essentials** — while available in Bali, familiar brands may not be stocked
- **Bring a small first-aid kit** with children's paracetamol, anti-diarrhea medication, plasters, and antiseptic
- **Download offline entertainment** for car journeys — Bali drives between areas can take 60-90 minutes
- **Laundry services** are cheap (IDR 10,000-15,000 per kg) — pack light and wash frequently
- **Babysitting** is available at most mid-range and luxury hotels — book 24 hours ahead
- **Balinese people adore children** — expect your kids to get lots of smiles, attention, and occasional treats from friendly locals
