## Avoiding Visa Problems in Bali

While most tourists enter Bali without any issues, visa-related problems do occur — and they can derail your entire trip. Understanding the common reasons for visa rejections, entry denials, and documentation issues helps you prepare properly and avoid unnecessary stress at the airport.

## Most Common Issues

### 1. Passport Validity (Less Than 6 Months)
**The single most common issue.** Indonesia requires your passport to be valid for at least 6 months from your date of entry. Airlines may refuse to board you if your passport does not meet this requirement, even before you reach Bali.

**Solution:** Check your passport expiry date before booking flights. If it expires within 7-8 months of your travel date, renew it before your trip.

### 2. No Return or Onward Ticket
Indonesian immigration requires proof that you will leave the country within your visa period. This means a confirmed return flight or an onward ticket to another country.

**Solutions:**
- Book a return flight before applying for your visa
- If you are a flexible traveler, book a refundable or cheap onward ticket
- Overland travel (ferry to Malaysia, etc.) may be acceptable — carry proof of the booking

### 3. Insufficient Blank Passport Pages
You need at least 2 blank pages for the VoA stamp and entry/exit stamps. Immigration officers will refuse to stamp a passport that is full or nearly full.

**Solution:** Count your blank pages before travel. If you are running low, get extra pages added or renew your passport.

### 4. e-VoA Payment/Technical Issues
Online e-VoA applications can fail due to:
- Credit card payment rejection (some banks flag Indonesian transactions as suspicious)
- System outages on the immigration portal
- Incorrect passport details entered

**Solutions:**
- Apply at least 48-72 hours before departure to allow time for troubleshooting
- Notify your bank of upcoming Indonesian transactions
- Have a backup plan — you can always get the VoA at the airport counter
- Double-check all passport details match exactly

### 5. Previous Overstay in Indonesia
If you have overstayed a previous Indonesian visa, you may face difficulties on return. Serious overstays can result in a ban from re-entering Indonesia.

**Solution:** Always depart before your visa expires. If you need more time, extend through proper channels.

### 6. Nationality Not Eligible for VoA
Not all nationalities are eligible for the Visa on Arrival. Citizens of some countries must apply for a visa through an Indonesian embassy before travel.

**Solution:** Check the latest eligible nationality list on the Indonesian immigration website or your country's foreign affairs website.

### 7. Incomplete Digital Declarations
Since the All Indonesia app became mandatory in late 2025, failing to complete digital customs and immigration declarations can cause delays.

**Solution:** Download the All Indonesia app and complete all declarations before landing.

## B211A Visa Rejection Reasons

For those applying for the longer-stay B211A visa, additional rejection reasons include:

- **Incomplete application** — missing documents, photos, or information
- **Inconsistent information** — details do not match between application and passport
- **No sponsor** — B211A applications require a sponsor (person or visa agent in Indonesia)
- **Previous immigration violations** — any past issues with Indonesian immigration

## What to Do If You Face Issues

### At the Airport
- Remain calm and polite — immigration officers have discretion
- Have all documents ready (passport, return ticket, hotel booking, e-VoA)
- If denied the e-VoA, try the manual VoA counter
- Contact your embassy if you face serious issues

### Before Travel
- Check all requirements at least 2 weeks before departure
- Ensure your passport meets all requirements
- Have digital copies of all documents (stored in phone and email)
- Purchase travel insurance that covers trip disruption

## Prevention Checklist

Before your Bali trip, confirm:
- [ ] Passport valid for 6+ months from entry date
- [ ] At least 2 blank passport pages
- [ ] Return or onward flight booked
- [ ] e-VoA applied for (or cash/card ready for airport VoA)
- [ ] Tourist levy paid via Love Bali portal
- [ ] All Indonesia app downloaded and declarations completed
- [ ] Hotel booking confirmation available
- [ ] Travel insurance purchased
- [ ] Bank notified of travel to Indonesia

Following this checklist virtually eliminates the risk of visa-related issues. The vast majority of travelers enter Bali smoothly — these problems affect a small percentage, and nearly all are preventable with basic preparation.
