## The Perfect 5 Days in Bali

Five days is the sweet spot for a Bali trip — long enough to explore the island's diverse regions without feeling rushed, but short enough to keep every day exciting. This itinerary takes you from Ubud's cultural heart to the dramatic cliffs of Uluwatu, the pristine shores of Nusa Penida, and the vibrant beach scene of Seminyak and Canggu.

**Recommended base strategy:** Stay 2 nights in Ubud, then 3 nights in Seminyak or Canggu.

## Day 1: Arrive and Explore Ubud

### Morning/Afternoon — Settle In
After arriving at Ngurah Rai Airport, transfer to Ubud (60-90 minutes by private car, IDR 350,000-500,000). Check into your hotel and take a moment to absorb the lush green surroundings.

### Afternoon — Campuhan Ridge Walk
Start with an easy, scenic walk along Campuhan Ridge, a narrow path between two river valleys lined with tall grasses and coconut palms. Best in the late afternoon when the light is golden. Free to access, takes 30-45 minutes each way.

### Evening — Ubud Royal Palace Dance Performance
Catch a traditional Balinese dance at the Ubud Royal Palace (7:30 PM, IDR 100,000). The Legong dance is a hypnotic performance of intricate finger movements, elaborate costumes, and gamelan orchestra. Arrive 30 minutes early for good seats.

Dinner at **Warung Biah Biah** for authentic Balinese cuisine (IDR 35,000-60,000 per dish) or **Hujan Locale** for upscale Indonesian fusion (IDR 80,000-180,000).

## Day 2: Ubud Deep Dive

### Early Morning
**5:30 AM — Tegallalang Rice Terraces at Sunrise**
Beat every tour bus by arriving at dawn. The terraces are magical when morning mist rises from the paddies. Entry: IDR 25,000. Spend 60-90 minutes walking the paths and enjoying the serenity.

**8:00 AM — Breakfast at Ubud Hotel**

### Mid-Morning
**9:30 AM — Tirta Empul Temple**
Drive 15 minutes to this sacred spring temple for a purification ritual. Wade through the holy spring pools, passing under each of the water spouts. A deeply spiritual experience regardless of your beliefs. Entry: IDR 50,000. Time: 90 minutes.

### Afternoon
**12:00 PM — Lunch at Locavore To Go**
Sample dishes from Bali's most acclaimed restaurant at more accessible prices (IDR 50,000-120,000).

**1:30 PM — Sacred Monkey Forest**
Explore the mossy temple ruins and watch the macaques in their natural habitat. Entry: IDR 80,000. Time: 60-90 minutes.

**3:30 PM — Ubud Art Market**
Browse handcrafted souvenirs. Bargain enthusiastically but fairly — starting at 50% of asking price.

### Evening
**5:00 PM — Tegenungan Waterfall**
An easy 20-minute drive south of Ubud, this waterfall is less crowded in the late afternoon. Swim in the pool at the base for a refreshing end to the day. Entry: IDR 25,000.

**7:30 PM — Dinner at Bebek Bengil**
The famous "Dirty Duck Diner" serves excellent crispy duck in a rice paddy setting (IDR 85,000-150,000).

## Day 3: Nusa Penida Day Trip

### Full Day
**6:30 AM — Fast Boat to Nusa Penida**
Head to Sanur Harbor (45 minutes from Ubud) for the 30-45 minute fast boat crossing to Nusa Penida. Book tickets in advance — round-trip costs IDR 300,000-500,000 (USD 20-33).

**8:30 AM — Kelingking Beach**
The T-Rex-shaped cliff viewpoint is one of Bali's most iconic photo spots. You can hike down to the beach (steep, 30-40 minutes each way) or simply enjoy the viewpoint.

**10:30 AM — Angel's Billabong and Broken Beach**
A natural infinity pool carved into the clifftop (Angel's Billabong) and a dramatic natural rock arch over turquoise water (Broken Beach). Both are free to visit and located close together.

**12:30 PM — Lunch at a Local Warung**
Simple but delicious Indonesian food on the island (IDR 30,000-50,000).

**2:00 PM — Crystal Bay or Atuh Beach**
Choose between Crystal Bay (great snorkeling, chance to see manta rays and mola mola) or Atuh Beach (stunning cliff-framed cove). Both are beautiful for swimming.

**4:30 PM — Return Boat**
Catch the afternoon boat back to Sanur and transfer to your south Bali accommodation.

**Evening — Settle into Seminyak/Canggu**
Dinner at **Old Man's** in Canggu for casual beach vibes and live music (mains IDR 60,000-120,000) or **Motel Mexicola** in Seminyak for Mexican food and cocktails (IDR 80,000-180,000).

## Day 4: Uluwatu and the Southern Coast

### Morning
**9:00 AM — Melasti Beach**
Start at one of Bali's most beautiful beaches. The approach road carved through white limestone cliffs is dramatic, and the beach itself features pristine white sand and turquoise water. Parking: IDR 10,000-25,000.

**11:00 AM — Padang Padang Beach**
This small cove tucked between cliffs is Bali's most photogenic beach. Enter through a narrow cave opening in the rocks. Entry: IDR 15,000.

### Afternoon
**12:30 PM — Lunch at Single Fin**
Legendary surf bar perched on the Uluwatu cliffs with panoramic ocean views and solid food (IDR 80,000-180,000).

**2:00 PM — Surf Lesson or Beach Time**
Take a 2-hour surf lesson at Padang Padang or Dreamland Beach (IDR 350,000-500,000 including board). Or simply relax on the beach.

### Evening
**5:00 PM — Uluwatu Temple and Kecak Dance**
Explore the clifftop temple before the 6 PM Kecak fire dance begins. Temple entry: IDR 150,000. Kecak ticket: IDR 150,000. This sunset performance is one of Bali's absolute highlights.

**8:00 PM — Jimbaran Seafood Dinner**
Dine on freshly grilled seafood at one of the beachfront restaurants lining Jimbaran Bay. Choose your fish, prawns, and squid by weight. Budget IDR 200,000-400,000 per person.

## Day 5: Beach, Wellness, and Farewell

### Morning
**8:00 AM — Yoga Class**
Start your final day with a yoga session. **The Practice Bali** in Canggu and **Yoga Barn** in Ubud are both excellent options (IDR 150,000-200,000 per drop-in class).

**10:00 AM — Tanah Lot Temple**
Visit Bali's most famous sea temple during the quieter morning hours. Walk around the coastal cliff paths and explore the smaller temples in the complex. Entry: IDR 75,000.

### Afternoon
**12:30 PM — Brunch/Lunch**
Enjoy a final Bali brunch at **Sisterfields** in Seminyak (IDR 80,000-150,000) or **Crate Cafe** in Canggu (IDR 60,000-120,000).

**2:00 PM — Beach Club Finale**
Spend your last afternoon at a beach club:
- **Finns Beach Club** (Canggu) — Multiple pools, bars, and beachfront. Day bed from IDR 350,000.
- **Potato Head** (Seminyak) — Iconic design and atmosphere. Day bed from IDR 300,000.
- **The Lawn** (Canggu) — Laid-back beanbags on the grass overlooking Echo Beach. Min spend IDR 200,000.

### Evening
**6:00 PM — Sunset Drinks**
Watch your last Bali sunset from Seminyak Beach or from the rooftop at **Double Six Rooftop** (cocktails IDR 120,000-180,000).

**8:00 PM — Farewell Dinner**
Splurge on your final meal at **Sarong** in Seminyak (Asian fine dining, tasting menu IDR 650,000) or **Mason** in Canggu (wood-fired European cuisine, mains IDR 150,000-300,000).

## Key Tips for 5 Days

- **Pre-book the Nusa Penida boat** at least 2-3 days ahead during peak season
- **Pack light layers** — mornings in Ubud can be cool, especially at the rice terraces
- **Arrange airport transfer** in advance for your departure day to avoid stress
- **Consider travel insurance** that covers water sports and scooter riding
- **Exchange money** at authorized money changers (BMC and Central Kuta are reputable) rather than random street booths

Five days in Bali leaves you with a rich tapestry of memories — temple silhouettes against orange skies, the sound of gamelan music drifting through rice paddies, the rush of your first wave, and the warmth of Balinese hospitality.
