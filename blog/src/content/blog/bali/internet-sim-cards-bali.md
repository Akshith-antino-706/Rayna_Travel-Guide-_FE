## Staying Connected in Bali

Reliable internet access is essential for modern travel — from ride-hailing apps and maps to restaurant bookings and staying in touch with home. Bali's connectivity has improved dramatically in recent years, and staying online is straightforward and affordable. Here is everything you need to know.

## Tourist SIM Cards

### Telkomsel (Recommended)
Indonesia's largest mobile operator with the widest coverage across Bali, including remote areas in the north and east. Their tourist SIM package is the best option for most visitors.

- **Tourist SIMPATI Card:** IDR 150,000 (USD 10) for 25GB data + 25 minutes voice calls
- **Validity:** 30 days from activation
- **Coverage:** Best on the island — works in rural areas where other providers struggle
- **Where to buy:** Airport counter (recommended), Telkomsel stores, authorized resellers, minimarts

### XL Axiata
Good value with competitive pricing and solid coverage in tourist areas (Seminyak, Canggu, Ubud, Kuta). Coverage drops off in rural and northern areas.

- **Tourist package:** From IDR 100,000 for 15-20GB
- **Best for:** Travelers staying in south Bali tourist zones

### Indosat Ooredoo
Competitive prices but less consistent coverage than Telkomsel. Acceptable in major tourist areas.

- **Tourist package:** From IDR 100,000 for 15GB
- **Best for:** Budget-conscious travelers in main areas

### Buying Tips
- **Buy at the airport** for convenience — Telkomsel has a counter right after customs
- **Bring your passport** — required for SIM card registration (Indonesian law)
- **Staff will set up the SIM** for you and confirm it is working before you leave
- **Check your phone is unlocked** — locked phones will not accept foreign SIM cards

## eSIM Options

If your phone supports eSIM (most iPhones from XS onwards, many recent Androids), this is the most convenient option:

### Telkomsel eSIM
- From USD 13 for 10GB, up to USD 25 for 87GB
- Purchase online before arrival at Telkomsel's website
- Activate via QR code — no physical card needed
- Same excellent Telkomsel coverage

### International eSIM Providers
- **Airalo:** Indonesia eSIM from USD 5 for 1GB (7 days) to USD 26 for 20GB (30 days)
- **Holafly:** Unlimited data eSIM from USD 19 for 5 days to USD 47 for 20 days
- **Nomad:** Indonesia eSIM from USD 5 for 1GB

**Advantages of eSIM:**
- Set up before you arrive — you are connected the moment you land
- No need to swap physical SIM cards
- Keep your home number active on one SIM slot

## WiFi in Bali

### Hotel and Accommodation WiFi
Virtually every hotel, hostel, and guesthouse in Bali offers free WiFi. Speeds vary:
- **Budget properties:** 5-15 Mbps (adequate for browsing and messaging)
- **Mid-range hotels:** 15-50 Mbps (good for streaming and video calls)
- **Luxury resorts:** 50-100+ Mbps (fiber connections common)

### Cafe and Restaurant WiFi
Most cafes and restaurants in tourist areas offer free WiFi, though quality varies. Canggu's cafes tend to have the best WiFi, catering to the digital nomad community.

### Coworking Spaces
If you need reliable, fast internet for work:
- **Dojo Bali** (Canggu) — Fast fiber internet, daily/weekly passes from IDR 150,000/day
- **Outpost** (Canggu/Ubud) — Premium coworking with strong connections, from IDR 200,000/day
- **Hubud** (Ubud) — Bali's original coworking space in a beautiful bamboo structure

## Coverage and Connectivity Notes

- **South Bali** (Kuta, Seminyak, Canggu, Uluwatu, Nusa Dua) has excellent 4G coverage
- **Ubud and surroundings** have good coverage in town, patchier in rural rice terrace areas
- **North Bali** (Lovina, Munduk, Singaraja) has acceptable but slower coverage
- **Remote areas** (mountain treks, isolated waterfalls) may have no signal
- **Nusa Penida** has improved but can still be spotty in some areas

## Useful Apps to Download Before Arrival

- **Grab** — Ride-hailing (Bali's Uber equivalent)
- **Gojek** — Ride-hailing and food delivery
- **Google Maps** — Download Bali offline maps before your trip
- **WhatsApp** — The primary communication app in Indonesia. Hotels, drivers, and tour operators all use it
- **XE Currency** — Quick IDR conversion reference
- **All Indonesia** — Digital immigration and customs declaration

## Tips for Staying Connected

- **Download offline maps** for areas where you expect poor signal
- **Carry a power bank** — your phone battery will drain faster with constant GPS and data use
- **Test your SIM card** at the shop before leaving to confirm it works
- **Top up data** at any minimart (Alfamart, Indomaret) if you run out — staff can help
- **Free WiFi networks** in public spaces may not be secure — avoid banking on public WiFi

Connectivity in Bali is affordable and accessible. A Telkomsel tourist SIM card at the airport takes 10 minutes to set up and keeps you reliably connected for your entire trip.
