## Bali: A Plant-Based Paradise

Bali has become one of the world's best destinations for vegetarian and vegan travelers. The combination of a health-conscious expat community, Balinese Hindu traditions that include many vegetarian dishes, and a wellness culture centered around yoga and mindful eating has created a thriving plant-based food scene. From raw food temples in Ubud to vegan beach clubs in Canggu, you will never struggle to find delicious meat-free meals.

## Best Vegan and Vegetarian Restaurants

### Ubud

**Alchemy** — Bali's most famous raw food restaurant and juice bar. The self-serve salad bar lets you create custom bowls of raw, organic, and living foods. The raw desserts are legendary.
- **Price:** IDR 80,000-150,000 per person
- **Must-try:** Raw chocolate cake, build-your-own salad bowl

**Sage** — Organic plant-based fine dining that proves vegan food can be elegant. Seasonal tasting menus showcase local ingredients in creative preparations.
- **Price:** Tasting menu from IDR 450,000
- **Must-try:** Seasonal tasting menu, mushroom dishes

**Sayuri Healing Food** — Japanese-influenced raw vegan cafe with fermented foods, sprouted dishes, and healing elixirs. Beautiful garden setting.
- **Price:** IDR 60,000-120,000
- **Must-try:** Living sushi rolls, fermented cashew cheese

**Moksa** — A permaculture-based restaurant that grows much of its own produce. The menu changes based on what is harvested, and cooking classes are available.
- **Price:** IDR 70,000-130,000
- **Must-try:** Farm-to-table tasting plate, tempeh dishes

### Canggu

**Peloton Supershop** — A trendy plant-based cafe with excellent smoothie bowls, burgers, and all-day breakfast. Popular with the digital nomad crowd.
- **Price:** IDR 60,000-120,000
- **Must-try:** Acai bowl, plant-based burger

**The Shady Shack** — A beloved Canggu institution serving creative vegetarian and vegan dishes in a charming open-air setting surrounded by rice paddies.
- **Price:** IDR 50,000-100,000
- **Must-try:** Nasi goreng bowl, raw snickers bar

**Cafe Organic** — Health-focused cafe with beautiful smoothie bowls, fresh juices, and all-day brunch options. Photogenic food in a photogenic setting.
- **Price:** IDR 70,000-130,000
- **Must-try:** Dragon fruit bowl, avocado toast

### Seminyak

**Kynd Community** — Instagram-famous for its pink decor and stunning smoothie bowls. The menu is entirely plant-based with creative options.
- **Price:** IDR 60,000-120,000
- **Must-try:** Unicorn smoothie bowl, vegan nachos

**Earth Cafe** — One of Bali's oldest vegan restaurants, serving a massive menu of plant-based versions of international dishes.
- **Price:** IDR 40,000-90,000
- **Must-try:** Raw pizza, tempeh steak

## Traditional Balinese Vegetarian Dishes

Indonesian cuisine has several naturally vegetarian or easily adapted dishes:

- **Gado-gado** — Mixed vegetables with peanut sauce (vegetarian staple)
- **Tempeh** — Fermented soybeans, a protein-rich Indonesian specialty. Available grilled, fried, or in curries.
- **Tofu (Tahu)** — Used extensively in Indonesian cooking. Try tahu goreng (fried tofu).
- **Nasi goreng sayur** — Vegetable fried rice (specify "tanpa daging, tanpa ayam" — no meat, no chicken)
- **Cap cay** — Stir-fried mixed vegetables
- **Urap** — Steamed vegetables with spiced grated coconut
- **Lawar nangka** — Traditional Balinese jackfruit salad (ask without meat)
- **Sayur asem** — Tamarind vegetable soup
- **Pecel** — Vegetables with spicy peanut sauce

## Ordering Vegan at Local Warungs

Many warungs do not have dedicated vegan menus, but with a few Indonesian phrases, you can navigate easily:

- **"Saya vegetarian"** — I am vegetarian
- **"Tanpa daging"** — Without meat
- **"Tanpa ayam"** — Without chicken
- **"Tanpa ikan"** — Without fish
- **"Tanpa telur"** — Without egg
- **"Tanpa susu"** — Without milk
- **"Hanya sayuran"** — Only vegetables

**Note:** Balinese cooking often uses **shrimp paste (terasi)** as a base seasoning, even in vegetable dishes. If you are strict vegan, ask "tanpa terasi" (without shrimp paste) or stick to dedicated vegan restaurants.

## Vegan Grocery Shopping

For self-catering or snack stocking:
- **Bali Buda** (Ubud, Sanur, Canggu) — Organic grocery store with extensive vegan products
- **Earth Market** (Ubud) — Health food store with bulk bins, vegan cheese, plant milks
- **Pepito Market** — Upscale supermarket with international vegan products
- **Local markets** — Fresh fruit, vegetables, tempeh, and tofu at local prices

## Tips for Plant-Based Travelers

- **Ubud is the epicenter** of Bali's vegan scene — plan to spend time there
- **Canggu comes second** with an excellent selection of plant-based cafes
- **Download Happy Cow** — the app for finding vegan restaurants worldwide, works well in Bali
- **Be specific about allergies** — "vegan" is not universally understood, but "no meat, no fish, no egg, no milk" is clear
- **Carry snacks** for rural excursions where vegan options may be limited
- **Temple offerings** sometimes include food — these are vegetarian-friendly

Bali makes plant-based eating not just easy but genuinely exciting. The island's vegan food scene is among the best in the world, combining tropical ingredients, traditional Indonesian flavors, and global culinary creativity in ways that will satisfy even the most devoted carnivore.
