## Bali's Incredible Food Scene

Bali has quietly become one of Southeast Asia's most exciting culinary destinations. The island's food scene spans the full spectrum — from roadside warungs serving nasi campur for a couple of dollars to world-class tasting menus that rival anything in Singapore or Tokyo. What makes Bali's dining landscape special is the fusion of authentic Indonesian flavors with global culinary influences, all set against backdrops of rice paddies, ocean cliffs, and lush jungle.

## Fine Dining

### Locavore — Ubud
Bali's most celebrated restaurant has earned its reputation through a fanatical commitment to local ingredients. Chef Eelke Plasmeijer and his team source 95% of ingredients from Indonesian producers, reimagining local produce through a modern fine-dining lens. The tasting menus change frequently based on what is available.
- **Price:** Tasting menu from IDR 1,200,000 (USD 80) per person
- **Cuisine:** Modern Indonesian, farm-to-table
- **Reservation:** Essential — book 2-4 weeks ahead
- **Address:** Jl. Dewi Sita, Ubud

### Mauri — Seminyak
Run by South Italian chef Maurizio Bombini, Mauri delivers Italian-influenced fine dining that approaches Michelin-star quality. The tasting menus are beautifully composed, and the wine list is one of Bali's most carefully curated.
- **Price:** Tasting menu from IDR 950,000 (USD 63) per person
- **Cuisine:** Modern Italian-Indonesian fusion
- **Reservation:** Recommended
- **Address:** Jl. Petitenget, Seminyak

### Merah Putih — Seminyak
Housed in a soaring bamboo cathedral designed by architect Andra Matin, Merah Putih serves modern Indonesian cuisine that celebrates the archipelago's diverse culinary traditions. Multiple award winner.
- **Price:** Mains IDR 150,000-350,000 (USD 10-23)
- **Cuisine:** Modern Indonesian
- **Reservation:** Recommended for dinner
- **Address:** Jl. Petitenget No. 100X, Seminyak

### Apéritif — Ubud
Set in a colonial-style mansion at the Viceroy Bali, this restaurant serves European-Asian fusion in one of Bali's most elegant settings. The sunset views over the Petanu River valley are extraordinary.
- **Price:** Tasting menu from IDR 1,500,000 (USD 100) per person
- **Cuisine:** European-Asian fusion
- **Reservation:** Essential
- **Address:** Viceroy Bali, Jl. Lanyahan, Ubud

## Best Mid-Range Restaurants

### Sardine — Seminyak
A beloved seafood restaurant set among rice paddies with an open-air bamboo pavilion. The daily menu features the freshest catch, and the atmosphere at sunset is magical.
- **Price:** Mains IDR 150,000-300,000 (USD 10-20)
- **Cuisine:** Seafood, international
- **Must-try:** Whole grilled fish, soft-shell crab

### Mama San — Seminyak
A bustling pan-Asian restaurant in a converted warehouse. The menu spans Chinese, Indian, Thai, and Vietnamese dishes, all expertly prepared and generously portioned.
- **Price:** Mains IDR 130,000-280,000 (USD 9-19)
- **Cuisine:** Pan-Asian
- **Must-try:** Duck spring rolls, lamb rendang

### Mason — Canggu
A wood-fired restaurant that has become a Canggu institution. The minimalist design lets the food take center stage, and the sommelier-curated wine list is excellent.
- **Price:** Mains IDR 150,000-300,000 (USD 10-20)
- **Cuisine:** European, wood-fired
- **Must-try:** Wood-fired steak, handmade pasta

### Hujan Locale — Ubud
Indonesian fusion cuisine in a charming heritage building. Chef Will Meyrick draws on flavors from across the archipelago, creating dishes that are both innovative and deeply rooted in tradition.
- **Price:** Mains IDR 80,000-160,000 (USD 5-11)
- **Cuisine:** Indonesian fusion
- **Must-try:** Rendang, coconut-braised dishes

## Best Local Warungs (Budget Eats)

### Warung Babi Guling Ibu Oka — Ubud
The most famous warung in Bali, specializing in babi guling (suckling pig) — a Balinese celebration dish of spit-roasted pig with crispy skin, served with rice, lawar, and sambal. Anthony Bourdain called it the best pig he ever had.
- **Price:** IDR 50,000-80,000 (USD 3-5) per serving
- **Must-try:** Special babi guling plate
- **Address:** Jl. Tegal Sari No. 2, Ubud

### Warung Biah Biah — Ubud
Authentic Balinese cuisine at local prices in the heart of Ubud. Try the nasi campur Bali — a plate of rice with an assortment of meat, vegetables, sambal, and crackers.
- **Price:** IDR 35,000-60,000 (USD 2-4) per dish
- **Must-try:** Nasi campur Bali, ayam betutu

### Nasi Ayam Kedewatan Ibu Mangku — Ubud
Legendary chicken rice mixed with shredded chicken, sambal matah, and spices. Often cited as one of the best affordable meals in Bali. Expect a queue at lunch.
- **Price:** IDR 25,000-40,000 (USD 2-3)
- **Must-try:** Nasi ayam campur

### Warung Nia — Seminyak
A simple but excellent warung hidden among Seminyak's upscale restaurants. Serves generous portions of Indonesian classics at a fraction of neighboring prices.
- **Price:** IDR 40,000-70,000 (USD 3-5)
- **Must-try:** Nasi goreng, mie goreng, gado-gado

## Dining Tips

- **Reserve ahead** for popular restaurants, especially Locavore, Sardine, and Merah Putih
- **Warungs are always cash-only** — bring IDR small bills
- **Water is rarely safe from the tap** — restaurants use filtered water for cooking and ice, but stick to bottled water elsewhere
- **Tipping** is not expected at warungs but appreciated at restaurants (5-10%). Many upscale restaurants add a 10-15% service charge
- **Sunset dinner reservations** are the most competitive — book early or aim for late seatings
- **Halal food** is widely available despite Bali being Hindu — look for "Halal" signage or ask

Bali's food scene rewards adventurous eaters. Do not limit yourself to hotel restaurants and tourist-oriented cafes — the island's best meals are often found at the simplest warungs, where generations of family recipes produce flavors that no amount of fine-dining technique can replicate.
