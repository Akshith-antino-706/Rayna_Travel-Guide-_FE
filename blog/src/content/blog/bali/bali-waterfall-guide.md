## Bali's Waterfall Wonders

Bali's volcanic interior and tropical rainfall combine to create dozens of spectacular waterfalls hidden among lush jungle, sacred groves, and remote mountain valleys. From easy-access cascades perfect for a quick swim to remote multi-tier waterfalls that require a jungle trek, Bali has a waterfall for every level of adventure.

## Easy Access Waterfalls

### Tegenungan Waterfall
**The most accessible waterfall near Ubud.** A 15-meter cascade flowing into a wide pool perfect for swimming. Well-maintained staircase down (10-minute descent), facilities at the top, and multiple viewing angles.

- **Location:** Kemenuh village, 20 min south of Ubud
- **Entry fee:** IDR 25,000 adults, IDR 15,000 children
- **Difficulty:** Easy (maintained stairs)
- **Swimming:** Yes — the pool at the base is great for a dip
- **Time needed:** 60-90 minutes
- **Best time:** Early morning (before 9 AM) or late afternoon
- **Tip:** The area above the waterfall has swing platforms and cafes with river views

### Kanto Lampo Waterfall
A beautiful terraced waterfall where water cascades over layered rock formations. One of Bali's most photogenic falls, especially popular for photos where you sit among the cascading tiers.

- **Location:** Gianyar, 25 min east of Ubud
- **Entry fee:** IDR 20,000
- **Difficulty:** Easy (short walk down steps)
- **Swimming:** Limited — the water is shallow but you can sit in the cascades
- **Time needed:** 30-45 minutes
- **Tip:** Visit in the morning for the best light filtering through the canyon

### Tibumana Waterfall
A serene twin waterfall in a jungle grotto setting. Less visited than Tegenungan, offering a more peaceful experience with a deep swimming pool at the base.

- **Location:** Bangli, 30 min east of Ubud
- **Entry fee:** IDR 15,000
- **Difficulty:** Easy to moderate (15-minute walk with some steps)
- **Swimming:** Yes — deep, clear pool
- **Time needed:** 60 minutes

## Moderate Difficulty Waterfalls

### Tukad Cepung Waterfall
**Bali's most magical waterfall experience.** Hidden inside a narrow cave-like gorge, sunlight filters through the gap above, creating rays of light that illuminate the cascading water. It feels like entering a cathedral of nature.

- **Location:** Bangli, 45 min east of Ubud
- **Entry fee:** IDR 15,000
- **Difficulty:** Moderate (15-20 minute walk, river crossing, slippery rocks)
- **Swimming:** Limited — you will get wet walking through the gorge
- **Time needed:** 60-90 minutes
- **Best time:** 9-10 AM when sunlight penetrates the gorge
- **Tip:** Bring a waterproof phone case — you walk through water to reach the falls

### Gitgit Waterfall
One of Bali's tallest waterfalls at 35 meters, surrounded by lush tropical jungle in the north of the island near Lovina. The multi-tiered cascade is impressive, and the jungle walk to reach it is beautiful.

- **Location:** Gitgit, North Bali (2 hours from Ubud)
- **Entry fee:** IDR 20,000
- **Difficulty:** Moderate (15-minute walk on maintained path)
- **Swimming:** Yes — pool at the base
- **Time needed:** 60-90 minutes

### NungNung Waterfall
A powerful 50-meter waterfall that plunges into a misty pool surrounded by jungle. The descent is steep (500+ steps), making this a workout, but the remoteness and power of the falls reward the effort.

- **Location:** Petang, Badung (90 min from Ubud)
- **Entry fee:** IDR 20,000
- **Difficulty:** Moderate to hard (500+ steps down and back up)
- **Swimming:** Yes — pool at the base, though the spray is intense
- **Time needed:** 90 minutes (including the climb)

## Challenging Waterfalls

### Sekumpul Waterfall
**Often called Bali's most beautiful waterfall.** Twin cascades plunge 80 meters into a jungle pool, creating a scene that looks like something from Jurassic Park. The journey to reach them is an adventure in itself.

- **Location:** Singaraja, North Bali (2.5 hours from Ubud)
- **Entry fee:** IDR 20,000 plus optional guide fee (IDR 100,000)
- **Difficulty:** Challenging (30-40 minute hike with river crossings, steep stairs, slippery paths)
- **Swimming:** Yes — the pool at the base is spectacular
- **Time needed:** 2-3 hours
- **Tip:** Hire a local guide for the trail — navigation can be confusing, and river crossings are easier with help

### Aling-Aling Waterfall
A series of waterfalls with natural water slides and cliff jumping spots. The main fall is 35 meters high, and the lower pools feature a natural water slide and jumping points at 5m and 12m heights.

- **Location:** Sambangan, North Bali
- **Entry fee:** IDR 20,000 (guide included for the sliding/jumping area)
- **Difficulty:** Moderate (the viewing is easy; the sliding and jumping require confidence)
- **Swimming:** Yes, plus natural slides and cliff jumps
- **Time needed:** 2 hours

## Best Season for Waterfalls

- **Wet season (November-March):** Most impressive water flow, lushest vegetation. However, paths are slippery and some waterfalls may be dangerous due to high water volume.
- **Dry season (April-October):** Reduced water flow but safer conditions and easier hiking. Some smaller waterfalls may be just a trickle by August.
- **Sweet spot:** April-May or October-November — good water flow with manageable conditions.

## Waterfall Packing List

- Waterproof phone case or dry bag
- Quick-dry clothing and swimwear
- Sturdy shoes with good grip (not flip-flops)
- Towel
- Change of clothes
- Sunscreen and insect repellent
- Drinking water
- Small amount of cash for entry fees and donations

Bali's waterfalls offer some of the island's most memorable moments. From the easy, Instagrammable Tegenungan to the epic adventure of reaching Sekumpul, there is a waterfall experience for every type of traveler.
