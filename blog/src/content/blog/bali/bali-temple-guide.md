## Bali's Temple Heritage

Bali is known as the "Island of the Gods" with good reason — there are over 20,000 temples across the island. Every village has at least three temples, every family compound has its own shrine, and the island's most spectacular temples are built on dramatic clifftops, volcanic lakeshores, and sacred springs. Visiting Bali's temples is essential to understanding the island's spiritual heart.

## Must-Visit Temples

### Tanah Lot Temple
**Bali's most photographed temple.** Perched on a rock formation just offshore, Tanah Lot was built in the 16th century by the Javanese sage Nirartha. The temple is silhouetted against the sky, and at low tide you can walk across to the rock's base where Hindu priests offer blessings from a sacred spring.

- **Location:** Beraban, Tabanan (45 min from Seminyak, 90 min from Ubud)
- **Entry fee:** IDR 75,000 adults, IDR 40,000 children
- **Hours:** 7:00 AM – 7:00 PM daily
- **Best time:** Sunset (most dramatic) or early morning (fewest crowds)
- **Time needed:** 60-90 minutes
- **Note:** The temple itself is closed to non-Hindu visitors, but the surrounding complex and coastal paths are open

### Uluwatu Temple (Pura Luhur Uluwatu)
One of Bali's six key directional temples, Uluwatu sits on a dramatic 70-meter cliff overlooking the Indian Ocean. The temple dates to the 11th century and is famous for its nightly Kecak fire dance performance at sunset.

- **Location:** Bukit Peninsula, southern Bali
- **Entry fee:** IDR 150,000 adults, IDR 100,000 children
- **Hours:** 7:00 AM – 7:00 PM
- **Kecak dance:** 6:00 PM daily (separate ticket, IDR 150,000)
- **Time needed:** 2-3 hours (including Kecak dance)
- **Warning:** The resident monkeys are notorious thieves — secure all glasses, hats, and loose items

### Besakih Temple (Pura Besakih)
**Bali's holiest and largest temple complex.** Known as the "Mother Temple," Besakih sits on the slopes of Mount Agung at 1,000 meters elevation. The complex comprises 23 separate temples spread across the mountainside, with Pura Penataran Agung as the central and most important.

- **Location:** Slopes of Mount Agung, East Bali (90 min from Ubud)
- **Entry fee:** IDR 60,000 per person plus sarong rental
- **Hours:** 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM
- **Time needed:** 2-3 hours
- **Tips:** Hire an official guide at the ticket office to avoid pushy unofficial guides. Check Mount Agung volcanic activity status before visiting.

### Tirta Empul Temple
**A sacred spring temple** where Balinese Hindus come for ritual purification (melukat). Visitors can participate — wade into the spring-fed pools and pass under each of the 30 water spouts while offering a prayer. It is one of Bali's most spiritually moving experiences.

- **Location:** Tampaksiring (30 min from Ubud)
- **Entry fee:** IDR 50,000 adults
- **Hours:** 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM
- **Time needed:** 90 minutes
- **What to bring:** Change of clothes (you will get wet), waterproof bag for electronics
- **Etiquette:** Follow the order of the spouts (left to right), skip the two reserved for funeral purification

### Ulun Danu Beratan Temple
**The iconic lake temple** that appears to float on Lake Beratan in the cool Bedugul highlands. Dedicated to the lake goddess Dewi Danu, this photogenic temple is one of Bali's most recognizable images.

- **Location:** Bedugul highlands (60 min from Ubud, 90 min from Seminyak)
- **Entry fee:** IDR 75,000 adults
- **Hours:** 7:00 AM – 7:00 PM
- **Best time:** Early morning for mist rising off the lake
- **Time needed:** 60-90 minutes

### Goa Gajah (Elephant Cave)
A 9th-century archaeological site featuring a carved cave entrance depicting demons and nature spirits, a bathing pool with fountains, and ancient rock-carved reliefs. The name "Elephant Cave" may refer to the elephant-headed Hindu god Ganesh.

- **Location:** Bedulu (10 min from central Ubud)
- **Entry fee:** IDR 50,000
- **Hours:** 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM
- **Time needed:** 45-60 minutes

### Taman Ayun Temple
A beautiful royal temple from the Mengwi Kingdom surrounded by a lotus-filled moat. Less touristy than the major temples, with peaceful grounds and impressive multi-tiered meru towers.

- **Location:** Mengwi (30 min from Seminyak)
- **Entry fee:** IDR 50,000
- **Hours:** 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM
- **Time needed:** 45-60 minutes

## Temple Dress Code

All Bali temples require:
- **Sarong** covering legs to below the knee (both men and women)
- **Sash** tied around the waist
- **Covered shoulders** (no tank tops or sleeveless shirts)

Most major temples provide sarongs and sashes at the entrance (free or IDR 10,000-20,000). Buying your own at a market (IDR 30,000-80,000) is more convenient.

## Temple Visit Tips

- **Remove shoes** before entering inner temple areas
- **Never stand higher** than a priest or shrine
- **Ask permission** before photographing ceremonies
- **Step over offerings** (canang sari), never on them
- **Women who are menstruating** are traditionally asked not to enter
- **Bring small bills** for donations and parking
- **Visit early morning** for the quietest experience
- **Hire a guide** at major temples for cultural context — it transforms the visit

Bali's temples are not museums — they are living places of worship where ceremonies happen daily. Visiting with respect and openness rewards you with insights into one of the world's most beautiful and enduring spiritual traditions.
