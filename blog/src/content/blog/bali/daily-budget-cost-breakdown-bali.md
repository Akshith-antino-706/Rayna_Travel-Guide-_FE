## How Much Does Bali Cost Per Day?

Bali remains one of the world's best-value destinations, but costs have risen noticeably in popular areas like Canggu and Seminyak since 2022. That said, the island still offers incredible value at every budget level. A shoestring traveler can get by on USD 35 per day, while mid-range travelers enjoy a comfortable experience at USD 80-120 per day — prices that would be impossible in most Western destinations.

## Budget Traveler: USD 35-50 Per Day

This budget is realistic if you stay in guesthouses or hostels, eat at local warungs, use a scooter, and visit free or low-cost attractions.

### Accommodation: USD 8-20/night
- Hostel dorm bed: IDR 120,000-200,000 (USD 8-13)
- Budget guesthouse private room: IDR 200,000-300,000 (USD 13-20)
- Includes AC, WiFi, and often breakfast

### Food: USD 8-12/day
- **Breakfast:** Included with accommodation or IDR 15,000-25,000 at a warung
- **Lunch:** Nasi campur at a warung: IDR 25,000-40,000 (USD 2-3)
- **Dinner:** Nasi goreng or mie goreng: IDR 25,000-40,000 (USD 2-3)
- **Snacks/drinks:** Fresh fruit, coconut water, coffee: IDR 30,000-50,000 (USD 2-3)
- **Water:** IDR 5,000 for 1.5L bottle

### Transport: USD 5-7/day
- Scooter rental: IDR 70,000-100,000 (USD 5-7) per day
- Fuel: IDR 10,000-20,000 per fill
- Alternative: Grab/Gojek rides average IDR 20,000-50,000 per trip

### Activities: USD 5-10/day
- Temple entries: IDR 25,000-75,000 (USD 2-5) each
- Beach: Free
- Waterfalls: IDR 15,000-25,000 (USD 1-2) each
- Rice terraces: IDR 25,000-40,000 (USD 2-3)

## Mid-Range Traveler: USD 80-120 Per Day

A comfortable experience with nice hotels, a mix of local and Western restaurants, and organized activities.

### Accommodation: USD 40-80/night
- 3-4 star hotel: IDR 600,000-1,200,000
- Boutique hotel with pool: IDR 800,000-1,500,000
- Often includes breakfast buffet

### Food: USD 20-30/day
- **Breakfast:** Hotel buffet (included) or cafe brunch: IDR 80,000-150,000
- **Lunch:** Mid-range restaurant: IDR 80,000-150,000 (USD 5-10)
- **Dinner:** Good restaurant: IDR 150,000-300,000 (USD 10-20)
- **Drinks:** Cocktails IDR 120,000-180,000, beer IDR 50,000-80,000

### Transport: USD 10-20/day
- Private driver for half day: IDR 300,000-400,000
- Grab/Gojek: IDR 50,000-150,000 per ride
- Airport transfer: IDR 200,000-350,000

### Activities: USD 15-30/day
- Guided tours: IDR 300,000-700,000
- Surf lesson: IDR 350,000-500,000
- Beach club day bed: IDR 300,000-500,000

## Luxury Traveler: USD 250-500+ Per Day

Premium experiences with 5-star resorts, fine dining, private tours, and exclusive activities.

### Accommodation: USD 150-500+/night
- 5-star resort: IDR 2,300,000-7,500,000+
- Private villa with pool: IDR 3,000,000-10,000,000+

### Food: USD 50-100+/day
- Fine dining tasting menu: IDR 950,000-1,500,000 (USD 63-100)
- Upscale lunch: IDR 200,000-400,000
- Resort cocktails: IDR 180,000-300,000

### Transport: USD 30-50/day
- Private driver full day: IDR 600,000-800,000
- Airport luxury transfer: IDR 500,000-1,000,000

### Activities: USD 50-150/day
- Private yacht charter: from IDR 5,000,000

## Money-Saving Tips

- **Eat at warungs** instead of tourist restaurants — same ingredients, 70% cheaper
- **Rent a scooter** instead of using Grab for every trip
- **Visit during wet season** (Nov-Mar) for 25-40% lower hotel rates
- **Buy temple sarongs** at a market (IDR 30,000-50,000) rather than renting at each temple
- **Drink local beer** (Bintang) rather than imported brands — half the price
- **Book activities online** through platforms like Klook or GetYourGuide for 10-20% discounts
- **Cook occasionally** if your accommodation has a kitchen — market produce is incredibly cheap
- **Avoid the main tourist markup** by walking 5 minutes away from any major attraction for cheaper food and drinks

## Hidden Costs to Budget For

- **Tourist levy:** IDR 150,000 (USD 10) per person, mandatory on arrival
- **Visa on Arrival:** IDR 500,000 (USD 35) for 30 days
- **ATM fees:** IDR 30,000-50,000 per withdrawal from most machines
- **Temple donations:** IDR 10,000-20,000 at various points within temple complexes
- **Parking fees:** IDR 3,000-10,000 per stop
- **Sunscreen:** Expensive in Bali — bring from home (saves USD 10-20)
- **SIM card:** IDR 150,000 (USD 10) for tourist package with 25GB data

Bali rewards smart travelers. With a little planning and a willingness to eat where the locals eat, you can enjoy a remarkable holiday at a fraction of what it would cost in most comparable destinations.
