## Arriving at Bali's Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS)

Ngurah Rai International Airport, located in southern Bali near Kuta, is the island's sole international gateway. The airport handles over 20 million passengers annually and serves as the entry point for virtually all visitors to Bali. Knowing what to expect before you land will save you time, stress, and money.

## Before You Fly: Pre-Arrival Checklist

Complete these steps before your flight to speed through the arrival process:

### 1. Apply for e-Visa on Arrival (e-VoA)
Apply online at **molina.imigrasi.go.id** at least 48 hours before departure. Cost: IDR 500,000 (approximately USD 35). This gives you a QR code that lets you skip the visa payment queue at the airport and use the faster automated immigration gates.

### 2. Pay the Bali Tourist Levy
Pay IDR 150,000 (approximately USD 10) per person via the **Love Bali** portal (lovebali.baliprov.go.id). This mandatory levy must be paid before or on arrival. Save your QR code receipt.

### 3. Complete the All Indonesia Digital Declaration
Since October 2025, the **All Indonesia app** has replaced paper arrival and customs cards. Download the app and complete your declaration before landing. This covers both immigration and customs declarations.

### 4. Register on SATUSEHAT
Complete the health declaration at **sshp.kemkes.go.id** if required (check current requirements before travel, as this may change).

### 5. Prepare Your Documents
- Passport valid for at least 6 months with minimum 2 blank pages
- Return or onward flight ticket
- e-VoA QR code (if applied online)
- Tourist levy payment receipt
- Proof of accommodation (hotel booking)

## Step-by-Step Arrival Process

### Step 1: Disembark and Follow Signs
After leaving the aircraft, follow "IMMIGRATION" signs through the terminal. The walk from gate to immigration takes 5-10 minutes.

### Step 2: Visa on Arrival (if not pre-purchased)
If you did not apply for an e-VoA online, join the **VoA payment queue** first. Counters accept IDR cash, USD cash, or major credit cards. This queue can take 30-60 minutes during busy periods.

If you have your e-VoA QR code, skip this queue entirely.

### Step 3: Immigration
Present your passport, e-VoA QR code, and completed digital declaration. If you have an e-VoA, you can use the **automated e-gates** for faster processing — this can save 20-30 minutes compared to the manual counters.

At the manual counter, the officer will stamp your passport with a 30-day entry permit. Processing takes 1-3 minutes per person, but queues can extend to 30-120 minutes depending on how many flights arrive simultaneously.

### Step 4: Baggage Claim
Collect your checked luggage from the carousel. Baggage wait times vary from 20 minutes (lucky) to over 60 minutes (normal for large flights). Trolleys are available free of charge.

### Step 5: Customs
Pass through the customs checkpoint. If you have nothing to declare, walk through the green channel. You may be asked to show your completed digital customs declaration.

**Duty-free allowances:**
- 200 cigarettes or 25 cigars or 100g tobacco
- 1 liter of alcoholic beverages
- Personal electronics (1 laptop, 1 phone, 1 camera)
- Goods up to USD 500 in value per person

### Step 6: SIM Card and Money
After customs, you will find:
- **SIM card counters** selling tourist SIM packages (Telkomsel recommended, IDR 150,000 for 25GB)
- **ATMs** for cash withdrawal (BCA and Mandiri are reliable)
- **Money exchange counters** (rates are decent but better in town)

## Transport from the Airport

### Grab/Gojek (Recommended)
The most affordable option. Download both apps before arrival. Walk to the designated ride-hailing pickup area (signposted). Note: Grab and Gojek are not allowed to pick up directly from the terminal — you need to walk to the designated area, about 5-10 minutes from the exit.
- **To Seminyak:** IDR 100,000-200,000 (USD 7-13), 30-45 minutes
- **To Canggu:** IDR 150,000-250,000 (USD 10-17), 45-60 minutes
- **To Ubud:** IDR 250,000-400,000 (USD 17-27), 60-90 minutes

### Official Airport Taxi
Prepaid taxi counters are located in the arrivals hall. Prices are fixed and slightly higher than Grab but convenient.
- **To Kuta/Legian:** IDR 100,000-150,000 (USD 7-10)
- **To Seminyak:** IDR 150,000-250,000 (USD 10-17)
- **To Nusa Dua:** IDR 150,000-200,000 (USD 10-13)

### Hotel Transfer
Most mid-range and luxury hotels offer airport pickup, typically IDR 200,000-500,000 (USD 13-33). Pre-arrange before arrival for a stress-free experience.

### Private Transfer Services
Book through Klook, GetYourGuide, or similar platforms for a pre-arranged driver with name card. Costs IDR 200,000-400,000 to most destinations.

## Tips for a Smooth Arrival

- **Avoid peak arrival times** (late evening, when multiple international flights land simultaneously) if possible
- **Carry USD cash** for the VoA if you did not apply online — exact change speeds things up
- **Keep your phone charged** — you will need it for digital declarations and ride-hailing
- **Bring a power bank** for the potentially long immigration wait
- **Have your accommodation address** saved offline on your phone for your driver
- **Do not accept help from random "helpers"** who approach you in the terminal — use official services only
- **The airport WiFi** is free but slow. Your SIM card purchase should be your first stop after customs.

The arrival process at Bali airport has improved significantly with the digital systems introduced in 2025. Pre-completing everything online can reduce your total arrival time from 2+ hours to under 45 minutes.
