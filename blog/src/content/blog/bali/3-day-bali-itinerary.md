## Your Perfect 3 Days in Bali

Three days gives you enough time to experience Bali's three distinct personalities: the cultural heart of Ubud, the dramatic cliffs and temples of the south, and the beach-and-nightlife scene along the coast. This itinerary is designed to minimize driving time while maximizing experiences.

**Where to stay:** Base yourself in Seminyak or Canggu for easy access to all three days of activities. Alternatively, split your stay with one night in Ubud and two nights on the coast.

## Day 1: Ubud — Culture, Nature, and Art

### Morning

**6:00 AM — Tegallalang Rice Terraces**
Start before the crowds descend. The cascading paddies are at their most photogenic in early morning light. Walk the main path through the terraces, stopping at viewpoints along the way. Budget 60-90 minutes. Entry: IDR 25,000.

**8:30 AM — Tirta Empul Temple**
Drive 15 minutes north to this sacred water temple where Balinese Hindus come for ritual purification. You can participate in the cleansing ritual yourself — wade into the spring-fed pools and pass under each of the 30 water spouts while offering a prayer. It is a genuinely moving experience.
- Entry: IDR 50,000
- Sarong rental available at the entrance
- Time needed: 60-90 minutes

**10:30 AM — Brunch in Ubud**
Return to Ubud center for brunch. Try **Hujan Locale** for Indonesian fusion (mains IDR 80,000-160,000) or **Warung Biah Biah** for authentic Balinese food at local prices (IDR 35,000-60,000).

### Afternoon

**12:00 PM — Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary**
Explore this atmospheric forest with its ancient temple ruins, mossy statues, and lively monkey population. Keep belongings secure and follow the guidelines from staff.
- Entry: IDR 80,000 adults, IDR 60,000 children
- Time needed: 60-90 minutes

**2:00 PM — Ubud Art Market and Royal Palace**
Browse the market's handicrafts — batik, woodcarvings, paintings, and silver jewelry. Bargaining is expected. Walk across to Ubud Royal Palace for a free look at traditional Balinese palace architecture.

**3:30 PM — Tegenungan Waterfall**
Drive 20 minutes south of Ubud to this accessible waterfall. Descend the well-maintained staircase to the base, where you can swim in the pool below the 15-meter cascade. Less crowded in the afternoon.
- Entry: IDR 25,000
- Time needed: 60 minutes

### Evening

**6:00 PM — Ubud Evening**
If you are staying in Ubud, catch a **traditional Balinese dance performance** at the Royal Palace or Pura Dalem temple (IDR 100,000-150,000). The Legong and Barong dances are mesmerizing displays of Balinese culture with elaborate costumes and gamelan music.

Dinner at **Locavore To Go** for upscale Indonesian fare or **Bebek Bengil (Dirty Duck Diner)** for their famous crispy duck (IDR 85,000-150,000).

## Day 2: Uluwatu and the Southern Cliffs

### Morning

**8:00 AM — Drive to the Bukit Peninsula**
Head south to Bali's dramatic Bukit Peninsula (60-90 minutes from Ubud, 30-45 minutes from Seminyak).

**9:30 AM — Padang Padang Beach**
Start at this small but stunning beach tucked between limestone cliffs, made famous by the film "Eat Pray Love." The entrance requires walking through a narrow cave opening — it feels like discovering a secret cove.
- Entry: IDR 15,000
- Time needed: 60-90 minutes
- Great for swimming and bodyboarding

**11:30 AM — Lunch at Warung Sunset Point or Single Fin**
Grab lunch with ocean views. **Single Fin** is a legendary surf bar perched on the Uluwatu cliffs with great food and panoramic views (mains IDR 80,000-180,000). **Warung Sunset Point** is more budget-friendly with equally stunning views (mains IDR 40,000-80,000).

### Afternoon

**1:00 PM — Explore Uluwatu Beaches**
Visit **Melasti Beach**, one of Bali's most pristine white-sand beaches, accessed via a dramatic road carved through limestone cliffs. Entry: IDR 10,000 for motorbikes, IDR 25,000 for cars. Or head to **Dreamland Beach** for good swimming and bodyboarding.

**3:00 PM — Relax or Surf**
Take a surf lesson at Padang Padang or Dreamland (IDR 350,000-500,000 for a 2-hour lesson with board rental) or simply soak up the sun.

### Evening

**5:00 PM — Uluwatu Temple and Kecak Dance**
Arrive at Uluwatu Temple early to explore the clifftop grounds before the famous Kecak Fire Dance begins at sunset (usually around 6:00 PM).
- Temple entry: IDR 150,000 (includes access to the dance performance area)
- Kecak dance ticket: IDR 150,000 additional
- **Important:** Beware of the monkeys here — they are notorious for snatching sunglasses and hats

The Kecak dance is one of Bali's most dramatic cultural performances — a chorus of 50+ men chanting "cak-cak-cak" while performers enact scenes from the Hindu Ramayana epic, all set against the backdrop of the Indian Ocean sunset.

**8:00 PM — Dinner in Jimbaran**
Drive 20 minutes to Jimbaran Bay for the famous **seafood-on-the-beach dinner**. Dozens of restaurants line the sand, grilling fresh fish, prawns, lobster, and squid over coconut husks. Choose your seafood by weight, and dine with your feet in the sand as waves lap the shore. Budget IDR 200,000-400,000 per person for a generous seafood feast.

## Day 3: Beach, Shopping, and Farewell

### Morning

**8:00 AM — Tanah Lot Temple**
Visit this iconic temple during morning hours when it is far less crowded than at sunset. The temple sits on a rock formation just offshore, and at low tide you can walk to its base. The surrounding cliff gardens and coastal paths are beautiful for an early morning stroll.
- Entry: IDR 75,000
- Time needed: 60-90 minutes

**10:30 AM — Seminyak Brunch**
Enjoy a leisurely brunch at one of Seminyak's excellent cafes:
- **Sisterfields** — Australian-style brunch with excellent coffee (IDR 80,000-150,000)
- **Revolver** — Hidden down a narrow alley, this coffee shop is a Seminyak institution (IDR 50,000-100,000)
- **Cafe Organic** — Healthy bowls and smoothies in a photogenic setting (IDR 70,000-130,000)

### Afternoon

**12:00 PM — Shopping in Seminyak**
Browse Seminyak's boutiques along Jalan Kayu Aya (Eat Street) and Jalan Petitenget for Bali-made clothing, homewares, and accessories. Notable shops include **Bamboo Blonde** for resort wear and **Kim Soo** for home furnishings.

**2:00 PM — Beach Club**
Spend your final afternoon at one of Bali's legendary beach clubs:
- **Potato Head Beach Club** — Iconic architecture, pools, and ocean-facing sun loungers. Minimum spend from IDR 300,000
- **Mrs Sippy** — Pool club with diving boards and party atmosphere. Entry from IDR 150,000
- **La Plancha** — Budget-friendly with colorful bean bags on the sand. No minimum spend.

### Evening

**5:30 PM — Sunset on the Beach**
Watch your final Bali sunset from Seminyak Beach with a cold Bintang (IDR 35,000) or a cocktail from a beach bar.

**7:30 PM — Farewell Dinner**
Make your last meal memorable:
- **Sardine** — Seafood restaurant set amid rice paddies in Seminyak. Reserve ahead. Mains IDR 150,000-300,000.
- **Merah Putih** — Award-winning Indonesian cuisine in a stunning bamboo pavilion. Mains IDR 150,000-350,000.
- **Naughty Nuri's** — Famous for their pork ribs and margaritas since the Ubud original. Ribs from IDR 120,000.

## Practical Tips

- **Book Uluwatu Kecak dance tickets** in advance during peak season — they sell out
- **Carry a sarong** in your daypack for impromptu temple visits
- **Traffic between south Bali and Ubud** is worst from 8-10 AM and 4-7 PM — plan accordingly
- **Sunscreen is expensive** in Bali — bring it from home
- **Mosquito repellent** is essential, especially during the wet season and in Ubud's forested areas

Three days gives you a genuine taste of Bali's diversity. You will leave with temple photos, a tan, memories of incredible food, and — almost certainly — plans to return for a longer stay.
