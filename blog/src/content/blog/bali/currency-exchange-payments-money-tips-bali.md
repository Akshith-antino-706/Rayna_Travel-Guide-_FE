## Understanding Money in Bali

The Indonesian Rupiah (IDR) is Bali's official currency, and the large denomination numbers take some getting used to. A single US dollar is worth approximately IDR 15,000-16,000, which means everyday purchases involve numbers in the tens and hundreds of thousands. A meal might cost IDR 50,000, a hotel room IDR 500,000, and a day tour IDR 1,000,000 — but in US dollars, those amounts translate to roughly USD 3, USD 33, and USD 67 respectively.

## Currency Exchange

### Best Exchange Options

**Authorized money changers** offer the best rates and are the safest option. Look for licensed exchangers with the following characteristics:
- Clear, posted exchange rates
- Official business license displayed
- Receipt provided for every transaction
- No hidden fees or commissions

**Recommended money changers:**
- **BMC (Bali Maspintjinra Centra)** — Multiple locations in Kuta, Seminyak, and Ubud. Consistently fair rates.
- **Central Kuta Money Exchange** — Well-established with a solid reputation
- **PT Dirgahayu Valuta Prima** — Professional exchange offices in tourist areas

### Exchange Rates to Expect
You should receive rates close to the mid-market rate (check Google or XE.com). A spread of 1-2% from the mid-market rate is normal at reputable exchangers. Anything more than 3% is a poor deal.

### Exchange Scams to Avoid
- **"No commission" signs** that then apply hidden fees — always confirm the exact amount you will receive before handing over cash
- **Counting tricks** — some exchangers count notes quickly and short-change you. Always recount your money before leaving
- **Street exchangers** offering rates that seem too good to be true — they always are
- **Distraction techniques** — an accomplice may try to distract you during the count. Stay focused.

## ATMs in Bali

ATMs are widely available throughout tourist areas in Bali. However, there are important considerations:

### ATM Tips
- **Use bank-affiliated ATMs** inside bank branches or shopping malls (BCA, Mandiri, BNI, CIMB are reliable)
- **Avoid standalone ATMs** on streets or in quiet locations — card skimming is a known issue
- **Maximum withdrawal** is typically IDR 2,500,000-3,000,000 (USD 167-200) per transaction
- **Fees:** Most ATMs charge IDR 30,000-50,000 (USD 2-3) per withdrawal, plus your home bank's international fee
- **Notify your bank** before traveling to prevent your card from being blocked

### Minimizing ATM Fees
- Withdraw the maximum amount each time to reduce per-transaction fees
- Use a travel-friendly bank card with no international ATM fees (Wise, Charles Schwab, Revolut)
- Consider bringing USD cash and exchanging it — this often works out cheaper than multiple ATM withdrawals

## Card Payments

Credit and debit cards are accepted at:
- Hotels and resorts
- Upscale restaurants and cafes
- Shopping malls and boutiques
- Beach clubs and bars
- Tour operators and major attractions

**Cards are NOT accepted at:**
- Warungs and street food stalls
- Traditional markets
- Small local shops
- Many temple ticket counters
- Some taxi and transport services
- Smaller guesthouses

**Visa and Mastercard** are the most widely accepted. American Express is less common. **QRIS (QR Indonesian Standard)** is Indonesia's QR payment system — some tourist businesses accept payments via apps linked to Indonesian bank accounts.

### Carrying Cash
Always carry IDR cash in a mix of denominations:
- **IDR 100,000 and 50,000 notes** for restaurant meals, activities, and transport
- **IDR 20,000 and 10,000 notes** for temple entries, parking, tips, and small purchases
- **IDR 5,000 and 2,000 notes** for offerings donations and very small purchases

## Tipping in Bali

Tipping is not mandatory in Bali but is increasingly common and always appreciated:

| Service | Suggested Tip |
|---------|--------------|
| Restaurant (no service charge) | 5-10% of bill |
| Restaurant (with service charge) | Not expected |
| Hotel housekeeping | IDR 20,000-50,000/day |
| Private driver (full day) | IDR 50,000-100,000 |
| Spa therapist | IDR 30,000-50,000 |
| Tour guide | IDR 50,000-100,000 |
| Porter | IDR 10,000-20,000 per bag |

Many upscale restaurants automatically add a **10-15% service charge plus 10% government tax** (sometimes shown as "++") to the bill. In these cases, additional tipping is not expected.

## Budgeting Apps and Tools

- **Wise (formerly TransferWise)** — Best multi-currency debit card for travel. Mid-market exchange rates with minimal fees.
- **XE Currency app** — Quick conversion reference
- **Trail Wallet** — Expense tracking app for daily spending
- **Grab/Gojek** — Show exact ride prices before booking, preventing price surprises

## Common Money Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Exchanging money at the airport** — Rates are typically 5-10% worse than in town. Exchange only a small amount on arrival.
2. **Not carrying small notes** — Vendors may not have change for IDR 100,000 notes for small purchases.
3. **Forgetting to budget for the tourist levy and visa** — IDR 650,000 (USD 45) before you even leave the airport.
4. **Over-relying on cards** — Many of Bali's best experiences (warungs, markets, temples) are cash-only.
5. **Accepting the first price** at markets without bargaining — you will pay 2-3x more than necessary.

Managing money in Bali is straightforward once you understand the system. Keep a mix of cash and cards, use reputable exchange services, and remember that the large denominations take a day or two to get used to — that IDR 500,000 note in your pocket is only about USD 33.
