## Bali in April: The Smart Traveler's Choice

April marks the transition from wet season to dry season in Bali, making it one of the best months to visit. The rain tapers off, temperatures are warm and pleasant, crowds have not yet reached peak-season levels, and prices remain moderate. Many experienced Bali travelers consider April their favorite month to visit.

## April Weather

- **Average temperature:** 27-30°C (80-86°F)
- **Rainfall:** 85-100mm — significantly less than wet season months
- **Humidity:** 70-75% (decreasing through the month)
- **Rain pattern:** Occasional brief showers, usually in the late afternoon. Many days are entirely dry.
- **Sea conditions:** Improving — calmer than wet season, good visibility beginning

The landscape retains the lush green of the wet season while the sun comes out more consistently. It is arguably the most beautiful time for the rice terraces, as they transition from deep green to golden hues depending on the harvest cycle.

## Prices and Crowds

April sits in the sweet spot between low and high season:
- **Hotels:** 15-25% cheaper than July/August peak rates
- **Flights:** Moderate pricing — not the cheapest (January-February) but well below peak
- **Attractions:** Manageable crowds at all major sites
- **Restaurants:** Generally no reservation issues except at the most popular venues

### Nyepi Consideration
Bali's Day of Silence (Nyepi) sometimes falls in late March or early April (it follows the Saka lunar calendar). If your trip coincides with Nyepi, you will experience a unique cultural event — but be prepared for a full day confined to your hotel.

## Best Activities for April

### Outdoor Adventures
- **Surfing** — The dry season swell pattern begins on the south coast. Uluwatu, Padang Padang, and Canggu start receiving consistent waves.
- **Mount Batur sunrise trek** — Clear skies improve your chances of a spectacular summit sunrise
- **Diving and snorkeling** — Visibility improves to 20-25 meters. Manta rays active at Nusa Penida.
- **Waterfall visits** — Water flow is still high from the wet season, making waterfalls impressive

### Cultural Experiences
- **Bali Spirit Festival** (usually late March/early April) — A week-long celebration of yoga, music, and dance in Ubud
- **Temple visits** — Comfortable temperatures and fewer crowds make April ideal for temple exploration
- **Cooking classes** — Local markets overflow with fresh produce from the wet season

### Beach Days
- **Nusa Penida day trips** — Calmer seas make the boat crossing more comfortable than in January-March
- **Beach clubs** — Begin their high-season programming
- **Snorkeling at Blue Lagoon** (Padang Bai) — Water clarity improves significantly

## What to Pack for April

- Light, breathable clothing for warm days
- A light rain jacket (occasional showers still possible)
- Sunscreen and sun hat — the tropical sun is strong
- Swimwear for beaches and waterfalls
- Comfortable walking shoes for temple visits and treks
- A sarong for temple entries

## April Tips

- **Book accommodation 3-4 weeks ahead** — not as urgent as peak season but popular hotels fill up
- **The Easter holiday period** (if it falls in April) brings a brief price spike
- **Early April** may still have occasional wet-season showers; late April is more consistently dry
- **The rice terraces** are stunning in April — look for recently planted or recently harvested paddies for different photographic effects
- **Water temperatures** are warm (28-29°C) — no wetsuit needed for water activities

April combines the best of both seasons: the greenery of the wet season with the sunshine of the dry season, at prices that have not yet reached their peak. It is an excellent choice for first-time visitors who want to experience Bali at its most balanced.
