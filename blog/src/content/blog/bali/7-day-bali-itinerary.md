## One Perfect Week in Bali

Seven days is the ideal duration for a first visit to Bali. You will have enough time to soak in Ubud's culture, explore the dramatic south coast, take a day trip to Nusa Penida, catch world-class waves or yoga classes, and still have lazy beach afternoons. This itinerary balances sightseeing with relaxation so you return home rejuvenated rather than exhausted.

**Accommodation strategy:** 3 nights in Ubud, then 4 nights in Seminyak or Canggu.

## Day 1: Arrival and Ubud Immersion

Transfer to Ubud from the airport. After checking in, take the gentle **Campuhan Ridge Walk** for stunning valley views. In the evening, watch a **Legong dance at Ubud Royal Palace** (IDR 100,000) and dine at **Warung Biah Biah** for authentic Balinese food (IDR 35,000-60,000).

## Day 2: Temples and Rice Terraces

**Early morning** at Tegallalang Rice Terraces (entry IDR 25,000) before the crowds arrive. Move to **Tirta Empul Temple** (IDR 50,000) for the purification ritual. After lunch at **Locavore To Go**, visit the **Sacred Monkey Forest** (IDR 80,000) and browse the **Ubud Art Market**. End the day at **Tegenungan Waterfall** (IDR 25,000) before a dinner at **Bebek Bengil**.

## Day 3: North Bali Waterfalls and Temples

### Full Day Adventure
Hire a driver for a northern Bali excursion (IDR 600,000-800,000 for the full day).

**7:00 AM — Sekumpul Waterfall**
Often called Bali's most beautiful waterfall, Sekumpul requires a 30-40 minute hike through jungle and river crossings. The twin cascades plunging into a jungle pool are worth every step. Entry: IDR 20,000 plus optional guide fee of IDR 100,000.

**10:00 AM — Gitgit Waterfall**
A more accessible 35-meter waterfall surrounded by lush tropical forest. Entry: IDR 20,000. The walk down takes 15 minutes on maintained paths.

**12:00 PM — Lunch in Lovina**
Stop in the quiet north coast town of Lovina for fresh fish at a beachfront warung (IDR 40,000-70,000). If time allows, visit the Brahma Vihara Arama Buddhist monastery — Bali's largest Buddhist temple, free to enter.

**2:30 PM — Ulun Danu Beratan Temple**
This photogenic lake temple on the shores of Lake Beratan in the cool highlands of Bedugul is one of Bali's most iconic images. The temple appears to float on the lake during high water. Entry: IDR 75,000.

**4:30 PM — Jatiluwih Rice Terraces (UNESCO)**
A UNESCO World Heritage Site, these terraces are far more expansive and less touristy than Tegallalang. Drive or walk through the terraces as the afternoon light turns the paddies golden. Entry: IDR 40,000.

Return to Ubud by evening for a quiet dinner.

## Day 4: Nusa Penida Day Trip

**6:30 AM** — Drive to Sanur Harbor. Fast boat to Nusa Penida (30-45 minutes, IDR 300,000-500,000 round trip).

Hit the island's top three spots: **Kelingking Beach** (the iconic T-Rex cliff), **Angel's Billabong** (a natural infinity pool), and **Broken Beach** (a dramatic natural arch). Lunch at a local warung. Afternoon at **Crystal Bay** for snorkeling or **Atuh Beach** for swimming.

Return to Sanur by 5 PM, then transfer to your south Bali accommodation in Seminyak or Canggu.

## Day 5: Uluwatu and the Southern Coast

Spend the morning at **Melasti Beach** and **Padang Padang Beach**. Lunch at **Single Fin** on the Uluwatu cliffs. Afternoon surf lesson or beach time. Evening at **Uluwatu Temple** (IDR 150,000) for the sunset **Kecak Fire Dance** (IDR 150,000). End with a seafood dinner on **Jimbaran Beach** (IDR 200,000-400,000 per person).

## Day 6: Canggu Surf and Wellness

### Morning
**7:00 AM — Yoga Class**
Start with a morning flow at **The Practice Bali** or **Desa Seni** in Canggu (IDR 150,000-200,000 drop-in).

**9:00 AM — Surf at Batu Bolong**
Whether you are a beginner or experienced, Batu Bolong offers consistent, forgiving waves. Surf school lessons cost IDR 350,000-500,000 for 2 hours including board. Board rental alone is IDR 50,000-100,000 per hour.

### Afternoon
**12:00 PM — Brunch at Crate Cafe**
Canggu's beloved brunch spot with excellent coffee and healthy bowls (IDR 60,000-120,000).

**1:30 PM — Tanah Lot Temple**
Drive 20 minutes to this iconic sea temple. Less crowded midday than at sunset. Walk the coastal paths and explore the gardens. Entry: IDR 75,000.

**3:30 PM — Echo Beach**
Return to Canggu for an afternoon at Echo Beach. Watch the surfers tackle the reef break while sipping a coconut from a beachfront warung.

### Evening
**5:30 PM — Sunset at The Lawn or Old Man's**
Two of Canggu's best sunset spots. The Lawn has beanbags on grass overlooking Echo Beach (min spend IDR 200,000). Old Man's is more casual with live music and cold Bintangs (IDR 40,000).

**8:00 PM — Dinner at Mason**
Excellent wood-fired cuisine in Canggu's dining scene (mains IDR 150,000-300,000).

## Day 7: Seminyak, Shopping, and Farewell

### Morning
**9:00 AM — Spa Treatment**
Treat yourself to a Balinese massage or flower bath at one of Seminyak's many spas. A 60-minute traditional massage costs IDR 150,000-300,000 at mid-range spas — a fraction of Western prices.

**11:00 AM — Shopping in Seminyak**
Browse the boutiques along Jalan Kayu Aya and Jalan Petitenget. Pick up Bali-made clothing, handcrafted jewelry, organic skincare, and homewares.

### Afternoon
**1:00 PM — Farewell Brunch at Sisterfields**
Seminyak institution serving excellent Australian-style brunch (IDR 80,000-150,000).

**2:30 PM — Beach Club**
Spend your final afternoon at **Potato Head Beach Club** — Bali's most iconic beach club with stunning architecture, infinity pools, and ocean views. Day beds from IDR 300,000 (redeemable on food and drinks).

### Evening
**6:00 PM — Final Sunset**
Watch the sun drop into the Indian Ocean from Seminyak Beach.

**7:30 PM — Farewell Dinner at Merah Putih**
End your Bali journey at this award-winning Indonesian restaurant housed in a soaring bamboo cathedral. Mains IDR 150,000-350,000. Book ahead.

## One-Week Packing Essentials

- Light, breathable clothing plus a sarong for temples
- Reef-safe sunscreen and insect repellent
- Waterproof phone case for waterfalls and water activities
- Comfortable walking shoes and flip-flops
- Light rain jacket (especially November-March)
- Reusable water bottle (many hotels have refill stations)

Seven days in Bali transforms a vacation into a genuine journey. You will leave with a deeper understanding of Balinese culture, tan lines from world-class beaches, and a photo roll that will make everyone at home jealous.
