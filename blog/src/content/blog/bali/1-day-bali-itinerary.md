## Making the Most of One Day in Bali

If you only have 24 hours in Bali — whether on a layover, a side trip from another Indonesian island, or squeezing in a final day before your flight — you can still experience the island's magic. This itinerary focuses on the Ubud-to-south-coast corridor, giving you a taste of Bali's culture, nature, and cuisine in a single action-packed day.

**Pro tip:** Hire a private driver for the day (IDR 500,000-700,000 / USD 33-47). This is the most efficient way to cover ground and lets you relax between stops rather than navigating Bali's chaotic traffic.

## Morning: Ubud and the Rice Terraces (7:00 AM – 12:00 PM)

### 7:00 AM — Tegallalang Rice Terraces

Start your day early at the iconic Tegallalang Rice Terraces, located about 20 minutes north of Ubud center. Arriving before 9:00 AM means cooler temperatures, fewer crowds, and the best light for photography. The cascading green paddies are genuinely breathtaking, and early morning mist adds an ethereal quality.

- **Entry fee:** IDR 25,000 (USD 1.60) per person
- **Time needed:** 45-60 minutes
- **Tip:** Watch for additional small donation requests along the paths — IDR 10,000-20,000 at each point is standard

Walk along the terraced paths and stop at one of the many swing platforms for that classic Bali photo. The famous Bali Swing nearby charges IDR 350,000-500,000 for the full experience, but smaller swings along the terrace paths cost just IDR 50,000-100,000.

### 9:00 AM — Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary

Head back toward Ubud center to visit the Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary (Mandala Suci Wenara Wana). This lush forest is home to over 1,000 Balinese long-tailed macaques and contains three ancient Hindu temples dating back to the 14th century.

- **Entry fee:** IDR 80,000 for adults, IDR 60,000 for children
- **Opening hours:** 8:30 AM – 6:00 PM
- **Time needed:** 60-90 minutes

**Important warnings:**
- Secure all belongings — monkeys will grab sunglasses, hats, water bottles, and phones
- Do not make direct eye contact or show your teeth (monkeys interpret this as aggression)
- Do not touch or feed the monkeys unless supervised by staff

### 10:30 AM — Ubud Market and Palace

Walk from the Monkey Forest to the Ubud Art Market (Pasar Seni Ubud), located across from the Ubud Royal Palace. Browse handcrafted goods including batik textiles, wooden carvings, woven baskets, and silver jewelry. Bargaining is expected — start at about 50% of the asking price.

Pop into Ubud Royal Palace (Puri Saren Agung) for a quick look at the traditional Balinese architecture. The palace courtyard is free to enter during the day and hosts traditional dance performances in the evening.

### 11:30 AM — Brunch in Ubud

Fuel up at one of Ubud's excellent cafes:

- **Locavore To Go** — Casual offshoot of Bali's most celebrated restaurant, with excellent Indonesian-inspired dishes for IDR 50,000-100,000
- **Clear Cafe** — Popular with health-conscious travelers, serving organic bowls and fresh juices for IDR 60,000-120,000
- **Warung Biah Biah** — Authentic Balinese food at local prices, with nasi campur for around IDR 35,000-50,000

## Afternoon: Temple and Beach (12:30 PM – 5:30 PM)

### 12:30 PM — Drive to Tanah Lot

Head southwest from Ubud to Tanah Lot Temple, one of Bali's most photographed landmarks. The drive takes approximately 45-60 minutes depending on traffic.

### 1:30 PM — Tanah Lot Temple

This iconic 16th-century sea temple sits on a rocky outcrop just offshore, making for a dramatic silhouette against the sky. At low tide, you can walk across to the base of the temple rock and receive a blessing from Hindu priests at a small spring.

- **Entry fee:** IDR 75,000 for adults, IDR 40,000 for children
- **Opening hours:** 7:00 AM – 7:00 PM
- **Time needed:** 60-90 minutes

The temple complex also includes gardens, smaller shrines, and a row of souvenir shops and restaurants. Grab a fresh coconut (IDR 20,000-30,000) as you explore.

### 3:00 PM — Seminyak Beach Time

Drive south to Seminyak (approximately 30-45 minutes from Tanah Lot) and spend the afternoon relaxing on the beach. Seminyak Beach offers golden sand, consistent surf, and a vibrant atmosphere with beach bars and loungers available.

If you prefer a more upscale beach experience, grab a day bed at one of the famous beach clubs:

- **Potato Head Beach Club** — Iconic design-forward venue with pools, restaurants, and ocean views. Day bed from IDR 300,000 (redeemable on food and drinks)
- **Ku De Ta** — Bali's original upscale beach club with excellent cocktails and a sophisticated atmosphere
- **La Plancha** — Budget-friendly option with colorful bean bags directly on the sand. No minimum spend.

## Evening: Sunset and Dinner (5:30 PM – 9:00 PM)

### 5:30 PM — Sunset Drinks

Bali sunsets are legendary, and Seminyak offers some of the best viewing spots:

- Watch from the beach with a Bintang beer (IDR 35,000-50,000 from beach vendors)
- Splurge on sunset cocktails at Ku De Ta or Potato Head (IDR 150,000-250,000 per cocktail)

### 7:00 PM — Dinner

End your day with an unforgettable meal:

- **Merah Putih** (Seminyak) — Award-winning modern Indonesian cuisine in a stunning cathedral-like bamboo structure. Mains from IDR 150,000-350,000.
- **Mama San** (Seminyak) — Pan-Asian cuisine in a converted warehouse. Mains from IDR 130,000-280,000.
- **Warung Nia** (Seminyak) — Excellent local food at reasonable prices if you want an authentic experience. Nasi goreng around IDR 40,000-60,000.

## Tips for Your One-Day Itinerary

- **Start early.** Bali's mornings are magical, and beating the crowds at popular spots makes a huge difference.
- **Wear comfortable walking shoes** that you can easily slip on and off (you will remove them at temples).
- **Bring a sarong** or buy one cheaply at any market — you will need it for temple visits.
- **Stay hydrated.** Bali's heat and humidity can be draining. Carry a refillable water bottle.
- **Download Grab and Gojek** as backup transportation options.
- **Carry cash in small denominations** (IDR 10,000 and 20,000 notes) for temple donations, parking fees, and small purchases.

One day barely scratches the surface of what Bali has to offer, but this itinerary ensures you experience the island's essential highlights — ancient temples, lush rice terraces, playful monkeys, golden beaches, and world-class food.
