## When Should You Visit Bali?

The short answer: Bali is a year-round destination. The longer answer involves weighing your priorities — weather, budget, crowds, and specific activities — to find the month that best matches your ideal trip. Bali has two distinct seasons, and each offers a different but equally valid travel experience.

## The Two Seasons

### Dry Season: April to October
- **Temperature:** 27-30°C (80-86°F)
- **Rainfall:** Minimal — brief showers possible but rare
- **Humidity:** Lower (60-70%)
- **Ocean conditions:** Calmer waters, best visibility for diving
- **Best months within dry season:** June, July, August (driest)

### Wet Season: November to March
- **Temperature:** 24-29°C (75-85°F)
- **Rainfall:** Regular afternoon downpours, 2-4 hours typically
- **Humidity:** Higher (75-85%)
- **Rainiest month:** January (average 350mm rainfall)
- **Important note:** It rarely rains all day. Mornings are usually sunny, with rain arriving in the afternoon or evening.

## Best Time For...

### Beach and Swimming
**April to October.** Calmer seas, clearer water, and minimal rain make this the best window for beach activities. Nusa Penida boat crossings are also smoother.

### Surfing
**April to October** for the south coast (Uluwatu, Canggu, Kuta) — the dry season brings consistent southwest swells. **November to March** for the east coast (Keramas, Sanur) — wet season swells hit from the northeast.

### Diving and Snorkeling
**April to November.** Visibility peaks during the dry season, reaching 30+ meters at top dive sites. Manta ray season at Nusa Penida is roughly September to April. Mola mola (ocean sunfish) appear July to October.

### Cultural Experiences
**Year-round**, but several important festivals fall in specific months:
- **Nyepi (Day of Silence):** Usually March — a unique and unmissable experience
- **Galungan and Kuningan:** Dates vary based on the 210-day Balinese calendar
- **Bali Arts Festival:** June-July — a month of traditional dance, music, and craft exhibitions

### Budget Travel
**January to March** and **October to November** offer the lowest prices. Accommodation drops 25-40% from peak rates, and you will have popular attractions largely to yourself.

### Trekking and Waterfalls
**April to October** for the most comfortable hiking conditions. Waterfalls are most impressive during and just after the wet season (December-April) when water flow is at its peak.

## Peak Season vs Off-Season

### Peak Season (July-August, December 20-January 5)
- Hotels are 50-100% more expensive
- Popular restaurants require reservations days or weeks ahead
- Uluwatu Kecak dance tickets sell out
- Traffic is significantly worse
- Book everything 2-3 months in advance

### Off-Season (January-March, October-November)
- Accommodation at its cheapest
- Attractions are uncrowded
- Rain typically comes in afternoon bursts, leaving mornings free
- Lush green landscapes — rice terraces are at their most vibrant
- Some outdoor activities may be affected by weather

## The Verdict

For most first-time visitors, **April-May or September-October** represent the sweet spot — dry weather, manageable crowds, and prices that have not hit peak-season levels. If budget is your priority, the wet season offers extraordinary value with weather that is far more manageable than most people expect. And if you simply want guaranteed sunshine, July and August deliver, albeit at premium prices.

There is no bad time to visit Bali. Every season has its advantages, and the island's magic is constant regardless of the weather forecast.
