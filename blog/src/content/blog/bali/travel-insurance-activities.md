## Why Travel Insurance Matters in Bali

Travel insurance is not optional for Bali — it is essential. The island's most popular activities (surfing, scooter riding, diving, trekking) carry inherent risks, and Bali's medical facilities, while good, can be expensive. A serious injury requiring medical evacuation to Singapore or Australia can cost USD 50,000-100,000+. The right travel insurance policy ensures a health crisis does not become a financial catastrophe.

## Common Bali Activities and Coverage

### Scooter/Motorbike Riding
**The most important coverage to verify.** Scooter accidents are the leading cause of tourist injury in Bali, yet many standard travel insurance policies exclude motorbike injuries.

**What to check:**
- Does your policy cover motorbike/scooter riding?
- Is there an engine size limit (many policies cap at 125cc)?
- Do you need an International Driving Permit for coverage to apply?
- Are passengers covered as well as riders?

**Important:** If you do not have a valid IDP with motorcycle endorsement, most insurers will deny your claim even if your policy includes motorbike coverage.

### Surfing
Most adventure travel policies cover surfing as it is considered a standard water sport. However:
- Verify surfing is not listed as an exclusion
- Some policies exclude surfing in "known dangerous conditions" — reef breaks like Uluwatu may fall into this category for basic policies
- Surf competition participation may require additional coverage

### Scuba Diving
Standard policies typically cover recreational diving to 18 meters (60 feet). For deeper dives:
- Verify your maximum depth coverage
- PADI Open Water certification covers you to 18m
- Advanced Open Water to 30m may need enhanced coverage
- Divers Alert Network (DAN) insurance is recommended as supplementary diving-specific coverage

### White Water Rafting
Generally covered under adventure sports provisions. The Ayung River (Class II-III) is considered low-risk. The Telaga Waja River (Class III-IV) may require enhanced adventure coverage with some providers.

### Trekking
Mount Batur (1,717m) is covered by most standard policies. Mount Agung (3,031m) may require policies that cover trekking above 2,000-3,000m depending on the provider.

## What Good Bali Travel Insurance Covers

### Medical
- Emergency medical treatment in Bali
- Hospital stays (private hospitals charge USD 100-500+ per night)
- Prescription medications
- Emergency dental treatment
- Medical evacuation to Singapore or Australia if needed

### Trip-Related
- Trip cancellation or interruption
- Flight delays and missed connections
- Lost, stolen, or delayed baggage
- Personal liability

### Adventure Sports
- Surfing, diving (verify depth limits), rafting
- Scooter/motorbike riding (with valid license)
- Trekking and hiking
- Water sports

- **True Traveller** — UK-based with excellent adventure sports coverage
- **DAN Insurance** — Essential supplement for scuba divers

## How to Choose the Right Policy

1. **List every activity** you plan to do in Bali
2. **Check each activity** against the policy's included and excluded activities list
3. **Verify scooter coverage** specifically — this is the most common gap
4. **Confirm medical coverage limits** — minimum USD 100,000 recommended for Bali
5. **Check evacuation coverage** — should include air evacuation to Singapore or Australia
6. **Read the fine print** on license requirements — especially for motorbikes
7. **Compare 3-4 providers** before purchasing

## Making a Claim in Bali

If you need to make a claim:
1. **Contact your insurer immediately** — most have 24/7 emergency hotlines
2. **Get police reports** for theft or accidents
3. **Keep all medical receipts** and documentation
4. **Take photos** of everything (injuries, accident scene, damaged property)
5. **Save all correspondence** with hospitals, police, and your insurer
6. **Do not pay large bills out of pocket** if possible — most insurers can arrange direct payment with hospitals

## Key Bali Hospitals

- **BIMC Hospital Kuta** — International-standard private hospital, direct billing with most insurers
- **Siloam Hospital Denpasar** — Good general hospital
- **Kasih Ibu Hospital** — Multiple locations across Bali

Travel insurance for Bali typically costs USD 5-15 per day for comprehensive coverage including adventure sports. Compared to the potential cost of a single medical emergency, it is the best investment you can make for your trip.
